Debate resumed on Clause 3.

Lord Brightman
moved Amendment No. 6:

Page 3, line 7, after ("whose") insert ("gross").

The noble and learned Lord said: This amendment seeks only to clarify Clause 3(4) of the Bill. In line 7, page 3 of the Bill, one sees the formula for registration:

"and whose income from all sources does not in aggregate amount to more than £1,000 a year".

Does that refer to gross income or net income? In Clause 41(1) the Bill uses the expression "gross income". It is exactly the same in Clause 42(1) (b). Presumably "income" in Clause 3(4) means gross income. If that is so, I submit that the Bill should so state. I beg to move.

Viscount Astor
: I appreciate the intention behind the amendment. As the noble and learned Lord said, there are other references in the Bill to gross income, but it looks a little odd that that phrase is not used in Clause 3. However, I hope that I can assure the noble and learned Lord that his amendment is not necessary. Clause 3 amends Section 4 of the 1960 Act and it therefore attracts the definitions of various terms contained in Section 45 of that Act. Paragraph 18 of Schedule 2 to the Bill amends Section 45 of the 1960 Act. This amended Section 45 makes it clear that references in the 1960 Act to "income from all sources" should be construed by reference to the gross revenues of the charity. That has the same effect on the meaning of Clause 3 as would this amendment. I hope that with that slightly complicated explanation the noble and learned Lord will see that I have answered his points.

Lord Brightman
: I am grateful to the noble Viscount. I am sure that it is due to my own denseness that I have not picked up the definition referred to in Section 45 of the Charities Act 1960. Can he point out where in the Charities Act 1960 one finds a definition of income as meaning gross income?

Viscount Astor
