    ".—(1) It shall be the duty of every energy conservation authority to prepare a report in accordance with this section.

    2. The report shall include—
      1. an assessment of the cost of the energy conservation measures set out in it,
      2. an assessment of the extent to which carbon dioxide emissions into the atmosphere would be decreased as a result of those measures, and

        Nothing in this subsection shall be taken as requiring the authority to set out in the report energy conservation measures to be taken in relation to any particular dwelling or building.
    3. The report may, if the energy conservation authority considers it desirable, include—
      1. an assessment of the extent of decreases in emissions into the atmosphere of oxides of nitrogen and sulphur dioxide which would result from the implementation of the measures set out in the report;
      2. an assessment of the number of jobs which would result from the implementation of those measures;
      3. an assessment of the average savings in fuel bills and in kilowatt hours or fuel used that might be expected to result from the measures by different types of household in different types of accommodation;
      4. such other matters as it considers appropriate.
    4. An energy conservation authority may in preparing the report consult such persons as it considers appropriate.
