Mr. Piers Merchant
(Beckenham)
: I beg to move amendment No. 23, in page 1, line 16, leave out 'may' and insert 'shall'.

The amendment is straightforward and would leave out the word "may" and insert the word "shall" in the provisions for regulations. We all agree that the Bill is important and that its effectiveness depends on the thoroughness with which the regulations are drawn up.

I am a little worried by the use of the word "may". That may seem to be a minor point, but if the Bill is to work as intended and give full protection to children and visitors to outdoor centres, it is vital that the regulations, when they are drawn up, should be thorough and far-reaching.

I am afraid that leaving the Bill as it is presently worded may result in regulations being loose and covering only some of those important matters. I do not propose to delay the Committee for long, but I want to say a few words about the need for the legislation to be complete.

By changing "may" to "shall", the amendment would impose a requirement on the Minister and the licensing authority to draw up regulations covering each of those aspects. For example, on requirements relating to safety, clause 1(3) states:

"Regulations may make provisions as to—


(b) any requirements relating to safety (whether applying to facilities for adventure activities or to other facilities) which must be satisfied by an applicant for a licence".

It should be crystal clear that the Bill will impose an obligation to make regulations on requirements relating to safety because that aspect is so important. That is one of the Bill's crucial objectives. When dealing with disasters such as the Lyme bay disaster, it is essential that, if we alter the law, we alter it to give full protection to all those involved in outdoor activity centres by ensuring that regulations on safety are of paramount importance.
