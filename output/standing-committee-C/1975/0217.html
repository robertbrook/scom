The clause extends the powers of an arbitrator beyond the bounds to which English law has confined arbitrators until now. An arbitrator's job is to decide as a matter of fact what must be done by a party in order to comply with his legal obligations. The clause enables the Lord Chancellor to make orders allowing single arbitrators to change the legal obligations of parties if they think they ought to, and sub-paragraph (ii) leaves it entirely to the whim of the arbitrator.

A landlord cannot serve a notice to do work unless the tenant is in breach JPEG 603 of his tenancy. No notice to do work can be served within 12 months of a previous notice. The notice must be in a prescribed form which sets out the tenant's rights and remedies. No tenant can claim ignorance; they are there on the form when it is presented to him. Any flaw in the notice to do work, even in respect of one of many items, will invalidate the notice to quit, which will be set aside.

Section 24(2) of the 1948 Act, as amended by the Act of 1958 and Section 19(6) of the Agriculture (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1963 sets out the deadly sins. No counter-notice may be served to a notice to quit grounded on a deadly sin. The notice to quit, if valid, operates automatically, but the tenant may go to arbitration on the validity of notices served under subsection (2)( b), ( d) and ( e), because the land is required for permitted development, because of the tenant's failure to comply with a notice to pay overdue rent, or where the notice is to remedy breaches of tenancy or the tenant is committing an irremediable breach of tenancy. These matters sound complicated, but I think that they are well known. I know full well that if I do not pay my rent my landlord will give me notice to quit that will stick. "within such reasonable period as was specified in the notice to remedy any breach by the tenant that was capable of being remedied of any term or condition of his tenancy which was not inconsistent with the fulfilment of his responsibilities to farm in accordance with the rules of good husbandry".

All interested bodies agreed that this elabora- JPEG 605 tion of the procedures was necessary for the protection of the tenant.

"a period of less than six months shall not be treated as a reasonable period within which to do any such work".
