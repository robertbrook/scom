Mr. Strang
: I beg to move, That the Clause be read a Second time. Under subsection (1), any eligible person as defined in new Clause 8 may apply to the Agricultural Land Tribunal within the relevant period of three months from the death of the tenant for a direction entitling him to a tenancy of the holding. If only one person applies for the tenancy, then, under subsection (2), the tribunal first has to satisfy itself that he is an eligible person as defined in new Clause 8—that is to say, that he is a close relative of the deceased, has worked on the holding for at least five years out of the seven years immediately preceding death, and that he is not already the occupier of a commercial unit of agricultural land—and then go on to determine whether he is in its opinion a suitable person to become the tenant of the holding. Subsection (3) requires the tribunal to go through the same procedure for every applicant if there is more than one.

Subsection (4) gives special priority to the deceased's wishes if these are set out in his will. Where there has been a valid designation—and by that I mean that the deceased states a clear intention in his will—the Agricultural Land Tribunal is required, first, to consider the claim of that person. If it is satisfied that he is both an eligible person and a suitable person to become the tenant of the holding, then it will disregard all other applications.

A valid designation has to be a clear and unequivocal statement, such as "I wish my son John to take over my holding". On the other hand, if the tribunal is not satisfied that he is an eligible person or a suitable person—for example, the named 1092 son may have been living abroad for the last 20 years— it then goes on to consider the other applicants.

Subsection (5) requires the Agricultural Land Tribunal to give effect to its decision by means of a direction, subject, of course, to its consideration of any application by the landlord for the repossession of his holding.

Under subsection (6), if the Agricultural Land Tribunal decides that more than one applicant is both eligible and suitable to take over the holding, it is required to choose between them and select one of them, unless the landlord is prepared to agree to joint tenants.

Subsection (7) is a safeguard for both the landlord and the agricultural interest, in that the Agricultural Land Tribunal is required to ask the landlord for his views on the suitability as a tenant of any or all of the applicants.

In deciding whether any applicant is a suitable person to take over the tenancy, the Agricultural Land Tribunal is required by subsection (8) to have regard to all relevant matters. These include the agricultural training and practical experience of an applicant, his age, his physical health, whether he has sufficient money to farm the holding properly, and the views, if any, of the landlord as to his suitability.

Subsection (9) enables the Agricultural Land Tribunal to make a direction specifying that there should be a joint tenancy of the holding, but only provided that the landlord is prepared to agree to it and to the number of tenants proposed. The maximum figure of four reflects current legislation elsewhere.

Subsections (10), (11) and (12) are technical provisions dealing with the JPEG 1093 deceased tenant's will. Under subsection (11), for instance, it will be possible for an elder son to be designated on condition that he survives the testator for a period of 30 days, after which the younger son is designated in his place; while subsection (12) follows the tenor of a somewhat similar provision in Section 27( 1) of the Administration of Estates Act 1925.

Subsection (13) is a particularly important subsection, because it provides a means whereby a landlord and the sole applicant or the sole remaining applicant may independently reach agreement without completing the procedure before the Agricultural Land Tribunal. In such a case, once agreement has been reached, the sole applicant is enabled to withdraw his application and no further proceedings before the tribunal need take place. This provision also enables any applicant who does not wish to proceed further with an application to bring it to an end.
