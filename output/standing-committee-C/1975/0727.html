    Mr. Davidson
    : These are drafting amendments which seek to clarify the effect of the "grace" period provided under subsections (4) and (5).

    Clause 25 provides that the Secretary of State may prescribe requirements as to the size, durability and legibility of documents to be delivered to the Registrar and also provides that the Registrar may require the re-submission of documents which do not meet the prescribed standards. If a document were to be submitted near to the end of the period provided in statute for its submission, by the time the document had been resubmitted in an acceptable form the company, or its directors, as the case may be, could be in default and liable to penalties. A grace period has therefore been provided which would not count towards the calculation of any period of default.

    As drafted, subsections (4) and (5) provide that the grace period runs from the date on which the document was originally delivered to either the fourteenth day after notice has been served by the Registrar—notice to the effect that the document was not in an acceptable form—or the day on which the document is delivered in an acceptable form, whichever occurs earlier. This complicated formula JPEG 419 does not, in effect, offer a genuine alternative, as I am sure hon. Members will appreciate if they examine it carefully. No doubt it is something which hon. Members would have raised in the debate on clause stand part. Once the document has been re-submitted in an acceptable form, no further liability to a penalty arises. It is therefore meaningless to say that the grace period ends on the day on which the acceptable document is re-delivered.

    The amendments redraft subsections (4) and (5) so as to provide that the grace period which does not count towards the calculation of the penalty for any default shall run from the date of original delivery to the fourteenth day after service of notice to the effect that the document was not acceptable. Amendment No. 116 is a paving amendment. Amendment No. 117 deletes paragraph ( b) of subsection (5).
