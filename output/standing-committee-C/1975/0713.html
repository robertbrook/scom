Clause 9
DUTY TO PREPARE AND DELIVER ACCOUNTS IN THE CASE OF OVERSEA COMPANIES

The Parliamentary Secretary to the Law Officers Department (Mr. Arthur Davidson)
: When the Committee adjourned my hon. Friend was on his feet and about to reply to the request for an explanation of the clause, a request made by the hon. Member for Worthing (Mr. Higgins). My hon. Friend is unfortunately detained at a meeting. He will be here shortly. Perhaps I could give an explanation in his absence.

The clause requires overseas companies to prepare accounts in respect of each accounting reference period and file copies with the Registrar.

Section 410 of the 1948 Act already requires overseas companies to make out accounts once a year and file copies with the Registrar, but for the accounts of British companies the existing requirements are difficult to enforce and widely disregarded. New arrangements for filing accounts are, therefore, being applied in this Bill to overseas as well as British companies. The accounts have to be made up in the same way and must contain the same information as the accounts of British companies except to the extent that any exceptions or modification are prescribed. If the accounts are not in English, a certified translation also has to be filed, and there is an exemption for certain unlimited companies.

Perhaps I should explain each subsection briefly. Subsection (1) requires that every overseas company shall prepare accounts in respect of each accounting reference period. Accounts must include a balance sheet, profit and loss account and, if the company is a holding company, group accounts, and must cover the same 212 period and contain the same particulars, subject to any exception and modifications which may be prescribed, as if the company were registered under the Companies Acts. This is similar to the existing requirement under Section 410 of the 1948 Act except that in future accounts will have to be made up in respect of each accounting reference period instead of, as at present, once in each calendar year.

The Overseas Companies Accounts (Exceptions) Order 1968 grants overseas companies certain exemptions from the normal requirements as to the contents of accounts. Overseas companies are not required to produce a directors' report and they are exempted from the provisions of Sections 3 to 8 of the 1967 Act which require companies to disclose information about their subsidiaries and ultimate holding company, directors' emoluments and salaries of higher paid employees. They are also exempted from the requirements to disclose turnover and give details of the United Kingdom corporation tax liabilities. It is expected that similar exemptions will be granted under subsection (1).

Subsection (2) requires overseas companies to deliver to the Registrar a copy of the accounts which they are required by subsection (1) to prepare, together with a certified translation of any document not in English.

Subsection (3) exempts certain overseas companies with unlimited liability from the requirements of the clause.

Subsection (4) provides that Section 410 of the 1948 Act, which is superseded by this clause, shall cease to have effect.

I hope that explanation will help the hon. Member.

Subsection (3) requires that the accounting records shall contain statements relating to dealings in goods and that such statements shall include statements of all stocktakings, except in the case of goods sold by way of retail trade, and of all goods sold and purchased, showing the goods and the buyers and sellers in sufficient detail to enable the goods and buyers to be identified.
