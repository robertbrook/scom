    RESTRICTION ON OPERATION OF NOTICE TO QUIT GIVEN BY REASON OF DEATH OF TENANT.

    (1) A notice to quit the holding given to the tenant thereof by reason of the death of the deceased and falling within section 24(2)( g) of the 1948 Act shall not have effect unless either—

    Under subsection (1), a notice to quit served on the death of a tenant under Section 24(2)(g) shall not have effect unless one of three sets of circumstances apply. First, if no application for the tenancy is made to the Agricultural Land Tribunal within the relevant period; or second, if an application has been made but the tribunal determines that no applicant is a suitable person to become a tenant of the holding; or third, although the tribunal may determine that an applicant is suitable to become a tenant of the holding, it nevertheless consents to the landlord's application for the notice to quit to operate.
