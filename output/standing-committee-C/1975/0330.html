Mr. Norman Buchan: I am glad that the Minister is here today because this debate is perhaps the most crucial in this series of discussions on the new clauses. I know that the Minister will have been following our debates and will be aware of the strength of mood on the Government side of the Committee that we must get the measure right.

The proposal that there should be a restriction to two transfers on two successions only lies at the heart of the discussion. The principle has been accepted and the clauses attempt to set out the method by which the principle will be implemented. The principle is based on two considerations.

The first is that a great deal of hardship or injustice would occur following the death or injury of the tenant farmer. Those who have been involved with him and are close to him in the working of the holding would be deprived of a continuing interest in the holding.

Secondly, there is the recognition that farming, unlike many other occupations, requires a continuation of involvement among those who have been involved in the holding in the interests of forward planning and good husbandry. It is because we recognise that farming is not a 12-monthly affair, but is a 5-, 10-, 15-, or 20-year affair, that we recognise the importance of that provision. It gives an Col. 986 interest and involvement in the future among the people who are most closely connected with the holding. It is not just the tenant himself who must put all his energies and forethought into taking in new land and acquiring more capital machinery, but those who continue to farm that holding who also have the same interests. Without that continuing interest, good husbandry will disappear. We face the twin problems of hardship and injustice and the interests of good farming.

It has been argued that good farming depends upon the landlord/tenant relationship, and a good deal of it does. It also depends upon the people who are doing the job and their confidence that they can continue to do it. That lies at the heart of the argument about the succession. We are not talking about two generations. Of itself two generations would not solve the problem of long-term forward thinking and forward planning, because two generations could be as short as a year or as long as 50 years.
