The formation of a safety committee is often indicative of a genuine desire on the part of senior management to tackle its accident problem with vigour. Such a management would take decisive and successful action through its normal management channels; setting up a safety committee might well be not so much a part of the campaign against accidents, but as a means of making management's determination to tackle its accident problem known to its employees' representatives. In many cases the setting up of a safety committee reflects the determination of management to reduce accidents; it cannot with certainty be claimed that it is the safety committee rather than the management's determination to reduce accidents that achieves the recorded success.
