Protection of Children Bill

Clause 11
FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

4.30 pm

Mr. Philip Hammond
(Runnymeade and Weybridge
: I believe that when we adjourned before lunch, no one had spoken to the clause and that the hon. Member for Stourbridge (Ms Shipley) intended to speak to it. I have a couple of simple questions about costs. Will the Minister or the hon. Lady tell us the Department's estimated cost of complying with the legislation? I realise that the checks carried out by the Department and the access to the list that it will make available mirror the service that the Department already provides, but it is anticipated that there will be a substantial increase in the number of applications for information from the list and I assume that the Department has estimated the cost of that.

The appeal tribunal that is to be set up will incur costs, and I cannot see in the documents any estimate of those costs. The Minister will be unable to give accurate figures, but it would help if he gave the Committee an indication of the order of magnitude of the costs.

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Health (Mr. John Hutton)
: Very briefly, those matters are dealt with in the regulatory impact assessment that accompanied the Bill, and were the subject of a short debate on the money resolution about two weeks ago. I cannot tell the hon. Gentleman what the additional running costs are likely to be, but we estimate that approximately £500,000 will be needed for the extra cost of the appeal tribunal.

Any additional running costs will have to be met from the Department's existing budget, but I cannot state in pounds, shillings and pence the additional cost of administering the Bill.

Question put and agreed to.

Clause 11 ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Mr. Andrew F. Bennett
(Denton and Reddish)
: I beg to move amendment No. 31, in page 8, line 13, leave out "18" and insert "16".

I simply want to ask my hon. Friend why 18 was used in the definition of "child". There are some hulks of 16 or 17 who would not like to be described as children and it seems particularly inappropriate to describe a married couple as children.

Ms Debra Shipley
(Stourbridge)
: I cannot accept the amendment. The Bill provides protection for all children and a child is defined in the Children Act 1989 as a person aged under 18. There can be no justification for not applying the protection afforded by the Bill to all children up to that age.

The definition is also consistent with other legislation, in particular the Police Act 1997, which allows criminal checks to be made by the criminal records bureau in relation to positions involving regular caring for, or training, supervising, or being in sole charge of, persons under the age of 18. That is also consistent with the stance that has been adopted by the interdepartmental working group, which is seeking to prevent unsuitable people from working with children and which recommends an integrated system of vetting and checks in relation to children up to the age of 18.

Mr. Hutton
: The age of 18 is also consistent with the European convention on human rights, which defines a child as a person who is under 18. That definition has also been used by the convention on the rights of the child. It would therefore be undesirable, and probably inappropriate, not to extend the Bill's protections to all children.

My hon. Friend the Member for Stourbridge may be interested to learn that we are broadly trying to align the list that we maintain in the Department of Health with the list that is maintained by the Department for Education and Employment. The Secretary of State for Education and Employment uses a similar age limit in relation to List 99—the DFEE scheme refers to a person who has not attained the age of 19.

For those reasons—as well as those that were given by my hon. Friend—we agree that the Bill should make provision for children up to the age of 18.

Mr. Bennett
: In view of those statements, I beg to ask leave to withdraw the amendment.

Amendment, by leave, withdrawn.
