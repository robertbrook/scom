Instead of adopting that broad-brush, steamroller approach, if one asks Ministers and those outside Government to answer detailed questions, one gets those answers in the end.

First, one writes and asks, "Can we please have such and such?"—and the letter gets lost. The second stage is to write again—and the Department writes back to say no. The third stage is to get two and a half pages that explain in detail why each point one is trying to achieve and the way in which one wants to achieve them will not be possible. At that stage, the Department and its advisors are listening to the arguments and considering points of view.
