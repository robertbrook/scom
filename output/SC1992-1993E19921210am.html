The amendments would remove any arrangements for the appointment, resignation and reappointment of members of the grant-maintained committee on an agreed syllabus conference. I am surprised that Opposition Members have tabled the amendments. The subsections that they would remove merely follow the arrangements under schedule 5 of the Education Act 1944 with respect to the other four existing committees of the agreed syllabus conference. I am sure that Opposition Members will realise that it is necessary to have a mechanism for dealing with appointments to and resignations and withdrawals from such committees. The arrangements in clause 10(5) and 10(6) are the most sensible way in which to proceed.

The Committee will know that the agreed syllabus conference determines the syllabus in a locality for the vital subject or religious education, which is an important part of the curriculum. Clause 10 provides that grant-maintained schools should be involved in that process and that a separate committee should reflect their distinctive contribution. It is clear that such schools will have an increasingly distinctive point of view as they grow and their number increases.

Clause 10(5) requires the LEA, when convening a conference, to ensure that the representatives appointed to the new fifth committee, the grant-maintained committee, are acceptable to a majority of relevant grant-maintained schools. That means that an LEA must take all reasonable steps to ensure that the committee is representative. It should consult the Church of England, other denominations and teacher association committees. The procedure is on all fours with that of the other committees. It is strange that the Opposition propose procedural differences between committees. The components committees of the agreed syllabus conference are supposed to work uniformly.

Clause 10(5) provides a safeguard for the LEA. Once the LEA has taken reasonable steps, the proceedings of the agreed syllabus conference cannot be invalidated on the grounds that the grant-maintained committee is unrepresentative. The hon. Member for Stretford mentioned that on Tuesday. If a grant-maintained school is unwilling to agree representation, the vital work of the conference will continue. An individual grant-maintained school could not veto the conference's work. The LEA will be responsible for convening the conferences. I am surprised that the Opposition have tabled an amendment that would weaken the LEA's position.

Clause 10(6) provides for circumstances in which members of a grant-maintained committee resign or withdraw or require the LEA to make a fresh appointment to fill a vacancy. There is no difficulty with the provision, which is drawn from and inspired by schedule 5 of the 1944 Act. It is slightly different, because it makes clear that reappointments should be made.

The procedures described in subsections (5) and (6) are reasonable and in the spirit of the 1944 Act. They reflect the importance that the Government attach to religious education and should command the sympathetic support 612 of the Committee. I hope that the hon. Member for Stretford will withdraw the amendment or, if he presses it to a vote, that the Committee will reject it.
