I am advised that there should not be a problem. 1334 I am told that authorities are well aware of the proposed principles for buying net of tax when drawing up their management schemes, and there seems no reason to suppose that their arrangements will need to be changed at this stage to take account of the net of tax arrangements. The right hon. Gentleman also raised a question regarding net of tax arrangements and, I think, powers of entry. The point he made was that a local authority, instead of agreeing a current use value with the vendor, or if the vendor was reluctant to agree the authority's current use value, might say "We will not bother about this. It is true that the authority need not send a notice of acquisition to the Board until the compensation has been agreed or determined, but if the vendor supplied the necessary information direct to the Board it could compute his liability and repay any excess of the amount deducted by the authority.

The protection is, therefore, twofold. There is the protection of having to pay compensation and interest, and there is the protection of the Board of Inland Revenue, which will stand behind him, as it were, and the Board will then have the information and can repay any excess of any amount eventually deducted by the authority.

I do not think this will present a problem in practice. I cannot envisage local authorities operating in that way, and I think that these safeguards are quite sufficient to prevent them from doing so, even if they wanted to.
