Mr. Ian Stewart
: Before we leave the clause, I should like to put a few remarks on the record. I am very glad to see that there is included among those bodies which are totally exempt from development land tax one which does not perhaps fit exactly into the pattern of the others and which is not a local authority in the absolute sense of the word. I refer to the Letchworth Garden City Corporation. I rise to speak on this occasion, if I may so put it, as the hon. Member not for Hitching but for Letchworth. I should like to say how pleased I am that for the purposes of this legislation, as for many other statutory purposes, the Letchworth Garden City Corporation has been included along with local authorities.

When the White Paper was published, the Garden City Corporation was not included among the bodies to be exempt. However, representations were made to the Government by representatives of the corporation last November, and these resulted in its inclusion. As I expect that similar representations may have to be made on future occasions in relation to other legislation of this kind, relating to land or other functions of local authorities it might be helpful to record the precedents which justify this decision.

First, the Inland Revenue has agreed that under the Local Government (Financial Provision) Act 1963 the Garden City corporation's issue of bonds falls under the local authority bond provision. In relation to value added tax in the Finance Act 1972, the Customs and Excise informed the corporation that the Commissioners of the Inland Revenue held that the corporation fell within the scope of Section 15(6) of the Finance Act 1972. The Public Works Loan Board has held that the corporation is a local authority for the purposes of the Local Authorities Loans Act 1945, and the then Ministry of JPEG 781 Housing and Local Government held that the corporation is a local authority as defined in Section 221(1) of the Town and Country Planning Act 1962. The Department of the Environment has held that the corporation is a local authority for the purposes of the Town and Country Planning (Grants) Regulations 1968, and the Ministry of Housing and Local Government held that as a local authority the corporation had powers under Section 28(2) of the Town and Country Planning Act 1968. Furthermore, the corporation is a local authority within the meaning of the Local Loans Act 1875, and it has been held by the Special Commissioners that the corporation is a local authority for the purposes of the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1970.

One of my distinguished predecessors, the late Mr. Martin Maddan, when he was the Member of Parliament for Hitchin, had a substantial part to play in the passage and passing of the Letchworth Garden City Corporation Act 1962, by Section (51) of which the Letchworth Garden City Corporation is a public authority. It is therefore entirely appropriate that the Letchworth Garden City Corporation, although its constitution is anomalous in many respects and has not been repeated for other new towns or foundations, should find its place among the exempt bodies. There has been no repetition of the arrangements for the Letchworth Garden City Corporation, but it nevertheless started the whole idea of new towns in this country, and the results of its work can be inspected with approval by anyone who visits the town of Letchworth.

I am glad that the corporation will be able to continue its activities unhindered by a charge to development land tax in the future, because its work dovetails closely with the North Hertfordshire District Council. If it were not excluded, it would create a large number of local difficulties. I am, therefore, grateful for this opportunity to put on record these remarks about the fair town of Letchworth.
