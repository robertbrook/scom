Friend the Member for Maryhill (Mr. Hannan), I gave notice when we were discussing Clause 11 that there was an Amendment on the Order Paper to include clinical psychologists where that was desirable.

In the majority of cases the psychological examination of the handicapped child should be carried out by an educational psychologist. There are, however, a minority of cases in which it might be more appropriate to have a clinical psychologist carrying out the examination. That is a view which, incidentally, the psychologists themselves in general accept. The kind of case which I have in mind is, for example, that of the severely handicapped spastic child in which a clinical psychologist would be a rather more appropriate person in the normal circumstances than an educational psychologist.
