Mr. Millan
: What the Clause does is to make explicit the power of local education authorities to charge fees for voluntary further education as distinct from compulsory further education, which at one time was envisaged through the junior colleges and in the case of facilities provided for social, recreative and cultural activities.

The reason why we need the words which would be deleted by this Amendment is that in the 1962 Act there is no explicit provision for the charging of fees for voluntary further education or for social, recreative or cultural activities. On the other hand there is no explicit prohibition, and the implication of the 1962 Act was that since the charging of fees for those activities was not explicitly barred then authorities had power to charge fees, and, as we know, authorities are making charges at the moment. We thought it right when redrafting these Sections to make this power explicit, since we are bringing the Act up to date and trying to improve the wording of the legislation.

Having made the power explicit I think that if we did not have these words, and were not deemed always to have had them, there would be, by implication, some doubt thrown on the activities of the authorities who in the past have been making these charges without the explicit power. It is for that reason and to meet the facts of the situation that these words are included.

Therefore I hope hon. Gentlemen will accept that to remove these words would cast doubt on what is happening all over Scotland at the present time with regard to the charging of these fees. The Clause as it stands makes no change in the actual position with regard to the charging of fees in further education and the provision of these facilities.
