10.30 a.m.

The Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Mr. Bruce Millan): I beg to move,

That during the further proceedings on the Education (Scotland) Bill the Committee do meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays at half-past Ten o'clock, on Thursday, 6th March, at Four o'clock, and, with effect from Wednesday, 12th March, on Wednesdays at Four o'clock.

This is a Motion of which I gave notice at our meeting last Thursday, but it is in slightly different terms from the notice I gave then. At that time I hoped I should be able to move a Motion that we have the normal Tuesday and Thursday Sittings, and Sittings on Wednesdays at 4 o'clock in the afternoon, but it was represented to me briefly last Thursday, and it has been represented to me since, that it would be unfortunate to meet on Wednesday of this week at 4 o'clock, because the Select Committee on Scottish Affairs will be having its first meeting on Wednesday of this week at 5 o'clock, and a considerable number of Members of this Committee are also Members of the Select Committee. To meet that circumstance, I suggest that this week we should meet not on Wednesday but on Thursday at 4 o'clock, and that we should revert to Wednesdays as from next week.

I am not sure, as matters now stand, when the Select Committee at its first meeting will decide subsequently to meet. I hope very much that it will take account of the fact that next week, at least if this Motion is accepted, we hope to meet in this Committee on Wednesday at 4 o'clock. I also hope very much that we shall not go beyond next week with this Bill, and that with this week's Sittings and next week's Sittings we shall finish it. If the Select Committee decides otherwise, I shall certainly be glad to take any decision of that Committee into account, but as things stand now, since we cannot be sure when the Select Committee is going to meet after Wednesday of this week, it would be better for every- Col. 428 one concerned if we reverted to Wednesdays from next week, because Thursday is normally a rather inconvenient day for Members to sit in the afternoon.

I am anxious that we should have each week an afternoon Sitting rather than an additional morning Sitting for the obvious reason that afternoon Sittings are not specifically limited in time and I wish that we should make progress on the Bill.

We have had nine Sittings so far, during which we have dealt with seven Clauses. Seven of the Sittings were principally taken up with Clause 1, which included a controversial provision. I am not in any way complaining about the length of time that we have taken on the Bill so far. If hon. Gentlemen wish, as they obviously do, and as they obviously have the right to do, to discuss some of these important Clauses at considerable length, I certainly would not complain about that, but I have to protect the rest of the Government business which is to come before the Standing Committee subsequent to this Bill.

I need only mention, for example, the very important Housing Bill which is still to come along, which deals with the recommendations of the Cullingworth Committee on older houses, and also with the question of Government assistance for Glasgow storm damage. Then there is the Town and Country Planning Bill, which is another important Bill to come along later, and we have the usual provision for Estimates days and so on. If we are to get all the business through and protect Estimates days and matter days it is necessary to make rather quicker progress with the Education (Scotland) Bill than we have been able to make so far.

I hope, therefore, that if this Sittings Motion is accepted this morning, we can make considerable progress this week and, if need be, if the debates on the Bill go over into next week, that we can also make considerable progress then and finish the Bill next week at the latest. If we can finish it before that, I should be very pleased indeed. But that is the sort of progress I have in mind now.

It seems to me that we shall not need excessively late afternoon Sittings on this basis, but I am afraid that if I do not move today, and have accepted, this Sittings Motion, a Sittings Motion will Image Col. 429 still be required a little later, and we may then have to have excessively long Sittings, which I should certainly hope to avoid on a Bill of this nature. It is with those considerations in mind that I move the Motion.
