Mr. N. R. Wylie
: I beg to move, Amendment No. 3, in page 4, line 13, at end insert:

(8) The provisions of this section shall apply to any joint planning authority established foi the purpose of preparing a structure plan under the provisions of section 2 of the Act of 1947.

This proposed new subsection refers to the provisions of Section 2 of the principal Act and it raises once again, as was indicated on the last occasion, when we discussed Clause 1, the underlying problem which arises when statutory obligations are imposed in the absence of adequate machinery for carrying them out. I am conscious of the observations which fell from your lips, Mr. Brewis, when we debated the Motion, "That Clause 1 stand part of the Bill" and I will, therefore, take up no more time except to emphasise one fundamental inherent problem—the nature of the duties imposed, and the inadequacy of local planning authorities to cope with them. The Minister of State indicated last time the Committee met that there were a number of planning authorities in existence now which might be able to fulfil these functions, although he was unable to give any indication as to when or which planning authorities would have these duties imposed upon them.

This Amendment is really aimed at that large batch of local planning authorities who could be said to fall into the category of authorities already capable of fulfilling the functions set out in Clause 1 or Clause 2 of this Bill. Once these parts of the Bill are applied to any area or group of authorities, the problem might to some 52 extent be resolved by having resort to the amalgamation provisions in the principal Act, because, under Clause 102(2) of this Bill, it is provided that this Bill and the 1947 Act shall have effect as if this Bill were part of that Act. So, once we have the situation where Part I of this Bill is applied to an area, the provisions of Section 2 and the First Schedule to the 1947 Act come into play.

It will be recalled that Section 2 of the principal Act does make possible the amalgamation of a number of local authorities for any of the purposes of this Bill. In other words, that amalgamation can be brought about either on a voluntary basis or under subsection (3) by order of the Secretary of State. That provision requires that there shall be a public inquiry and it is not a power which the Secretary of State would be likely readily to resort to; but the First Schedule to the 1947 Act makes it quite clear that the joint planning committee set up, or any committee so established, can have delegated to it a wide range of functions. Indeed, paragraph 4 of the First Schedule states that the Secretary of State can make such a committee a body corporate, and Part III deals with the situation where joint advisory committees are set up.

So, the real reason of this Amendment is that, in advance of having these duties imposed, it may be necessary for a group of authorities to get together to work out the details of a survey and what is called a structure plan; but till Part I of this Bill is put into effect there will be no statutory powers for the authorities to get together for this purpose, there would be a purely ad hoc arrangement. This Amendment seeks to give a group of local authorities power to get together for the purpose of preparing what is now called a structure plan. Let us remember that the structure plan is a new thing, not found in the 1947 Act, and Part I must be applied before local authorities can be combined under the provisions of Section 2.

This Amendment is also put forward partly in the nature of a probe because we should like to know how the grouping of the smaller authorities, quite distinct from the larger authorities which we discussed last time, will be treated. This Amendment gives power to local authorities, and particularly those of the JPEG 53 smaller kind, for the purpose of getting together to prepare structure plans. Having got together for that purpose and got themselves organised, then it would be appropriate to impose these duties on them. Yet nothing is more ridiculous than to impose these obligations on quite minute authorities without first having grouped them together so that they can meet their responsibilities under this Clause.
