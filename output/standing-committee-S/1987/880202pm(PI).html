The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Lord James Douglas-Hamilton)
: I wish to give some more information on below tolerable standards. The hon. Member for East Lothian (Mr. Home Robertson) was given a comprehensive reply on 30 October 1987, and 262 Hansard at that time set out, district by district, the houses below tolerable standards as on 31 March 1987, from the returns of local authorities. From that parliamentary answer, the hon. Gentleman will see that there was a very small number in the public sector compared with the considerable numbers in the private sector.

Below tolerable standard houses are almost all privately owned, and are either owner-occupied or privately tenanted. About 2 per cent. of the total housing stock is now below the tolerable standard, and the figure has been reduced by more than half since 1979. The latest figure is 55,000, based on local authority returns for March 1987; 57,000 was an early analysis of the same but incomplete data. The data were completed recently, revealing a final figure of 55,000. The figures which I gave this morning have dropped steadily from 150,000 in 1977 to the figure for 1987 which I have already given.

The number of houses of below tolerable standard has come down in large measure due to the work of the housing associations and to improvement grants. The big difference is that in past decades, progress was achieved through demolition and slum clearance. In the present decade, there has been considerable emphasis on the renewal and improvement of houses, and of keeping communities intact, which has been of substantial benefit to them.
