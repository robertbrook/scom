He quoted his favourite White Paper to argue that the Government's intentions were entirely above board and reasonable and that everything would be sweetness and light.

The Opposition have their doubts about the provision. We have made it clear that the Labour party believes in local democracy and believes that the local authority should give the lead in these functions, if necessary with the back-up and co-ordination of the research and funding of Scottish Homes.

I want to put the Under-Secretary of State on the spot. Can he tell us which body he believes will give the lead and initiate housing policy in the districts in Scotland? Will it be the local authorities, as is the present statutory position, or will it be Scottish Homes? In case the Under-Secretary tries to find a form of words to get out of that, can he tell us who will get the housing budget in future? That is the crucial issue. Who will spend the money? The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Lord James Douglas-Hamilton): The hon. Gentleman asked which would be the lead body. The answer is that it depends on circumstances.
