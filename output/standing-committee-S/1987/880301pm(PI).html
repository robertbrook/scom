The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Lord James Douglas-Hamilton)
: It may be helpful to Members if I make a number of summary points on the changes that I announced this morning on the right to buy cost floor arrangements.

Cost floor means the price below which the sale price cannot fall. Outstanding debt means the original debt less the debt that has been repaid, and historic costs are those incurred by the authority. Those definitions may be obvious, but I thought that I should mention them.

1114 We are replacing the present test, in which the debt outstanding on the house is the crucial factor, with a cost floor based on historic costs—the actual costs incurred by the authority in providing or improving the house since the relevant date. That date will be the beginning of the financial year five years before the right to buy application is made. Thus, if a local authority tenant applies to buy his house in November 1988, the cost floor will be the total costs incurred by the authority on that house since 1 April 1983. The price that the tenant has to pay for his house will not fall below that figure. This date will roll forward each year. So, if a tenant applies in July 1989, the cost floor will be the costs incurred on the house since April 1984. For local authorities, 1 April is the beginning of the financial year.

As a result of the change that we announced, some tenants will be able to buy their houses at a local price. Those tenants who decide to remain as local authority tenants will not, however, be badly affected.
