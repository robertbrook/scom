It seems that we are never able to conduct Scottish business without getting lost in arguments about procedure, the size of the Committee, and so on. I understand that you have no responsibility for appointments to the Committee or for its size, but somehow we seem unable to regulate our business through the usual channels as other Standing Committees do. On this occasion, the difficulties turn on the fact that the Government have appointed to the Committee the hon. and learned Member for Perth and Kinross (Mr. Fairbairn) who is currently very ill. We are all very concerned for him and send him our warmest wishes for a speedy recovery. We should like to see him with us, but if his illness is as serious as is reported in the press we must conclude that he will not be able to attend the sittings of this Committee. My hon. Friend the Member for Dumbarton (Mr. McFall) and I therefore sought to persuade the Government through the usual channels that it would be in the interests of the Committee if the hon. and learned Member for Perth and Kinross were released from the service of the Committee and another Member appointed. It would not be appropriate to go into detail as the discussions were confidential, but all manner of arguments ensued about the size of the Committee, and so on. The Government wanted to increase the size of the Committee.

The point that I wish to bring to your attention, Mr. Knox, is that under the Standing Orders of the House 16 Scottish Members—hon. Members representing Scottish constituencies—are required to serve on a Standing Committee of the House when it is considering Scottish business. Surely it was in the Government's power to appoint another Scottish Member to the Committee. Had they taken that action we would not have had any of our difficulties about starting the Committee, the date of the sittings motion and all the rest of it.

There is something wrong here and I seek your guidance, Mr. Knox, about how we might proceed. I am anxious to be as constructive and helpful as possible in our proceedings today. Is there some way in which we can tell the Government through the usual channels or whatever means are open to us that the appointment Col. 4 of the hon. and learned Member for Perth and Kinross is not appropriate?
