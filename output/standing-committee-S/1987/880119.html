debates

Housing (Scotland) Bill

10.30 am

Mr. John Home Robertson (East Lothian): On a point of order, Mr. Martin. I welcome you and your co-Chairman, Mr. Lambie, to the Committee. I am sure that you will show your customary impartiality on all housing matters and that you will be most interested to hear our discussions on inner city and new town housing in due course.

My point of order concerns the strange and unprecedented circumstances in which the Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland, the hon. Member for Edinburgh, West (Lord James Douglas-Hamilton—

The Chairman: Order. There is a Member of the House here who is not a member of the Committee. The hon. Member for Falkirk, West (Mr. Canavan) is not entitled to be present in the Committee during our proceedings. If he wishes to remain, I must ask him to use the Public Gallery facilities. [Interruption.] Order. I have given my ruling. I must ask the hon. Gentleman to leave. If he does not—

Mr. George Galloway (Glasgow, Hillhead): Will you take a point of order, Mr. Martin?

The Chairman: I cannot take any points of order. Unless the hon. Member for Falkirk, West leaves, I shall have to suspend the sitting. That is my ruling. I ask the hon. Gentleman to leave. [Interruption.] In that case, I must suspend the sitting until the hon. Member leaves. The sitting is suspended for five minutes.

10.31 am

Sitting suspended.

10.36 am

On resuming

The Chairman: I thought that the five-minute suspension would help to cool matters down. I have asked the hon. Member for Falkirk, West to leave because he has no right to be in the Committee. I am bound by the rules of the House and I must ask him to leave. He has no right to be in the Committee or to make any contribution to our proceedings.

Mr. Galloway: On a point of order, Mr. Martin.

The Chairman: I cannot deal with points of order until the hon. Member for Falkirk, West has left the Committee.

Mr. Galloway: My point of order may help us out of the difficulty, Mr. Martin.

Col. 4

The Chairman: I am sorry, but until anyone not entitled to be here has left I cannot take any points of order. I cannot proceed with the Committee until the hon. Member for Falkirk, West has left. I ask him to respect my decision.

Mr. Galloway; How can it be in order—

The Secretary of State for Scotland (Mr. Malcolm Rifkind): Order.

Mr. Home Robertson: Who do you think you are?

Mr. Rifkind: Grow up!

Mr. Home Robertson: Try and get yourself elected.

The Chairman: Order. I ask the hon. Member for Falkirk, West to leave the proceedings of this Committee and allow us to get on with the business in hand. [Interruption.] Order. I am in a very difficult position. If the hon. Gentleman does not leave, I shall have to suspend the sitting again. We cannot proceed until the hon. Gentleman leaves.

Mr. Galloway: The former mayor of Kensington and Chelsea, the hon. Member for Wanstead and Woodford (Mr. Arbuthnot) is sitting stuffing envelopes in Committee on a Scottish Bill. That sums up the situation. It shows contempt for our proceedings.

Mr. Allan Stewart (Eastwood): Go back to Greece.

Mr. Galloway: That is very witty—very droll. Stuffing envelopes shows contempt for the Committee.

Several hon. Members rose—

The Chairman: Order. I cannot take points of order now. When I am standing, no one else should be on his feet. I ask the hon. Member for Falkirk, West to leave. Will he do so? [Interruption.] In those circumstances, I am afraid I shall have to suspend the sitting again. We are taking up valuable time. I will suspend the sitting until 10.55 and I ask the Whips to try to sort the matter out during the suspension.

10.39 am

Sitting suspended.

10.55 am

On resuming—

The Chairman: Order. This is the second time that the Committee has had to be suspended. The hon. Member for Falkirk, West is still present within the Committee area. I ask him once again if he will leave the proceedings.

My authority has been flouted by the hon. Member. I cannot continue to suspend the Committee, so I have no alternative but to adjourn the proceedings until Thursday.

Committee adjourned at five minutes to Eleven o'clock till Thursday 21 January at half past Ten o'clock.
