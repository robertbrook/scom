Mr. Stewart
: I am delighted about the prompt start that we are making to our proceedings this morning. I shall not delay the Committee.

The schedule is consequential to clause 33, which we have already discussed. The schedule contains minor and consequential amendments to the Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1972. In particular, they provide an enabling power for an authority to carry out a survey of the whole or part of its district, if it thinks fit.

The provisions in clause 33 and in the schedule deliver our commitment to maintain the key components of the current planning system, so ensuring stability in the transition to me new structure. A number of detailed comments on the planning system were made when we discussed clasue 33. A certain amount of unease has been expressed about the continued effectiveness of the planning system in general. Indeed, some commentators have suggested that we ought to have taken the opportunity of local government reform to change the basis of the present planning system rather than seek to retain it in its present form. While we are aware of some criticisms that have been levelled against the working of the planning system—as set out in schedule 4—mainly in terms of cumbersome procedures and delays in decision making, we concluded that, in the interests of causing least disruption to business and industry, it would be prudent to concentrate on ensuring that the new structure is bedded down first, as set out in clause 33 and in the schedule.

I am keen that the new authorities should have the best possible framework for delivering the planning functions in a truly professional manner. Within the next few months, we shall publish a consultation paper, inviting people's views on the working of the planning system, as set out in clause 33 and the schedule. We shall invite ideas on how the system might be improved. The consultation paper does not conflict with schedule 4.

I shall be happy to answer any questions. I commend the schedule to the Committee.
