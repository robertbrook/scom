: I beg to move amendment No. 248, in page 38, line 16, leave out from "Authority," to end of line 20 and insert—

"() Section 9 of this Act shall apply to any person transferred to the Authority under this section as if any reference in that section to a new authority included a reference to the Authority.".

 The amendment clarifies the transfer of functions, staff, property, rights, liabilities and obligations from Strathclyde regional council as the current passenger transport authority to the new Strathclyde passenger transport authority as from 1 April 1996.

Clause 40(2) provides that the functions, staff, property, rights, liabilities and obligations of the existing PTA will transfer to the new PTA on 1 April 1996, but it goes on to provide that the order-making powers of the Secretary of State in chapters 2 and 3 would also apply to the transfer insofar as it dealt with staff, property, rights, liabilities and obligations.

It is clear that automatic transfer and vesting is the most straightforward way of dealing with the transfer of functions, staff, property, rights, liabilities and obligations between the two authorities, and it is therefore considered that the references to chapters 2 and 3, which are deleted by this amendment, are unnecessary.

It is clearly right that any staff who might be transferred to the new authority should not be disadvantaged in any way. The amendment makes it clear that they will retain continuity of employment by the application to them of the provisions in clause 9. The amendment clarifies and simplifies the transfer between the two authorities and makes suitable provision for any staff who might go to the new authority. I therefore commend it to the Committee.
