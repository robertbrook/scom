Mr. Home Robertston
: Before we broke for dinner, I was giving the example of Archerfield in my constituency where the presence of a powerful, effective, independent strategic planning authority has been able to protect the community and the landscape. The prospective developer had persuaded the local planning authority, the district council, that it was a price worth paying to give it planning consent for 500 houses in the area so that it would spend the necessary money on redeveloping the old building, associated golf course, the hotel and other leisure facilities.

The local planning authority was, understandably, quite rightly, keen on that redevelopment and on such input into the local economy. It did not want to concede to the housing, but it thought that that was the price that it would have to pay. That would have been the end of the story had there been only a single-tier planning authority. As the strategic planning authority, Lothian region could, however, call in the application and, as a consequence of its intervention, 25 houses, instead of 500 houses, were part of the package. That is a graphic example of the achievement of the strategic planning authority. That is an example of development control.

I could have cited a similar case to show the strategic planning authority's ability to co-ordinate and promote positive development. I could give the example of a major economic development plan, such as that in West Lothian in the constituency of my hon. Friend the Member for Linlithgow, or of transport and highways. Much can and has been achieved by the regional planning authorities and it would be a tragedy if that were to be lost now. The only way of reintroducing that safeguard into the new system would be through the amendments, which would provide a statutory requirement for structure planning areas to be designated by the Secretary of State and appropriate boards, with teeth, to be established.
