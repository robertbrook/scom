Mr. Tam Dalyell (Linlithgow): I beg to move amendment No. 168, in page 12, line 40, at end insert

", and any such scheme shall continue in effect the provisions of any scheme made by an existing authority in accordance with Section 105 of the Education (Scotland) Act 1980 the terms of which have been agreed by a new authority or authorities.".

The purpose of the amendment is simply to ensure that the transfer of educational endowments to the new authorities is by agreement between authorities rather than on the decision of the Secretary of State. The justification for that is that the transfer of educational endowments should as far as possible be by agreement between authorities rather than on the decision of an individual Minister.

Clause 17(7) limits the Secretary of State's powers only to making no more changes than appear to him to be necessary or expedient following the local government boundary changes. No recognition is therefore given to possible agreements reached between authorities. Clause 17(6) details the extent to which the Secretary of State may act but that overlaps with section 105 of the Education (Scotland) Act 1980. It is important that clarification is sought between this provision in the Bill and that section of the 1980 Act. It is that clarification that I seek.
