The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Lord James Douglas-Hamilton)

That the Chairman do now report to the House that the Committee has considered Class XIV, votes 7 and 8.

I am glad to have the opportunity to speak about housing in Scotland and to hear hon. Members' views on the many important issues involved. The Government have always placed a strong emphasis on pursuing a housing policy which seeks to offer the people of Scotland better housing in terms of choice, quality and value for money, and it gives me great satisfaction to see that this policy, pursued over the past 10 years, is making steady progress.

I make no apology for beginning with home ownership. The reason is simple. It is what most people want. It is clear from repeated surveys that between 70 per cent. and 80 per cent. of people want to own their own home. We have already made tremendous strides in enabling people to own their homes. When we came into office in 1979, only 35 per cent. of households owned their own homes. Now over 55 per cent. do so, and I believe that we are well on the way towards achieving our target of 60 per cent. That has mainly been achieved through the sale of over 284,000 public sector homes to tenants through the right to buy by the end of last year. That represents 27.6 per cent. of the total public sector stock as at 1979.

When I became a Minister we were 5 percentage points behind England on the percentage of public sector stock sold to sitting tenants. We are now ahead of England and the gap is widening all the time. Some 26.8 per cent. of the public sector stock has been sold to tenants in England and 27.6 per cent. is the figure in Scotland. That is significant.
