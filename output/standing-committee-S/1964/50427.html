The Minister of State for Scotland (Mr. George Willis)
: This Amendment at first sight might seem rather attractive because it deals with something which is of exceptional importance in the Highlands. Member for Inverness (Mr. Russell Johnston) wishes it to do, and there is, therefore, no need for his Amendment.

Once again, this is a matter of selecting one aspect for mention while not mentioning many other important aspects, equally important in many cases. If we were to provide that the Board should urgently submit a transport plan, we ought also to include that it should submit a land use plan, or a tourism plan, or an industrial plan, or an education plan, or something else. All these things are equally important and it would be 416 quite wrong to select one aspect while leaving out these others.

The hon. Member for Inverness asked what would happen to the Highland Transport Board. The Transport Board is now engaged in a comprehensive study of transport in the Highlands and Islands and its remit is to make recommendations for an adequate, efficient and integrated transport system. Its report will be available within a year to 18 months. It is proposed to allow the Transport Board to complete its remit. What will happen then is that the Highland Development Board will take over from the Transport Board the function of keeping under constant review all transport matters in the Highlands. In other words, the work now being done by the Transport Board will be carried on by the Development Board when the Transport Board has completed its present remit. The report of the Transport Board is to be a report to the Secretary of State and the Minister of Transport, but on completion it will be made available to the Development Board in the same way as the report on the study of the Highlands, now being made for the Secretary of State, will be available to the Development Board.

I hope that in the light of these considerations hon. Members will agree that the Amendment hardly seems necessary. It would call into question the Board's other functions. The Board's terms of reference are sufficiently wide to include all these matters and the Amendment would weaken rather than strengthen the Clause. I hope that the hon. Member for Inverness will consider withdrawing the Amendment.
