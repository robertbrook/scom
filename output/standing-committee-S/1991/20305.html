Gentlemen shake their heads. What would happen under the convention scheme is that the amount of money allocated to the Assembly would go down. "One is tempted to say 'Let the Scots have their heads; let us continue this devolution exercise; let us consider this monstrous constitutional step as no more than an experiment in advancing democracy'. Local government would have to go cap in hand to an Assembly that has no control over the majority of its revenue. The amount of Government support as part of local authority expenditure is 60 per cent. higher in Scotland than in England, and 40 per cent. of local authority debt is in respect of Scottish local authorities.

What about Europe? Who would speak for Scotland in Europe? The convention offers an office in Brussels and a Image Col. 9 man to attend the delegation, but who would speak in Europe? It would be the voice of Westminster; the voice of Scotland would be marginalised.

Perhaps the hon. Member for Cathcart will answer the question put by the hon. Member for Banff and Buchan (Mr. Salmond), who is not present either. What would happen at a meeting of the Council of Ministers if the Scottish Parliament wanted the social charter and the Westminster Parliament did not? Westminster's view would prevail.

All that the convention scheme would achieve would be the creation of a vast bureaucracy with extra civil servants and costs. The money would be better spent on extra home helps and nursery teachers. [HON. MEMBERS: "What a cheek."] Hon. Members will be aware of the extent to which both of those groups have expanded under the Government.

The Government stand to improve the machinery of Government, not to extend it. We believe in the diffusion of power, not confusion of power. We believe in giving power to people through NHS trusts, school boards and tenant housing co-operatives. We believe that devolution would be a one-way ticket to independence. We believe in unity that permits and encourages diversity—power to people, not to politicians.

The Government and the Conservative party stand four square behind the Union. We will fight to defend it and in the weeks ahead we will put the case for maintaining the Union before the people—a Union within which Scotland's voice is strong and a Union that will never be cast aside lightly, as Opposition Members have done for party political advantage.
