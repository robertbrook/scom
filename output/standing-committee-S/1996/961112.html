The issues contained in the Bill are of fundamental importance to the rule of law. It is vital that all parts of the Bill receive the scrutiny that they deserve. We must therefore ensure that the Committee makes good progress in considering the matters before it. Naturally, I hope that we can be suitably thorough without causing any unnecessary inconvenience to Ministers.

It is absolutely right that we should devote whatever time is necessary to effect the passage of the Bill because it is one of the most significant pieces of legislation on crime and sentencing for years and it is in the interests of the public's protection. The Bill is designed to meet the needs and demands of the people of Scotland for protection. The sooner the provisions are in statute, the safer our country will be. The public must have confidence and faith in the system, which exists to defend their fundamental rights and freedoms. That is what the Bill seeks to achieve.

We need sufficient time in Committee to do justice to the Bill. It covers changes in five major areas: minimum sentences, increased court powers, abolition of parole and automatic early release, more controls on teenage drinking and action to control the spiralling costs of criminal legal aid. The necessary time has to be made available in Committee because the Bill covers a lot of ground.

This significant Bill will make the punishment fit the crime. Sentences served will be the sentences handed down by the court, and repeat drug traffickers will face a minimum of seven years in gaol. Repeat sexual and violent offenders will face a mandatory life sentence. Image Col. 5 Even if they are subsequently released, they will be supervised for the rest of their lives and may be recalled to prison at any time. The Bill's provisions mean that people who commit serious crimes will go to prison and stay there until they are no longer a threat to public safety. That is why the Bill merits the Committee's serious and in-depth consideration. If we take seriously our responsibility to protect the Scottish people, we cannot afford to delay the Bill's passage on to the statute book. The motion will allow sufficient time for the discussion of the important matters that I have mentioned. I hope that we have a satisfactory discussion of the sitting arrangements through the usual channels.
