Mr. Michael Brown
(Brigg and Cleethorpes)
: I am glad that we have had a break, because I realise, incredible though it is, that the amendment is indeed in the name of the honourable Member for Fife, Central (Mr. McLeish). I find it difficult to believe the amendment paper.

Subsection (3) provides an opportunity for opted-out schools to offer additional education, apart from the statutory requirement to educate children. I have deliberated on the effects, were the amendment accepted. I had a conversation just before the Easter recess with a headmaster of a school in my constituency who proudly told me that his school offered education to adults who wanted to take GCSEs and A-levels—the examinations are different in Scotland— alongside the education provided for schoolchildren.

Amendment No. 39 would rule out opportunities that we all wish to see extended to adults. Several schools in my area have voluntarily provided adult education, which is given in the classroom at the same time as the schoolchildren are being educated. One 70-year-old is taking an A-level course at Sir John Nelthorpe school in my constituency.

Clause 7(3) would enable an opted-out school to become part of the community. Whether schools are opted-out schools, local authority maintained schools, private schools or public schools, surely we all agree that in any community in the United Kingdom schools should maximise their facilities and opportunities not only for schoolchildren but for the whole community. Of all the provisions, subsection (3) should commend itself to Opposition Members.

On the Floor of the House the criticism is often made that Governments do not encourage the maximum use of school facilities for adult education. It is said that during school holidays local education authorities do not make available their school premises and other services. Subsection (3) enables an opted-out school to maximise its facilities for the benefit of the 314 whole community. An opted-out school may wish to take advantage of a particular expertise. For example, a Russian teacher may wish to run a summer school for the local community.
