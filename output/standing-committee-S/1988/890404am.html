Mr. Henry McLeish
(Fife, Central)
: I beg to move amendment No. 58, in page 4, line 2, leave out "suitable" and insert "adequate".

I hope that you are refreshed after the Easter break, Mr. Hogg, and ready to enjoy the fruits of the Committee, as are we, on the Opposition Benches.

Amendment No. 58 is a fairly straightforward, probing amendment. In section 1(1) of the Education (Scotland) Act 1980, the words "adequate" and "efficient" are used. We are concerned to find out why the word "adequate" has been changed to "suitable". The change may imply a multitude of sins or there may simply have been a change of wording without much thought having been given to it. The Committee would like to hear the Minister's comment on why the wording has changed.

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Mr. Michael Forsyth)
: The honourable Gentleman is right. I recognise the origins of the amendment. Under section 1 of the 1980 Act, education authorities are required to provide adequate and efficient school education for their areas. The point is that the term "adequate" refers to an education authority which has a territorial responsibility to look at its whole area, decide how many school places it needs to provide, and provide them. In relation to a self-governing school, the term cannot have such a clear meaning, and that is why we preferred to use the term "suitable" in the Bill. Unless the honourable Gentleman thinks that there is a new plot, he should look at section 99 of the 1980 Act which defines the circumstances in which the Secretary of State can serve a notice of complaint on the proprietor of an independent school. One of the circumstances is where the school is not providing

"efficient and suitable instruction … having regard to the ages and sex of the pupils attending thereat".

The word "suitable" is simply a more appropriate term to use in the context of a school rather than an education authority. On that basis, I hope that the honourable Gentleman, having probed, will withdraw his amendment.

Mr. McLeish
: The Minister surely cannot conceive that I would think that there was a plot behind any clause in the Bill. We were worried that the change 278 from adequacy to suitability could mean a number of things which, in view of the Minister's comments, we shall not go into. The wording of an Act is important, but unless my honourable Friends wish to add further comments, we shall accept that the changed nature of an opted-out school means that the word "suitable" is more appropriate. However, we would make the point that "adequate" should always be the minimum that children can expect from their schooling, and our concern was that there should be any suggestion that schooling would be less than adequate. If suitability means what the Minister is suggesting, we are happy to accept that. On that basis, I await comment before withdrawing the amendment.
