Mrs. Fyfe
: Both amendments are straightforward and easily understood. They aim to extend the procedures for consulting the local community about the proposals for school self-government. Other individuals besides parents, or other bodies may have a stake in the future management of the school. They include members of the community who use the school or its facilities outside school hours. Although those individuals or bodies may make representations to the Secretary of State, our amendments would require school boards to initiate consultations with parents and other individuals or bodies affected by the proposed changes. Public meetings, for example, would allow people to air their views, debate them with each other and thereby see the force of each other's arguments. Such consultations, important for any school, are especially important in relatively isolated areas where the school's role as a focus of community life goes far beyond its basis task.

The amendments are highly reasonable and a natural follow-on to the concessions that were made last Thursday night. We assume that the Government will accept the principle behind them.
