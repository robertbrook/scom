First Scottish Standing Committee
Tuesday 14 March 1989

[MR. MICHAEL J. MARTIN in the Chair]

SELF-GOVERNING SCHOOLS ETC. (SCOTLAND) BILL

10.30 am

Motion made.

That during the proceedings on the Self-Governing Schools etc. (Scotland) Bill, the Committee do meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays at half-past Ten o'clock and at half-past Four o'clock. - [Mr. Michael Forsyth.]

The Chairman
: Order. I note that three hon. Members are present who are not entitled to be on the Committee. I ask them to leave so that we can get on with our proceedings.

Mrs. Margaret Ewing
(Moray)
: On a point of order, Mr. Martin.

THE CHAIRMAN
: Order. I shall not take points of order. The Committee is not properly constituted. Three hon. Members will have to leave and I have asked them to do so. [Interruption.] Order. As the three hon. Members have not responded to my request, I take it that they are unwilling to leave the Committee. Therefore, I suspend the sitting for 10 minutes.

10.31 am

Sitting suspended.

10.41 am

On resuming—

The Chairman
: I ask the three hon. Members to leave the Committee to allow us to proceed.

Mr David Maclean
(Penrith and The Border)
: I beg to move,

That the Chairman do report the hon. Members for Banff and Buchan (Mr. Salmond), for Glasgow, Govan (Mr. Sillars) and for Angus, East (Mr. Welsh) to the House.

4

Question put,

The Committee proceeded to a Division.

Mrs Margaret Ewing
: On a point of order, Mr. Martin. If, as you say the Committee is not properly constituted, is it possible to hold a Division?

The Chairman
: I would not be doing so if it were not possible.

The Committee having divided: Ayes 17, Noes 1.
