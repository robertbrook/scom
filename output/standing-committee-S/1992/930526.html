4.30 pm

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary for Scotland (Mr Allan Stewart)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the draft Alcan Aluminium UK Ltd. (Rateable Values) (Scotland) Order 1993.

The Chairman
: With the agreement of the Committee, it will be convenient to discuss at the same time the other orders before us, namely, the draft Caledonian MacBrayne Limited (Rateable Values) (Scotland) Order 1993 and the draft Forth Ports plc (Rateable Values) (Scotland) Order 1993.

Mr. Stewart
: The order relates to the valuation of Alcan Aluminium UK Ltd., formerly British Alcan Primary and Recycling Ltd. This order would normally have been included with the bulk of the formula-valued companies which were debated and approved by the House in March. However, a change in the company's name required the original draft to be withdrawn, amended and relaid. Notification was received too late for this order to be included with those orders.

The order prescribes the rateable value for qualifying lands and heritage of the company for 1993–94. It updates the prescribed rateable value for the defined lands and heritages for this year to take account of material change in circumstances and the revised level of industrial derating.

I assure the Committee that the order has been subject to consultations with the company, the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and the Scottish Assessors Association, and no substantive comments have been received.

The other two orders before us prescribed rateable values for 1993–94 for the operational lands and heritages of Forth Ports plc and Caledonian MacBrayne Ltd. Forth Ports and CalMac are treated separately from other statutory dock and harbour authorities because in each case the land involved spans more than one valuation area. Consequently, the valuations for the two undertakings are calculated not by individual assessors from different authorities, but centrally, following the same method as is applied to other statutory port authorities, then apportioned among the local authorities concerned.

The method of assessment, as hon. Members are aware, is to assess rateable value at 9 per cent. of the relevant income of the authority. Apportionment is on the basis of the relevant income for each area.

The assessments and provisions in the orders have been subject to consultations with the local authorities concerned, the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and the Scottish Assessors Association, and they are content with the provisions. The orders are not controversial, and I commend them to the Committee.
