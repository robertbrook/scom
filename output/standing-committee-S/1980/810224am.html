10.30 am

Question again proposed, That the amendment be made.

The Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Mr. Malcolm Rifkind): Paragraph (a) is the provision of the clause which deals with the .power of the Secretary of State, by order, to amend the definition of "capital expenses" in subsection (2). I (hope .that I can briefly assure the hon Gentleman of the reason why such an amendment makes considerable sense.

The hon Gentleman said that the present definition of "capital expenses" in the 1973 Act seemed to be extremely broad. There are two areas, however, where it may be appropriate, after consultation with COSLA, to consider amendment to the definition of "capital expenses". The definition in the 1973 Act describes capital expenditure as being

"to be met otherwise than out of current revenue."

It is, however, the fact that in a number of authorities it has become the .practice in parts of the United Kingdom, including Scotland, to finance capital expenditure out of current revenue. That is, therefore, a factor which has to be .taken into account when considering the total of capital expenditure of the authority in question, as there is this facility at the present time, and that clearly is not covered by the existing definition of capital expenditure.

There is also a disputed area concerning the construction of the term "current revenue" used in the existing definition. The Secretary of State's view is that if one is financing capital expenditure out of public revenue at the present time, that means that the total financing has to be met within the year in question—within the financial year in which the capital expenditure has been incurred. It has to be financed out of current revenue for that year. Certain individual local authorities, however, have interpreted it in an alternative way, and have suggested that if the annual payments due under an arrangement are each met out of the current revenue of the year in which they fall due, consent under section 94 is not required. It is a point of dispute at the moment. Clearly, it indicates that the present definition is to some extent ambiguous and uncertain.

Col. 574 Clause 23 does not itself propose an alternative definition, but it enables the Secretary of State to produce such a revised definition. Before he did so he would wish to consult the convention, both on the need to amend and on the nature of any amendment that might be appropriate. Without a facility of this kind it would need new primary legislation once that consultation had taken place. So if it were appropriate to make a change, I have indicated two areas where some change is considered appropriate, but I can assure the hon Gentleman that there is no question of any change being made until there has been the fullest discussion with the convention on the points that I have raised.
