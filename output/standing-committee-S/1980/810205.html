I recognise that this debate could well become a clause stand part debate, and I want to avoid that. The provision for comparability with a preceding year has serious implications. Local government is local democracy in action. In the event of a change of political power, of the electorate wishing to have a different administration in local government, there may well be a complete change of emphasis. Therefore, it is not really fair to compare one year's priorities with another's, when a different political emphasis is behind them.

One could give examples of some local authorities in Central Scotland, where nationalist authorities in Clackmannan and Falkirk districts were replaced by Labour authorities. The nationalists tended to be Centre-Right in political persuasion and in their priorities, and Labour local authorities committed to different programmes, which were substantially supported by the electorate, would be coming in with a different mandate. For the Secretary of State to take the preceding years into account, without taking into account the change in the political circumstances, would be an abuse of democracy.

Therefore, we think that this provision is unduly rigid. It does not take account of the political realities. On occasions there can also be changes in population. This was referred to in a previous sitting by my hon Friend the Member for South Ayrshire (Mr. Foulkes), when he was speaking about social work budgets. As fewer old people are dying from one year to another, there are more of them needing care, treatment and support. Therefore, to take one year against another in that instance is not always a fair comparison.

We ask that this phrase "or any preceding" be deleted, not on the grounds that we think that one cannot make comparisons, but rather that there are reasons, especially electoral change of fortune, why such comparisons are not necessarily fair.
