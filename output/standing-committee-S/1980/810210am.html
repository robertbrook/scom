The clause does not in itself introduce any new principle whatever to the Secretary of State's powers. The hon Member for Garscadden nods in agreement even if his hon Friend the Member for Fife, Central (Mr. Hamilton) does not. There have been for some 50 years powers available to the Secretary of State to withdraw grant for unreasonable and excessive expenditure. That may be very controversial. The Opposition may dislike it intensely. Local authorities may hate it in every respect. The fact is that the Labour Government not only lived with these powers but re-enacted them on two specific occasions—under the Attlee Administration and in the 1966 Act. On both occasions a Labour Government, although the powers had not been used since 1929, sought to re-enact them. The right hon Member for Greenock and Port Glasgow (Dr. Mabon), who was the Minister in charge of 1966 measure, defended those provisions as being highly desirable and sensible.
