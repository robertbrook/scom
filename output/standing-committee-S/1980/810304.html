Clause 1 is one of the clauses which made me raise my eyebrows slightly when I first read it, as it is drafted remarkably widely. The key sentence reads:

"'For the purpose of preventing disturbance by the engine noise of vehicles and aircraft to the enjoyment by the public of quiet areas of the countryside, district and islands councils shall, subject to the following provisions of this section, have power by bye-laws to control—

  1. the use on land or waterways of vehicles (including hovercraft and boats); and
  2. the landing and taking off of aircraft."

This is a very wide power at one level but at another level it is strangely limited, because it does refer not to noise in general but to the noise of specific kinds of engines. I am rather curious about the basis on which this was included.
