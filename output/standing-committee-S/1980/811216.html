First Scottish Standing Committee
Tuesday 16 December 1980

[Mr. HARRY GOURLAY in the Chair]

Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Bill

10.30 am

The Under-Secretary of State for Scotland (Mr. Malcolm Rifkind)
: I beg to move,

That during the proceedings on the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Bill the Committee do meet on Tuesday 13 January and thereafter on Tuesdays and Thursdays at half-past Ten o'clock.

 Before speaking to the motion, perhaps I may offer my congratulations—and, I am sure, the congratulations of all my hon Friends—to the hon Members for Clackmannan and East Stirlingshire (Mr. O'Neill) and Glasgow, Garscadden (Mr. Dewar) on their elevation to the Opposition Front Bench. The hon Member for Hamilton (Mr. Robertson), who was to have led for the Opposition in the Committee, has been transferred to another responsibility. I do not know whether it is significant that it has been thought necessary to appoint two hon Gentlemen to speak for the Opposition instead of him. In any case, we offer them our congratulations without reservations, and look forward to their contributions.

The motion is very straightforward. It is proposed that the Committee should not deal with the Bill this morning but that discussion should commence on 13 January. I commend the motion.

Mr. Donald Dewar
(Glasgow, Garscadden)
: First, I thank the Under-Secretary of State for his civilised and civil words, which I hope will set a pattern. My hon Friend the Member for Clackmannan and East Stirlingshire (Mr. O'Neill) and I were both delighted to hear the appointment referred to as an elevation; some of our friends have referred to it in very different terms recently.

We on the Opposition Benches have no objection to what is proposed, and we do not wish to delay the 4 Committee on these matters. We look forward to making reasonable progress when we return on 13 January and plunge into the Methylated Spirits (Sale by Retail) (Scotland) Act 1937. I shall not be giving any hostages to fortune if I say that I am not, in total detail, familiar with that important enactment. No doubt my hon Friend will quickly put that right.

I think that we shall be able to make progress. If clauses 13 and 14 and one or two other minor matters were dropped, we could promise very fast progress indeed.

I do not know whether notes on clauses will be available to the Opposition. 1 believe that it is normal for them to be available.

Mr. Rifkind
: I am anxious to be as helpful to the hon Gentleman and his colleagues as possible, and I shall be happy to facilitate the provision of notes on clauses to hon Members on both sides of the Committee, so that they may familiarise themselves with the more complex details of certain of the provisions.

Question put and agreed to.

The Chairman
: I draw the attention of the Committee to the fact that there is a financial resolution. Copies are available in the Committee Room.

I also draw attention to the fact that adequate notice should be given of amendments. As a general rule it is not my intention to call manuscript amendments.

Resolved,

That the Bill be considered in the following order: Clauses 1 to 21; Schedule 1; Clause 22; Schedule 2; Clauses 23 to 30; Schedule 3; Clause 31; Schedule 4; Clauses 32 and 33; new Clauses; and new Schedules.

[ Mr. Rifkind.]

Further consideration adjourned.

[ Mr. Rifkind.]

Adjourned accordingly at twenty-three minutes to Eleven o'clock till Tuesday 13 January 1981 at half-past Ten o'clock.
