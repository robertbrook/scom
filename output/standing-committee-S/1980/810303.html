When local authorities are being encouraged—in fact, required—to cut their staffing, to become what is regarded as more efficient, which basically means that they do more work with fewer people, to add to those pressures this additional work at a time when most people in Scotland in the months of July and August are beginning to think about their holidays is an unfair burden to place on the local authorities. The Bill, if it is implemented, should be implemented in 1982-83. It will not escape the attention of hon. Members that in 1982 the local government elections at regional level will take place. One of the problems that I have encountered in discussing legislation of this nature has been the question of who has the mandate to determine policy. By that I mean that a Government are elected and have rights over the whole of the United Kingdom. I am not one of those people who maintain that because the Conservatives do not have a majority of seats in Scotland they do not have the right to govern. But what I do believe is that when they seek to impinge on the rights of local authorities they must seriously consider the feelings of the local authority electors. These feelings are best tested at local government elections.

One of the arguments that was advanced at great length before you, Mr. Gourlay, on the Tenants' Rights, Etc.
