There is another side to the issue. If we take together the amendments covering school and tertiary education, there arises straightaway the anomalous position of the Educational Institute of Scotland. That could be arrived at by some general undertaking and, as membership increased or declined, the necessary adjustments could be made. We want the Minister to assure us that it would be bona fide trade unions which are affiliated to the Scottish Trades Union Congress. We do not have any sectarian desire to squeeze out organisations, but we want to make it clear that the best way of policing a body such as this is to make the organisations involved responsible. We consider that the self-discipline of the membership of the Scottish Trades Union Congress would be the best way of achieving that aim.

We are not making a party point. We are simply making sure that these organisations are prepared to abide by the decisions of the Scottish Trades Union Congress. Therefore, we ask the Minister to accept the amendments in the name of my hon Friend the Member for Glasgow, Cathcart. If he is not yet prepared to do so, I imagine that my hon Friend would be willing to consider withdrawing them, but that, of course, depends on what the Minister says. However, we have no desire to be obstructive at present.

Two different sets of circumstances have to be taken into account. It may be appropriate for the Minister to stand back, re-examine the matter and return to it on Report if he cannot now give us answers that would allay the fears of the bodies that have made representations to us.
