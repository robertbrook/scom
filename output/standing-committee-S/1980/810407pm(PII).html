These are disarmingly headed "Miscellaneous". Whenever I see that word printed, especially on accounts, I begin to worry because, as sure as fate, something is hidden under it. We are asked to put these two sections after section 30 in the principal Act, under the heading

"Duty of parents to provide education for their children"

which seems a strange heading.

I have no great quarrel with the purpose of new Section 30A. I take issue with some of the things said by the Minister, who was quoted extensively in the press on the Bill. He often implied that the local authorities are especially bad at making consultative arrangements and that this type of clause is necessary so that they will engage in consultation. However, the record is interesting and salutary. Most local authorities, especially those that are Labour-controlled, when they engage in consultation pay attention to the representations that are made to them.

Let me take a local authority at random, the Lothian regional council. I choose that council because it is known to the Minister. Occasionally he praises it; more often he criticises it. Two examples spring to my mind. One is the proposed changes in catchment areas, which was the subject of extensive consultation in the Lothian region two or three years ago. It was proposed that there should not be a separate school at Balerno, but after consultations and representations the decision was changed. I do not oppose the proposed new section 30A, but it is ironic that it comes from a Government who pay no attention to representations made to them.

However, I do not know whether everybody has realised the implications of the proposed new section 30B, although from the way that the hon Member for Perth and East Perthshire (Mr. Walker) has reacted he seems to have done so. It states that:

"An education authority shall submit to the Secretary of State for his consent any proposal of theirs (of a prescribed kind) and shall not implement such a proposal without his consent."

The proposal continues:

"In this section 'prescribed' means prescribed for the purposes of this section by the Secretary of State by regulations for the time being in force and the Secretary of State may prescribe different kinds of proposals in relation to different education authorities."

My interpretation of new section 30B is that a Secretary of State may prescribe for authorities those matters which have to be approved by him. The Minister told us earlier that this is a great libertarian Bill which gives powers to local authorities. Understandably, he cited certain schemes for the provision of education as examples. But if the Secretary of State sees a local authority introducing a scheme for the development of community schools, for instance, he can say that such a change will be prescribed. So he will have a veto. Therefore, if any local authority—theoretically autonomous—wants to do something which the Secretary of State does not like, he will be able to prohibit the proposal.

There has been previous mention of draconian powers, and that seems one of the most draconian of all. It has been introduced under the heading "Miscellaneous" in the Bill and

"Duty of parents to provide education for their children'"
