In that statute, " open country " is defined as any land that consists

wholly or predominantly of mountain, moor, heath, hill, woodland, cliff or foreshore, with the waterways therein or contiguous or adjacent thereto (including any bank, barrier, dune, beach, flat or other land adjacent to the waterway or foreshore).

In spite of that extensive definition, the present wording appears to require an access agreement to relate not only to waterways but also to the adjacent or contiguous land, and difficulty has arisen in trying to achieve access over water alone. For example, where public access is already available over the adjacent land, riparian owners have been found to be reluctant to enter into what they see as another unnecessary access agreement for the same land, with the end result that access over the water cannot be obtained.

The clause removes that difficulty by redefining " open country " so as to allow water, including the foreshore around it, to be dealt with independently of any adjacent or contiguous land.
