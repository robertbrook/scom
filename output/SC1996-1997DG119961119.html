Draft Dental Auxiliaries (Amendment) Regulations 1996

10.30 am

The Minister for Health (Mr. Gerald Malone)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the draft Dental Auxiliaries (Amendment) Regulations 1996.

The regulations amend section 45(2)(c) of the Dentists Act 1984 to raise fees for first enrolment from £7 to £10, for the retention of the roll from £10 to £20, and for the restoration of a name to the roll from £2 to £5.

I shall not detain the Committee, save to explain that the amendment has been requested by the General Dental Council, which is required by section 45(2)(c) to maintain rolls of registered dental therapists and hygienists—collectively known as dental auxiliaries.

4 The previous increase in connection with auxiliaries' enrolment was made five years ago. Retention fees for dentists have been increased during the past five years. The change in the regulations is very minor and the General Dental Council's request to increase the fees for auxiliaries is entirely appropriate.

10.31 am

Ms Tessa Jowell
(Dulwich)
: As the Minister said, registration fees have not risen for several years. Although few hygienists and therapists will be itching to reach further into their pockets, Labour Members agree that the increases suggested by the General Dental Council are fair and long overdue.

We all benefit from the high professional standards of those who work as dental auxiliaries. An effective registration scheme is fundamental to maintaining those standards. There will be other opportunities to debate the crisis concerning access to national health dentistry.

Question put and agreed to.

Resolved,

That the Committee has considered the draft Dental Auxiliaries (Amendment) Regulations 1996.
