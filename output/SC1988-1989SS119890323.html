Dumfries and Galloway regional council has put forward a strong case that 25 per cent, of the board of management should be elected from the local community, and not in the capacity of parent, member of staff or appointed member. The Bill includes the provision for a parish priest to be appointed to the board. It is sensible for such a person to be appointed to the board of a denominational school. However, many schools in Scotland are non-denominational, and it would be wise to appoint a priest and a local minister in such schools because they are vital members of our community, especially of the rural community where there is a close liaison between Church and school.

In Grampian region there is a strong trend towards community education that covers a range of age groups from pre-school playgroups to senior citizens. We should ensure that community education groups are represented on boards of management because schools are often centres for community activities. Their involvement would ensure that the board of management took account of the wider needs of the community.

Within the Grampian regional council area many adults attend secondary schools to take certificates in various subjects. That is an effective part of education in several schools in my constituency, in particular, Speyside high school. Surely, there must be a way to ensure that that group, seeking opportunities in education, is represented on the board.

Major employers in the communities should also be involved on the board of management, not to represent particular sectoral interests, but because in rural communities one major employer may often provide employment opportunities for school leavers. Those employers should therefore have the opportunity to be involved in the school community and to put forward their views.

Amendment proposed, No. 60, in page 3, leave out lines 16 to 19 and insert—

  1. '(1) Subject to subsection (1A) below, a parent member elected under paragraph (a) of subsection (1) of section 3 of this Act, or a staff member elected under paragraph (b) of the said subsection (1), shall hold office for a term of four years.

  3. (1B) The parent members whose term of office is to be restricted to two years under subsection (1A) above, failing agreement between all the parent members elected to the Board, shall be selected by drawing lots.
  4. (1C) A staff member elected under paragraph (a) of subsection (1) of section 3 of this Act shall hold office for a term of four years.'.— [Mr. McLeish.]

Mr. Forsyth
: Amendment No. 60 would stagger the terms of office of elected members on a board of management of a self-governing school on a basis similar to that achieved for the elected members of school boards under the 1988 Act. The amendment is unnecessary, given that its purposes are achieved under schedule 4 and clause 4. Under paragraph 2 of schedule 4, elected members of the board of management who have transferred on the incorporation date from the school board continue in office for such periods as they would have done had they remained members of the school board. If a vacancy occurs in the elected membership of the school board before the incorporation date, but no by-election has been held before that date under the 1988 Act, under paragraph 4(b) of schedule 4 the vacancy becomes one of the board of management.

Under clause 4(3), the filling of a post on the

"death, resignation or removal of a member"

of a board of management has the effect that the person serves in office for only the remainder of the term of office of the person whose place is filled. In the case of the board of management on transition, that would be the previous holder of the office that has become vacant. In terms of paragraph 4(b) of schedule 4, the vacancy would be on the board of management, but would be derived from a vacancy on the preceding school board.

The effect of the provisions is to preserve the staggering of the terms of office of elected members of a board of management, as would be the case with a school board. That means that the amendment is superfluous.
