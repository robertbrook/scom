I regard this Amendment as one of considerable importance because we believe it right that people should know 106 when a local authority intend to designate a housing treatment area. The effect of the Amendment is to put a duty on the local authority to publish information about treatment areas.

The arguments here are similar to those which we had on an earlier Town and Country Planning Bill, concerning the principles in relation to publicity. We believe that publicity is just as important here and that where a housing treatment area has been declared, there should be as much public information about it as possible so that all those who are concerned know what their local authority are doing.

We have to remember that the designation of a housing treatment area affects not only houses in that area for which demolition or improvement to a tolerable standard is recommended but also good property which happens to be within that particular area. The point which I should like the Minister of State to bear in mind is that we are concerned not only with houses which will be affected by way of demolition or improvement but also with good property within that area. The fact of it falling within a treatment area could have an influence on the value of that good property. It will concern not only those people whose property is directly affected in physical terms but also the owners of other property within the area which is good and, indeed, above the standard, for that property could in a certain respect almost become blighted as a result of what is taking place in the rest of the area. It is only right that people should know about this, and a duty should be laid on the local authority to publish information so that all concerned may know exactly what it is that the local authority are doing.

I know that the Minister of State is always concerned about the drafting of Amendments, particularly where a completely new subsection is proposed, but I hope that in this case there will be no complaints about the drafting. We have drafted the Amendment in the best way we can, and we have had the best advice that is available to us. Therefore, whatever other defect there may be in this Amendment, I hope that the Minister of State will not fault it on drafting lines.

In the English Bill it was thought fit to JPEG 107 include a completely separate Clause which puts this duty on the local authority which I am asking to include in the Scottish Bill. I was asked the difference between owner-occupiers and those mentioned in paragraph ( a). There is, in fact, no difference.
