Clause 118 amends the rules in section 59B of the Taxes Management Act 1970, which enable tax shown as repayable on a return and self-assessment to be repaid to the taxpayer. The clause inserts a new subsection (4A) into section 59B of that Act, which will override the repayment provisions where an officer of the board—for example, an inspector—has commenced inquiries under section 9A, which concerns the power to inquire into returns. In such cases, the repayment will be delayed until the end of those inquiries and the officer is empowered to make provisional repayment.

"to such extent as he thinks fit."

That is the key issue. The wording seems to convey enormous discretion to an officer of the board. Indeed, the test seems to be almost subjective, which leads me to my first question to the Financial Secretary. Does not the wording of the clause make the test subjective? It gives the Inland Revenue a wide discretion and, therefore, a wide power.

The Inland Revenue will want discretion and power: the state and its instruments always do. It is, however, our job as Members of Parliament to control any overweening power of the state and its bureaucracy and to ensure that the Administration take account of individual citizens.

The Inland Revenue already has wide powers, and we are in danger of giving it more. The Labour party seeks to fetter and direct that power so that it is used on a just and equitable basis. That is an entirely reasonable way of approaching the matter. The Conservative party says that it is opposed to the excessive power of the state. Well, here is an opportunity to prove it by providing a clause that restricts the Inland Revenue's discretion.

The Chartered Institute of Taxation is clear. In its representations to us on clause 118, it says:

"The proposed TMA 1970 Section 4A as currently drafted is inequitable in that the officer of the Board takes the decision 'as he thinks fit' with no corresponding right of appeal being given to the taxpayer.


The taxpayer could be due a large refund, but there may only be a small amount of tax at stake during the enquiry. In this situation giving the Inspector the power to cancel the repayment until enquiries were completed is inequitable and cannot he the intention of the provision.


We suggest that the wording 'to such extent as he thinks fit' be amended as it is too wide and gives the Inspector too much discretion. As an alternative. an appeal mechanism against the Inspector's decision is required.
