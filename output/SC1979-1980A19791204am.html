EXCEPTIONS FROM S. (PROHIBITION OF LOANS ETC. TO DIRECTORS AND CONNECTED PERSONS)

"(1) Where a director of a relevant company or of its holding company is associated with a subsidiary of either of those companies, sub-paragraphs (ii) and (iii) of subsections (1)( b) of section (Prohibition of loans etc. to directors and connected persons) above shall not by reason only of that fact prohibit the relevant company from—

  1. making a loan or quasi-loan to that subsidiary; or
  2. entering into a guarantee or providing any security in connection with a loan or quasi-loan made by any person to that subsidiary.

(2) Subsection (2) of that section shall not prohibit a company's entering into—

  1. any transaction for any person if the aggregate of the relevant amounts does not exceed £5,000; or
  2. any transaction for any person if—
    1. the company enters into the transaction in the ordinary course of its business; and
    2. the value of the transaction is not greater, and the terms on which it is entered into, are no more favourable to the person for whom the transaction is entered into than it is reasonable to expect the company to have offered for a person of the same financial standing as that person (any connection between the company and that person being disregarded).

(3) Subject to the following provisions of this section, each of the following is excepted from the prohibitions in section (Prohibition of loans etc. to directors and connected persons) above, that is to say—

  1. a loan or quasi-loan by a company to its holding company or a company's entering into a guarantee or providing any security in connection with a loan or quasi-loan made by any person to its holding company;
  2. a company's entering into a credit transaction as creditor for its holding company or entering into a guarantee or providing any security in connection with any credit transaction made by any other person for its holding company;
  3. a company's doing anything to provide any of its directors with funds to meet expenditure incurred or to be incurred by him for the purposes of the company or for the purpose of enabling him properly to perform his duties as an officer of the company or a company's doing anything to enable any of its directors to avoid incurring such expenditure;
