OPERATION OF NOTICE TO QUIT GIVEN BY REASON OF DEATH OF TENANT

    : I beg to move, That the clause be read a Second time.

    The main purpose of the clause is to set out those persons to whom the family succession scheme shall apply and those who are excluded from it.

    Subsection (1) causes the succession provisions of Part II to apply on each occasion when a tenant or assignee dies and is survived by any of the small range of closerelatives set out in the subsection. These are the widow or widower of the deceased, brother or sister, child, which includes adopted child, and any " treated child "—a phrase imported from the Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Act 1975. A treated child can include a stepchild, foster-child or a childtaken in without formal adoption, provided that the child was treated by the deceased for any length of time as a child of the family in relation to any marriage towhich he was a party.

    Subsection (2) requires the Lord Chancellor to set out in an order the procedure tobe followed by a person applying to the Agricultural Land Tribunal for the tenancy of the holding, in particular whom it is that he has to notify of his intentionto apply.

    Subsection (3) is particularly important because it defines eligible person". An eligible person has first of all to be a close relative of the deceased as defined insubsection (1). Secondly, he has to have derived his only or principal livelihood from the holding for at least five years out of the seven years immediately preceding death, thereby establishing a close relationship with that particular land.Thirdly, he may not be someone who is an occupier of a commercial farm elsewhere.

    Subsection (4) allows up to three years' training in agriculture at a college or similar educational establishment to count towards the five years' qualifying time actually spent on the holding. A precedent for this is to be found in the Smallholdings (Selection of Tenants) Regulations 1970.

    Subsection (5) sets out the exclusions from the family succession scheme. The mainones are, first, where the deceased is 808 survived by a joint tenant with him on the holding which I have already mentioned under new Clause 7. Hon. Members will, I am sure, recall that it is part of the general law of this country that on the death of a joint tenant the tenancy continues in the name of the sole survivor or surviving joint tenants.

    Secondly, the succession provisions are excluded where there is already a valid notice to quit on the holding at the date of the tenant's death which, in one way oranother, has become final. A notice to quit for this purpose is basically one thatis incontestable—based on bankruptcy, for example—or has either beenupheld by the Agricultural Land Tribunal or is one to which the deceased tenant had not objected within the statutory time limit for making objections.
