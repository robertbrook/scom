STANDING COMMITTEE B
Tuesday, 28th October, 1919

SIR SAMUEL ROBERTS in the Chair.

ELECTRICITY (SUPPLY) BILL.

[OFFICIAL REPORT.]
LIEUT.-COMMANDER NORMAN CRAIG'S DEATH.

The CHAIRMAN
: Perhaps before we begin the business I might be allowed to express in the name of the Committee the deep regret we all feel at the death of one of the Members of this Committee (Lieut.-Commander Norman Craig) who had taken a most active part in the proceedings of this Committee, and to express our deep sympathy with his relatives.

The Committee signified assent.

SITTINGS OF THE COMMITTEE.

The CHAIRMAN
: I also feel that I must consult the Committee about the meetings. I have had an intimation from the Government that they wish us to proceed as quickly as possible in order to get this Bill through the Committee stage. I shall do my best, but I feel that the matter is in the hands of the Committee themselves. As far as I can see, the remainder of the Bill is more or less machinery, and the Clauses we have already taken up to Clause 7 are the contentious and critical parts of the Bill. I hope that we shall be able to make quicker progress with the remaining parts of the Bill than we did with the first seven Clauses. I am reluctant to ask the Committee to sit in the afternoon, as I know the inconvenience of that course both to Ministers and to individual Members. What I propose to do this week is to only ask the Committee, to sit in the mornings; then we will see what progress we make, and if after that I form the opinion that the progress is not sufficiently quick, I am afraid I shall have to ask the Committee next week to sit in the afternoon. I should deprecate that for many reasons, but perhaps some Member of the Committee may be able to assist me by suggestions in regard to this matter.
