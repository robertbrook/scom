MINISTRY OF WAYS AND COMMUNICATIONS BILL.

THIRTEENTH DAY'S PROCEEDINGS.
NEW CLAUSE.
—(Advisory Committees.)

For the purpose of giving him advice and assistance in connection with the exercise and performance of his powers and duties, the Minister shall set up a panel of experts, appointed from nominees, chosen in such manner as he may prescribe, of the various classes of undertakings affected by this Act, and of labour, the trading interest, local authorities and such ether interests as he may deem desirable.

Before exercising any of the powers under Section 3, Sub-section (1) ( b), or directing the establishment of new transport services by land or water, the Minister shall, provided he considers the question involved to be one of sufficient importance to justify such a reference, refer the matter to a Committee selected by him from the said panel.—[ Mr. Shortt.]

I submit it is quite impossible, where these great interests are concerned, that we should leave to the Minister the decision whether a matter shall be referred to the Committee or not. There, I suggest, lies the whole difference between the Minister and myself. And then I propose these words

"The advisory panel or any committee to whom any matter is referred under this Section shall before reporting or advising if they see fit, give public notice and permit any person affected or likely to be affected to place their views before them either orally or in writing."

It will be seen that we have whittled down considerably the powers I wanted to give to the panel in my former Amendments. We have in fact gone a long way to meet the Minister. We have done away with legal machinery; we have made it much simpler. I propose now to leave it to the Minister to say how far he proposes to meet me, and then, if necessary, I will supply further arguments.
