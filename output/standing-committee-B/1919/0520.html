Sir E. GEDDES
: I beg to move

"That the Clause be read a second time."

This Clause provides in the first place that officers and servants may be transferred either permanently or temporarily from undertakings which have been taken possession of by the Ministry under the powers of the Bill, and may be retained on the superannuation or similar funds of the undertakings during the period of control. The reason of that will be obvious to the Committee. The intention is that during the two years while the Ministry is being set up, and in anticipation of a possible closer relationship between the State and such undertakings as we are considering, it shall be possible for the officer to retain his pensions rights without JPEG 1023 the State having to incur the very considerable liability of practically buying him out. The consent for permanent transfer rests with the officer or servant himself, that being simply a freedom of choice as to his employer. For a temporary transfer the consent of both officer or servant and employer is necessary. It is provided that that consent shall not be unreasonably withheld. The reason for that is really "this: Later in the Clause compensation is given to an officer or servant if he is displaced, or if he is offered work not reasonably analogous to the work which he is already performing. It might be possible for the servant or officer to decline to accept some other reasonable work given to him and then claim compensation; it is only for that reason the wording is put in. The remainder of the Clause safeguards officers and servants from any depreciation of their position owing to the acts of a Minister—and I hope and believe successfully does that! It provides in the two cases, the transfer and the case of the servant who is not transferred, but remains in the service of the undertaking for certain arbitrations. That is really the main purport of the Clause.
