Finance Bill

New clause 10
TAX RELIEF FOR DONATIONS TO POLITICAL PARTIES

".— (1) The following shall be inserted after section 379 of the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1988 (interpretation of sections 369 to 378)—

'Relief for donations to political parties

379AA.— (1) Tax relief shall be available to an individual (the donor) in accordance with this section on qualifying political donations made by him of up to £500 in any year of assessment.

  1. A donation is a qualifying political donation for the purposes of this section if it is made to an eligible political party and—
    1. it takes the form of the payment of a sum of money,
    2. it is not subject to a condition as to repayment,
    3. it is not conditional on or associated with, or part of an arrangement involving, the acquisition of property by the political party, otherwise than by way of gift, from the donor or a person connected with him, and
    4. the donor is resident in the United Kingdom at the time the donation is made.
  2. For the purposes of this section a political party is an eligible political party if, at the last general election preceding the donation in question—
    1. two members of that party were elected to the House of Commons, or
    2. one member of that party was elected to the House of Commons and not less than 150,000 votes were given to candidates who were members of that party.
  3. If an individual makes a qualifying donation he shall be entitled, on making the payment, to deduct and retain out of it a sum equal to basic rate tax thereon.
  4. Where a sum is deducted under subsection (4) above the sum deducted shall be treated as income tax paid by the person to whom the payment is made.
  5. Any person by whom a qualifying donation is received shall be entitled to recover from the Board, in accordance with regulations, an amount which by virtue of subsection (5) above is treated as income tax paid by him; and any amount so recovered shall be treated for the purposes of the Tax Acts in like manner as the qualifying political donation to which it relates.
  6. The following provisions of the Management Act, namely—
    1. section 29 (1) (c) (excessive relief) as it has effect apart from section 29 (2) to (10) of that Act,
    2. section 30 (tax repaid in error, etc) apart from subsection (1B),
    3. section 86 (interest), and
    4. section 95 (incorrect return or accounts),

      768 shall apply in relation to an amount which is paid to any person by the Board as an amount recoverable in accordance with regulations made by virtue of subsection (6) above but to which that person is not entitled as if it were income tax which ought not to have been repaid and, where that amount was claimed by that person, as if it had been repaid as respects a chargeable period as a relief which was not due.
  7. In the application of section 86 of the Management Act by virtue of section (7) above in relation to sums due and payable by virtue of an assessment made for the whole or part of a year of assessment ('the relevant year of assessment') under section 29 (1) (c) or 30 of that Act, as applied by that subsection, the relevant date—
    1. is 1st January in the relevant year of assessment in a case where the person falling within subsection (5) above has made a relevant interim claim; and
    2. in any other case is the later of the following dates, that is to say—
      1. 1st January in the relevant year of assessment; or
      2. the date of the making of the payment by the Board which gives rise to the assessment.
  8. The Board may by regulations make provision—
    1. for the purposes of any provision of this section which relates to any matter or thing to be specified by or done in accordance with regulations;
    2. with respect to the furnishing of information by donors or recipients, including, in the case of recipients, the inspection of books, documents and other records on behalf of the Board; and
    3. generally for giving effects to this section.
  9. In this section—
  10. 'financial year' in relation to any person, means a financial year of that person for the purposes of the relevant regulations;
  11. 'interim claim' means an interim claim within the meaning of the relevant regulations;
  12. 'relevant interim claim' means, in relation to an assessment made for a period coterminous with, or falling wholly within, a person's financial year, an interim claim made for a period falling wholly or partly within that financial year; and
  13. 'the relevant regulations' means regulations made under subsection (9) above.
  14. Section 839 of this Act shall apply for the purposes of this section to determine whether one person is connected with another.'
  15. (2) The provisions of this section shall be brought into effect from 1st April in the year in which the Home Secretary certifies that the recommendations in the Fifth Report of the Committee on Standards in Public Life has been substantially brought into effect.".—[Mr. Gibb.]

Mr. Nick Gibb
(Bognor Regis and Littlehampton)
: I beg to move, That the clause be read a Second time.

I apologise to hon. Members for bringing them back into the Committee Room to debate the new clause. It is important, however, as it goes to the heart of our democratic process, and of our two-party system, as I see that it is this afternoon. The new clause JPEG 769 would introduce tax relief at the basic rate of tax on political donations of up to £500 a year. It would also ensure that the relief would be introduced only when the rest of the recommendations in the Neill committee report on the funding of political parties in the United Kingdom have been implemented. That is why new subsection (2) states:

"The provisions of this section shall be brought into effect from 1st April in the year in which the Home Secretary certifies that the recommendations in the Fifth Report of the Committee on Standards of Public Life has been substantially brought into effect."

The wording is based on MIRAS and the gift aid scheme, so it has some precedent in the Taxes Act. The reason for the caveat at the end of the new clause, which, as I said, states that it will come into effect when the recommendations in the Neill report have been substantially implemented, is that the Opposition do not believe in cherry picking those recommendations. Our view is that they should be implemented in full.

Everybody contributes in different ways. Some wealthy people need to contribute in the manner to which the hon. Gentleman referred.

Mr. Geraint Davie
(Croydon, Central)

Mr. Gibb
: The hon. Gentleman has reached the crux of the debate, to which the Committee will come in a moment. This country faces a choice between whether we want political parties to be directly funded by taxpayers, or whether we want to implement procedure that will encourage more voluntary donations from members of the public. The new clause goes a long way towards encouraging more voluntary donations from members of the public.

In the evidence that it submitted to the Neill Committee, the Conservative party stated:

"Rather than call for state funding we would welcome the Committee's views about how more individuals could be encouraged to donate. We note that in some countries individuals are offered tax relief for political donations, elsewhere tax credits or matching grants are provided. We believe that the Committee should give serious consideration to the practicalities of these approaches in the context of the UK's voluntary system".

770 That was our submission to the Neill Committee, and its recommendation is set out in our new clauses. It is important in the interests of democracy that the funding of political parties is as broadly based as possible.

PART II
STAMP DUTY RESERVE TAX ON DEALINGS WITH UNITS IN UNIT TRUSTS

Charge to tax

2.— (1) There is a charge to stamp duty reserve tax where—

  1. a person authorises or requires the trustees or managers under a unit trust scheme to treat him as no longer interested in a unit under the scheme, or
  2. a unit under a unit trust scheme is transferred to the managers of the scheme, and the unit is a chargeable security.

    Those events are referred to in this Part of this Schedule as a "surrender" of the unit to the managers.
  1. The tax is chargeable—
    1. whether the surrender is made or effected in the United Kingdom or elsewhere, and
    2. whether or not any party is resident or situate in any part of the United Kingdom.
  2. The persons liable for the tax are the trustees of the unit trust.
  3. This paragraph is subject to the exclusions provided for in paragraphs 6 and 7.

Rate of tax

3.— (1) Tax under this Part of this Schedule is chargeable at the rate of 0.5 per cent, of the market value of the unit.

  • This is subject to any reduction under paragraph 4 or 5.
  • (2) The market value of a unit means whichever is higher of—
    1. (a) the price the unit might reasonably be expected to fetch on a sale in the open market at the time of surrender, and
      2. 780
    2. its cancellation price, or if it is redeemed its redemption price, at that time, calculated in accordance with the trust instrument.

    Proportionate reduction of tax by reference to units issued

    4.— (1) The amount of tax chargeable shall be proportionately reduced if the number of units of the same class as the unit in question that are surrendered to the managers in the relevant two-week period exceeds the number of units of that class issued by the managers in that period.

    1. The "relevant two-week period" in relation to a surrender is the period from the beginning of the of the week in which the surrender occurs to the end of the following week.

      For this purpose a week means a period of seven days beginning with a Sunday.
    2. The reduction is made by applying the following fraction to the amount otherwise chargeable—

      I/S
    3. Where:
    4. I is the number of units of the class issued by the managers in the relevant two-week period, and
    5. S is the number of units of the class surrendered to the managers in that period.
    6. If a consolidation or sub-division of units affects the comparison of the number of units surrendered and the number of units issued, the numbers shall be determined as if the consolidation or sub-division had not taken place.
    7. "Consolidation or sub-division" includes any alteration of the number of units of the class in question otherwise than in consequence of an increase or reduction in the trust property.
    8. This paragraph does not apply if on the surrender of the unit the unit holder receives anything other than money; and for the purposes of this paragraph no account shall be taken of a surrender or issue that is not entirely for money.

    Proportionate reduction of tax by reference to assets held

    5.— (1) The amount of tax chargeable after any reduction under paragraph 4 shall be further reduced if in the relevant two-week period the trust property is invested in both exempt and non-exempt investments.

    1. The reduction is made by applying the following fraction to that amount—

      N/N+E
    2. Where:
    3. N is the average market value of the non-exempt investments over the relevant two-week period, and
    4. E is the average market value of the exempt investments over that period.
    5. In this paragraph "exempt investment" has the same meaning as in section 99(5A)(b) of the Finance Act 1986; and "non-exempt investment" means any investment that is not an exempt investment.

    Exclusion of charge in certain cases of change of ownership

    6.— (1) This paragraph applies where in pursuance of arrangements between the person entitled to a unit and another person ("the new owner")—

    PART IV
    GENERAL DEFINITIONS

    Meaning of "unit trust scheme" and related expressions

    14.— (1) The following definitions apply for the purposes of the enactments relating to stamp duty and the enactments relating to stamp duty reserve tax.

    1. "Unit trust scheme" has the same meaning as in the Financial Services Act 1986, subject to paragraphs 15 to 18.
    2. In relation to a unit trust scheme—
    3. "trust instrument" means the trust deed or other instrument (whether under seal or not) creating or recording the trusts on which the property in question is held;
    4. "trust property" means the property subject to the trusts of the trust instrument;
    5. "unit" means a right or interest (whether described as a unit, as a sub-unit or otherwise) of a beneficiary under the trust instrument;
    6. "unit holder" means a person entitled to a share of the trust property; and
