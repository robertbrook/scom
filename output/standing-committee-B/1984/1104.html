Therefore, the structure of the industry led the distributors and exhibitors to go always for the safe film. The safe film will almost always be non-British and almost always an American film. Quite apart from the resources which are, or are not, made available to the British film industry, the whole structure of the industry militates against the survival of an indiginous industry.

Two thirds of the market is controlled by two major distributors and two major distributors dominate the exhibition of films in this country. The JPEG 45 whole cosy relationship is tied up through alignments of various sorts and then bolstered by restrictive practises. We see the malign consequences of that duopoly in the sad story of cinema attendances. It is a universal phenomenon that cinema attendances have fallen over the past two decades. But our experience has been particularly bad and one reason for that is that as the demand for a product falls, if the suppliers enjoy anything approaching a monopoly, their natural reaction is to put up prices, which further chokes off demand. That has happened in this country.
