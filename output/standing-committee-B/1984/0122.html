The purpose of the Bill is to give wider statutory authority for the payments, because we are paying out £50 million over five years. I can give the Committee the figures: 1,200 farmers have already accepted our offer to take the outgoers scheme, while 879 have already been paid. If no dairy farmer, from Wales or anywhere else, producing under 200,000 litres had wanted to take advantage of the outgoers scheme, we would naturally have gone on to the larger producers.

It was thought that small farmers would be hardest hit and an arbitrary figure of a family farm with 40 cows was chosen.

Some of the small farmers have realised that their way out may be to accept the outgoers scheme. But many other farmers on marginal land with perhaps more than 40 cows are finding themselves in great difficulty. Even though the National Farmers Union welcomed the Bill and the £50 million, it pressed for necessary funding to allow the extension of the scheme.

There were two estimates this morning. One was for an increase of 50 per cent. to give £25 million more. Four times the original allocation has also been suggested if we are to do the farming community justice. The figure of £200 million may be arbitrary, but is not the Government's £50 million figure equally arbitrary?

Mr. MacGregor
: First, we had to strike a balance between what it was fair to ask other taxpayers to pay and what we could do to help the dairy industry. We had to bear in mind that substantial sums are being paid by the taxpayer in other directions to support the dairy industry.

Secondly, one always has to draw the line somewhere and, given that most people agree that it is the small producer who wants to stay in business and who has no other viable farming opportunities who is most hard hit, we thought it right to draw 40 the line at that small producer. The £50 million was calculated to enable him to get back to his 1983 production levels and also to deal with the exceptional hardship cases which, following the debate in the House on the draft regulations, we included in the final regulations. I hope that we shall have achieved that with the £50 million.
