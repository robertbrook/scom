Mr. Hayhoe
: As the hon. Gentleman has suggested, this is a relatively narrow point. It is when Customs and Excise discovers amounts of assessable tax which cannot be directly related to individual accounting periods and, bearing in mind that sometimes that discovery goes back many years, it is impossible to allocate it precisely to the quarter concerned. Customs and Excise apparently, assesses such amounts using bulk assessment covering more than one period. However, with the advent of period specific interest, surcharge and penalties, that is no longer possible, because it could lead to considerable unfairness. Clause 20(3) authorises Customs and Excise to attribute such amounts of tax to individual prescribed accounting periods, and in doing so it must exercise its best judgment.

Under existing legislation—section 40(1)(m) of the Value Added Tax Act 1983—people may appeal against JPEG 183 the amount of any tax assessment. Clause 24(1) of this Bill extends that right of appeal to

"the amount of any penalty, interest or surcharge specified in an assessment under section 20".

So there is an increase in traders' rights.

It follows, therefore, that when an amount of penalty, interest or surcharge is tax geared, and is dependent upon the amount of tax, the amount of tax, including any attributions to periods by Customs and Excise, can be taken to the VAT Tribunal on appeal. In those circumstances, Customs and Excise will have to demonstrate that its attribution was made to the best of its judgment. So the onus of proof is on Customs and Excise to show that best judgment has been used, for otherwise the appeal will succeed. The safeguard which the amendment seeks to introduce is already provided in clause 24, coupled with the existing provisions of the Value Added Tax Act 1983. I hope that the hon. Gentleman will therefore think it right not to press his amendment.

Clause 25(4), like the other parts of the clause, follows the Keith committee recommendation that VAT legislation should mirror closely section 54 of the Taxes Management Act 1970.
