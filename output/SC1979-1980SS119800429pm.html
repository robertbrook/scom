Mr. Norman Buchan

We are concerned with the question whether the person should be given a caution of some kind, and with this amendment, that the person is under no obligation to answer any question or give any statement other than to give his name and address. That is crucial in the whole process of detention, arrest, and, as we shall see, in judicial examination. This is the beginning of the chain which, up to now, has been fairly tight, binding, and with a good many safeguards. We are now beginning a chain in which some of those safeguards are being loosened. First, we have detention, meaning detention without the legal redress of other safeguards of arrest.
