The Secretary of State for Wales (Mr. David Hunt)

that the chairman do now report to the House that the Committee has considered the matter of education and training in Wales.

I am delighted that we are debating the important subject of education and training. I am personally grateful to you, Mr. Coleman, because I know that you made considerable changes in your itenary and engagements in order to chair our proceedings today.

I am glad to have the opportunity to debate a subject that we last debated some two-and-a-half years ago, as since then, we have made some dramatic strides. We are well on the way to having an education and training system that is responsive to the needs of the labour market, that is driven by consumer demands and that is accessible throughout life, offering recognised qualifications and clear value for money.

Education and training are not just important today; they will be even more improtant in the world of tomorrow. As we debate these issues, I hope that we shall consider not just Wales or the United Kingdom, but the European Community. Many challenges and opportunities will arise with the introduction of the single European Market in 1992. There will increasingly be new job opportunities, but also greater competition, and it is essential that our young people should have the skills to enable them to compete successfully. We must look to the education service to provide them with those skills.

I am glad to be a part of a Government who have been prepared to take a radical look at education and training in this country and who have introduced the reforms needed to meet the challenges before us.

The education service exists not for the benefit of those who run it, but for the benefit of the consumers—children, parents and prospective employers. Parents must have the opportunities to become much more widely involved in schools than they have been in the past. I want more and more parents to become governors in schools and have a real say in what goes on. I want parents to have a wider choice of schools. I also want schools and colleges to have JPEG 7 much more control over their own destiny than they have had in the past, especially in the management of resources. I want schools and colleges of which everyone in Wales can be proud. I want and education service of which teachers and everyone else connected with it can be proud. The dedication, talents and energies of our teaching force are the very rock on which the education and training service is built, and we must ensure that we support our teachers to enable them to provide the education and training needed in the 1990's and beyond.
