The suggestion, therefore, that can be made—I am sure that my hon. and learned Friend will make it—is the argument as to whether this information can be put in a code of practice. If it were to be incorporated in a code of practice, that would not be the position. There might be a charity clause in the union membership agreement and—to come back to the point made by the hon. Member for Rotherham (Mr. Crowther)—the man might well say that he had a conscientious reason for not paying to a charity. If he had, would he, at the end of his time, find that the right of the employer—which is the right on the question of conscience—overrode the code of practice? If it did, the employer would find that his union membership agreement did not stand up in front of a tribunal.

For these reasons, therefore, and others to which I may wish to return, I ask my hon. and learned Friend to consider this matter very carefully.
