The hon. Gentleman has raised the matter with me outside the House because of his anxiety about the travel concession arrangements for employees of the National Freight Corporation who were employees of National Carriers Limited when National Carriers was transferred from British Rail to the NFC in 1969. Those former employees of National Carriers, together with their widows and dependants, have, since that time, continued to enjoy the usual travel concessions on British Rail and London Transport services which are available to all railway staff.

The present position is that there are substantial numbers of employees and their widows and dependants enjoying such benefits. Under the Transport Act 1978 the previous Government undertook to reimburse the corporation for the payments that the corporation was making to British Rail and to London Transport for these travel concessions.

The position of the employees is entirely clear. The new company will succeed to all the rights and obligations, particularly all the contractual rights and obligations, of the corporation. Contracts of employment will, therefore, remain intact. The present terms and conditions of employment will remain intact, and there is no question of the company, in the unlikely event of it wishing to do so, seeking to avoid its obligations where those terms and conditions include travel concessions.

The Government's powers under the Transport Act 1978 to make payments to compensate the corporation for travel concessions will continue. The aim of the amendment is achieved by para 7(1) of the schedule which expressly provides that:

"The obligations of the Corporation transferred by virtue of section 36 include any obligation to comply with terms attached to a payment made under section 21 of the Transport Act 1978."

That specifically refers to the travel concessions and it therefore underlines the fact that the successor company will remain liable for the same obligations as the former corporation. The Government's position will not be changed either. We have stood behind the concessions since 1978 and it is our intention and that of the corporation to continue to do so.

I appreciate the concern of the com- JPEG 1651 pany's present employees and those who represent them about the position of the widows and dependants who have acquired the rights. There is no need for that concern. All the corporation's legal obligations to maintain the concessions will be transferred to the successor company and the Government will stand behind it and reimburse it for the cost of those concessions.

I suggest that the amendment be withdrawn. It is not technically perfect, but I do not retreat behind technical points. It is unnecessary because the Bill adequately covers the position.
