However, we are worried about whether sufficient money will be raised to meet the first obligation to meet the pension commitment—that is, to finance the fund to an extent that will be determined by the Minister.

It may be assumed that, whatever happens to a public corporation, the Government will always maintain the security of its pension funds. Historically, there is plenty of evidence of that. The actuarial profession will decide—or judge—the increasing obligation to a pension fund over a period.

The Bill, like the Acts of 1974 and 1978, is intended to relieve the board and the corporation of the costs of discharging their obligations to their pension funds. The Committee will be well aware that the pensions provisions of the 1974 and 1978 Acts did not stand on their own but formed part of a comprehensive financial reconstruction of the board and of the corporation. Our general policy remains that nationalised industry pension schemes should be funded. Let me make that absolutely clear. It is right that the cost of current service pensions liabilities should be met by contributions levied at the time when those liabilities are incurred, so that the full cost of the labour employed should appear in the accounts at the time of the employment and it is fully reflected in the charges paid by customers. The Bill does not affect this. It preserves the principle of funding for the proportion of the historic pensions liabilities that can be met out of the assets of the funds.

We have made clear at all stages that it was an essential part of our approach that members and pensioners of the schemes concerned should not be worse off under the Bill than they would have been under the Railways Act 1974 and the Transport Act 1978. The purpose of the Bill, like that of the 1974 and 1978 Acts, is to assist the board and the corporation to discharge their obligations to their pension schemes. It does not affect the entitlement of members and pensioners under the rules of the schemes. Nor does it affect the contributions that they have to pay.
