: I hope that I can persuade the right hon. Gentleman that only amendment No. 279 would be acceptable. I have already declared the policy of forming a company especially for our purpose, whose only assets will be all the assets of the National Freight Corporation and whose only shareholder will be the Minister or nominees who will hold the Companies Act company shares.

Amendment No. 279 makes it clear that the company will be formed for that purpose in order to avoid the possible suggestion that it might be some other company to which the assets are transferred. My hon. Friend the Member for Bebington and Ellesmere Port (Mr. Porter) during the lunchtime adjournment pointed out to me that it is rather a belt and braces change. Subsection (5) makes it clear that whether this company is nominated or formed for the purpose, it must be 100 per cent. Government owned. There are not many existing companies which the Minister could nominate with 100 per cent. Government shareholdings.

In the spirit of good will and cooperation abounding on some issues I am able to give an absolute assurance that the Bill will bind the Government to the policy we are enunciating, and will therefore bind all our successors. We therefore accept amendment No. 279.

It will be necessary eventually to have consequential amendments to subsection (5) which uses the word "nominate" and I undertake that the Government will bring those forward. I trust that as the Government have accepted an Opposition amendment, consequential Government amendments will not cause unnecessary delay at Report stage.

I accept that the other amendments are aimed at material points, but amendment No. 280 describes the form of the company as:

"formed under the Companies Act 1948 and registered thereunder".

It undoubtedly will be, and it is unnecessary to spell that out. We are not thinking of going for any other sort of company. The company will have articles of association following Table A of the Companies Act. The share ownership is already covered by subsection (5)( b) and I think that we agreed not to accept amendments which write in unnecessary words. There is no loophole 1594 in the Bill for the Opposition to close with amendment No. 280.

Amendment No. 300 has been dealt with. Because the company has to be formed for the purpose, it cannot be a company existing prior to the transfer of the assets. As it is formed for the purpose, its sole assets will be the assets of the National Freight Corporation. It cannot be a subsidiary of any other company because of the requirement that all the issued shares of the company—the entire share capital—must be held by the Minister or his nominees. If it were a subsidiary company, it would be partly owned, at least, by some other company. But this will be a wholly-owned company and therefore, per se, it is not a subsidiary.

Finally, amendment No. 299 deals with the apparent discretion given to the Minister in subsection (5). The powers are indeed permissive and not mandatory, but that is normal in this sort of Bill. They also enable the Minister to proceed as described there, but not in any other way. Therefore, the only alternative to the policy I have described is for the Minister not to do anything at and not to proceed with our proposals.

Clearly that is not the hon. Gentleman's fear. There is no question of that provision being interpreted as meaning that the Minister may decide by order to transfer the company in some totally different way to some other company. I am assured that that would not bear that interpretation. The only options open to the Minister as the Bill stands would be to proceed to transfer to a wholly-owned Companies Act 1948 company, as described, or not to transfer the assets to any company at all but to allow it to continue as it is.

I believe that by accepting amendment No. 279 the wording confines the Minister and any successor Ministers precisely to the policy that has been described. Although I realise that that policy itself is not acceptable to the Opposition, nevertheless, I am sure that they prefer the Bill as it stands to one that might give a wider discretion.

Amendment agreed to.
