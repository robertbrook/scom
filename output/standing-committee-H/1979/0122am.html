The Minister of Transport

(Mr. Norman Fowler)
: I beg to move,

That during the further proceedings on the Transport Bill the Committee do meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays at half-past Ten o'clock and at half-past Four o'clock.

I am sure that the Committee will agree on the importance of a thorough debate on this important Bill which makes fundamental changes to our transport regulations and law.

There is much to discuss, and the Opposition have already shown a desire to explore the Bill in depth and express their views in full. But I should point out that in six sittings and 15 hours, not counting today, we have progressed half way down page 3. The Bill contains 77 pages and I, for one, do not intend to spend the next three-and-a-half years in this Committee. It would not be unduly contentious to say that so far we have made disappointing progress.

Mr. John Prescott:
: It is a disappointing Bill.

Mr. Fowler
: I am sure the Committee will wish to ensure that our deliberations do not unreasonably restrict consideration of the Bill in the House or in another place. There must be no pretext for saying that the Bill was not properly considered. I hope, therefore, that in the interests of the Committee the sittings motion will be agreed. That will allow good progress to be made and at the same time give all hon. Members the opportunity for thorough discussion.

The length of sittings must depend on how much time members of the Committee wish to devote to the debate, but I expect that the usual channels will be open, and we shall be pleased to consider an arrangement to suit the Opposition.

It is now necessary to make progress on this important Bill and, with the considerations I have outlined in mind, I commend the motion to the Committee.
