The qualification proposed by Amendment No. 32 and the singling out of redundancy payments as though they were not part of arrangements for the protection of staff by Amendment No. 33 seem to us to be quite unnecessary and they could be misleading.

I must emphasise that protection of existing staff extends, broadly speaking, to two types of situation. If that were to be the case we would not be rightly concerned with compensation provisions, including redundancy payments.

We want the parties to the transfer schemes to approach the problems which will arise with the utmost care for the interests of the individual, but we cannot expect responsible local authorities to try to achieve full and fair protection by a deliberate policy of overmanning or unnecessary upgrading.

: I beg to move, Amendment No. 83, in page 5, line 33, after "directions", insert:

"and if, after consultations with the county councils and district councils adjacent to the district council making the transfer scheme, he is satisfied that the scheme would be compatible with the proper development of the new town".

The object of this amendment is to ensure that the district council to which land, housing accommodation, other assets and rights are being transferred under the provisions of this. Bill will be in a position to carry out all such works as may be necessary and desirable for the proper development of those assets and of the town as a whole.

I must confess that I have encountered some difficulty in drafting an amendment which would facilitate this, because it is not possible to transfer from the development corporation or the commission to district councils powers which those development corporations and the commission do not already possess. However, I must point out in this connection that JPEG 189 there is a division of responsibility between district and county authorities and as a result in some cases in the past, there have been difficulties or delays, which have arisen as a result of the problems of obtaining the approval of county authorities for works essential for developments to go ahead. I am not particularly complaining about the relationship of Harlow with Essex County Council, I must say in case there should be any misunderstanding on this, but I am referring to the situation generally up and down the country.

The kind of development I am thinking of is one, for example, in which a road requires county council approval before a local development can be proceeded with satisfactorily. A development corporation or the commission, in so far as they are direct Government agencies, have somewhat more weight and better connections for securing the objective of persuading a county council to approve a development than has a district council. It is essential, after transfer takes place, that a district council in a new town should not by a disagreement with a county authority over such essential works, be delayed unreasonably, or even be prevented, from proceeding with essential development. Similar problems can arise between new town authorities and neighbouring district councils. It is possible that works may overlap or even be situated within a neighbouring authority's district.

It is important to ensure that the district authority responsible for the proper development of any area which is part of a new town should have the power to carry out those works without being blocked by a neighbouring authority. Sometimes, neighbouring authorities have been known not to look upon developments proposed by the district council's neighbouring new town with the greatest possible enthusiasm. In these circumstances it would be wrong that we should permit this Bill to be approved without taking this matter into account. New town district council boundaries are frequently tightly drawn, and in these circumstances it is not inconceivable, for example, that a road which is essential to the development of a new town runs in part through a neighbouring district council's area.

190 The Minister should give consideration to this problem. When a transfer takes place he should do everything possible to ensure that a district council to which the assets are being transferred will not in any way be inhibited as a result of a disagreement or difficulties arising with other authorities, the county councils or neighbouring district councils.

This amendment is an effort to see that this point can be covered. I assure my hon. Friend that it is not something frivolous, but about something which I know from experience has arisen in the past. All of us could agree that it is necessary that essential developments should not be held up or prevented as a result of differences arising between different authorities. I am, therefore, moving this amendment in the hope that it will cover the point that I am seeking to make and will strengthen this Bill in this respect.
