I come now to the amendment. At first sight, it might appear to be identical to an amendment which was previously discussed in the context of Clause 8, but this amendment raises matters which are sufficiently different to merit the attention of the Committee. The difference between the two Clauses is that under Clause 8 the Appeals Board is dealing with a person aggrieved by the removal of his name from the register of equal opportunity employers whereas in Clause 14 a much more specific matter is at issue, namely, one or more of the grounds set out in subsection (2). Directions have been given by the agency, of course, and the appellant is arguing either that it is unreasonable for him to comply with them or that compliance with them would not secure equality of opportunity, or that he is already affording equality of opportunity.
