Mr. Michael Morris

Primarily this part of this clause makes good sense. It refers to the districts "in which the dwellings are situated". Our amendment is largely a probing amendment. When adjoining districts are involved we must recognise that not all local authorities will welcome the transfer of resources and the implications that go with that.

The financial implications will be discussed during debates on Clauses 8 and 9 and there will be considerable reservations about those clauses, reservations which mainly involve districts which are not involved in new towns. In the not too distant future there may be certain boundary changes. Modifications are still being made, local government election changes are being made, and others are pending. In addition, there are possible, peculiar circumstances in which a local authority may wish to take over certain dwellings from an adjacent authority. The local authority should not be railroaded. I hope that the Minister will assure us that adjoining district councils will not be forced to assent.
