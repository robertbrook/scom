But as the report emphasises, the ages of the children involved and their patterns of attendance vary considerably across a range of settings. Provision for education for children under 5 requires a sensitive and variable approach.

Additionally, new clause 22 would make education authorities responsible for management of educational services in establishments provided in the voluntary and independent sectors in playgroups and private nursery schools.

The guidelines supporting the Children Act 1989 have, through the registration provisions, placed greater emphasis on quality of the experience provided for children in day-care establishments. The education authority is obliged to act jointly with local authorities when they review, as they must under the provisions of the Act, the pattern of day care in their area.
