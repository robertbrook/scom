The Minister of State for Agriculture, Fisheries and Food (Mr. E. S. Bishop)
: When we adjourned this morning, the Committee was considering the points made by the hon. Member for Macclesfield (Mr. Winterton). I told him, with regard to his anxieties about the restrictive practices legislation, that a very wide measure of exemption for agricultural associations had been provided in the legislation since 1962, and that the Agricultural and Forestry Associations Act of that year exempted from registration the agreements made by formally constituted co-operatives and their members in connection with the marketing of members' produce and the supply of requisites to those members.

I recognise that over a period of time this matter has engaged the attention of the industry, the NFU and many others engaged in the production of food. The Agriculture (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1968 widened the classes of co-operativcs and agreements which could benefit under the 1962 Act. As recently as 1974, extensive consultations were carried out with the NFU and other interested organisations on the way in which the 1972 arrangements should be expanded and brought up to date. A substantial measure of agreement was reached and, as I mentioned this morning, the outcome was the Agricultural and Forestry Associations Order 1974 which came into force on 1st March 1975, only just over a year ago.

1190 The position has been reviewed, action has been taken and changes have been made. The most important change which the order made was to extend exemption from the restrictive trade practices legislation to arrangements under which a number of co-operatives consisting of individual producers agreed to sell their members' produce through a second-tier co-operative, which is referred to as a federal.

This new order widens still further the range of agreements to which exemption is given and it goes as far as possible to assist the legitimate activities of agricultural co-operatives while preserving a balance between their needs and the interests of consumers. I would remind the Committee of some of the points I made in this connection this morning.

As I said, the provisions of the Agricultural and Forestry Associations Order 1974 are somewhat complex, and I recognise that they need to be tested in the light of experience. I can give the hon. Member for Macclesfield the assurance that if any substantial difficulties are reported these will be fully considered in consultation with the interests concerned. If any changes in the existing legislation are considered to be desirable, they will be made.

Finally, the proposed EEC directive on producer groups could, when it appears, involve a much more radical reappraisal of the relevant legislation affecting agriculture associations, and it would seem good sense to wait for this. I think I can give an assurance that the matter is under review.

I would also emphasise that when changes of this sort are proposed a great deal of investigation and consultation is necessary on the restrictive practices front, with the Office of Fair Trading, with other Ministries, with the NFU and with a number of other organisations. Although I appreciate the hope of the hon. Member for Macclesfield and his hon. Friends that something might be done on this occasion, I think he will agree that this could bring about a radical change in the operation of agriculture. New Clause 18
NOTICES TO QUIT: TENANCIES OF OVER TWENTY-FIVE YEARS

'(1) Save for section (Operation of notice to quit given by reason of death of tenant) this Part of this Act shall not apply where—

  1. on or before the commencement of the tenancy, the landlord gave notice in writing to the tenant that possession might be recovered under this section; and
  2. the tenancy commenced on or after 29th September 1976.

(2) Section 24(1) of the Agricultural Holdings Act 1948 (which provides for the challenging of notices to quit by counter notices) shall not apply where notice to quit is served in respect of a tenancy to which this Part of this Act (save for section (Operation of notice to quit given by reason of death of tenant)) does not apply by reason of the foregoing subsection and at the date of the giving of the notice to quit at least 1200 twenty-five years had expired since the commencemept of the tenancy, and it is stated in the notice to quit that it is given by reason of the matter aforesaid.'.—[Mr. Benyon.]
