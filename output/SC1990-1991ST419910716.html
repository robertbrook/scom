The Chairman

Mr. Stewart
: It is always a pleasure, Mr. Lofthouse to serve under your chairmanship. I hope that the content of these two draft orders will not be controversial. They prescribe the rateable values for 1991–92 for the operation of lands and heritages of the Forth ports authority and Caledonian MacBrayne Ltd. For all other statutory dock and harbour authorities in Scotland, rateable values are calculated 4 each year by regional assessors, according to a formula prescribed by the Secretary of State in an order approved in 1990.

The Forth ports authority and Caledonian MacBrayne are treated separately because in each case the lands concerned span more than one valuation area. Consequently it would be awkward to have their valuations carried out by different regional assessors acting independently. We have therefore arranged for the valuations for these two undertakings to be calculated by officials following the same method that is applied to the other statutory port authorities and then to be apportioned among the local authorities concerned.

Mr. Tam Dalyell
(Linlithgow)
: May I ask the Minister whether the valuations are affected by the proposals for the new arrangements at Rosyth?

Mr. Stewart
: I can assure the hon. Gentleman that the announcement about Rosyth does not affect the orders.

The valuations are calculated by officials following the same method that is applied to the other statutory port authorities and are then apportioned among the local authorities concerned. The figures are in the draft order. The resulting rateable values are then prescribed. The method of valuation is to assess the rateable value at 9 per cent of the relevant income of the undertaking. Relevant income is the total income for the year, less that for certain defined activities which are not related to the occupation of property or are connected with properties separately assessed. This method is the same as that used for assessing other statutory ports in Scotland and south of the border. The apportionment of the rateable value of each undertaking among the relevant authorities is made on the basis of its relevant income in each area and is set out in the draft order.

The assessments and provisions contained in the orders have been the subject of consultations with the authorities involved, with the Convention of Scottish Local Authorities and with the Scottish Assessors Association. I am happy to assure hon. Members that those organisations are content with the draft orders.

Mr. Stewart
: Broadly speaking, the hon. Gentleman is correct. However, there are problems of definition. I was saying that some areas had difficulty applying the valuation principles to a network that crosses several valuation area boundaries.

It is not feasible to go further now because of the workload on the assessors. The next evaluation is in 1995, which is perhaps the earliest date at which change might be made, given the need for the assessors to collect essential information about each undertaking. I assure the Committee that we understand the point about this method not being entirely satisfactory. The hon. Member for Cathcart made that point well. We shall make the necessary changes and set up a working party that will reflect all the relevant interests.

The relevant authorities, COSLA and the Scottish Assessors Association are content with the orders 14 and the formulae. On that basis, I commend them to the Committee.
