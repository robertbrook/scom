(Belfast, South)
: When the Committee last met I raised the question of the figures that were given by my right hon. Friend the Member for Strangford (Mr. Taylor) about births in the Ulster hospital compared with those in the City hospital. As there might be some dispute about the figures, to clarify the issue.

I am not here to argue against the Ulster hospital. In my role as the Ulster Unionist health spokesman I seek to be fair and represent health needs and sort out the problems. However, as the Member for Belfast, South I have a remit to look after the services in my constituency.

I am thankful for the funding that has been given. However, some questions need to be asked and, during the appropriations debate, I raised the question of how we spend our money. In one particular hospital, there is a plan for a building, costing about £1 million, to house medical records. That plan could be replicated in other hospitals. However, people in information technology tell me that such records could be stored much more efficiently and cost-effectively in a modern computer system that would not cost £1 million and would not take up the same amount of space.

I had experience of medical records when I visited hospitals on chaplaincy calls. Each week, I tried to get a record of the admissions of patients with Presbyterian backgrounds. Quite often, I discovered that members of my own congregation were patients, yet I had been given no record of them. As I had just begun the work there, it was rather embarrassing.
