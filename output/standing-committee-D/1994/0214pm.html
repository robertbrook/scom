clause Clause 46
title MEANING OF "INSURANCE COMPANY"

Question proposed [this day]: That the clause stand part of the Bill.

4.30 pm

Question again proposed.

The Minister of State, Treasury (Mr. Anthony Nelson): When we adjourned for lunch, I was in the process of concluding our short debate on clause 46. One outstanding point remained. The hon. Member for Ashfield (Mr. Hoon) asked about the addition of Iceland and Norway. The new regulatory regime—the single passport in Europe—applies to all countries that have adopted the third life insurance directive. Under the European Economic Area agreement, EEA countries have signed up to and have been required to adopt the third life insurance directive. Of the original signatories, only two countries, Iceland and Norway, remain members of the EEA without joining the European Union. But since they have adopted the third life directive, Icelandic and Norwegian life insurance companies, which are not regulated by the DTI, come within the new extension to the definition of insurance company.

clause Clause 47
title TRANSFER OF LIFE INSURANCE BUSINESS

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

Mr. Alistair Darling (Edinburgh, Central): I wish to make a brief point. Can the Minister explain exactly what schedule 9 does and under what circumstances it is meant to come into play?

Mr. Nelson: The clause and its measures adapt the current provisions that deal with transfers of life and other long-term insurance business. Under current legislation, a number of special provisions apply that aim to provide continuity of tax treatment of the business, notwithstanding change of ownership. Those provisions apply where a scheme of transfer has been sanctioned by a United Kingdom court. The aim is twofold.

First, it ensures that the provisions will apply when the transfer of UK business has been sanctioned by the regulatory authority in another member state of the EU or the EEA. Each member state is now almost exclusively Col. 256 responsible for the regulation of its own insurance companies. That includes regulating transfers of business by a branch in another state such as the UK. Secondly, it prevents one of the provisions relating to capital allowances from being used where the transferee does not continue to carry on the business in the UK. That will ensure that appropriate balancing adjustments are made when an asset leaves the UK tax net so that tax relief is given on the whole of the net expenditure by a UK taxpayer, but on no more than the whole.

Those measures apply to transfers sanctioned or authorised after 30 June 1994 and will coincide with the EC third life and third non-life insurance directives which came into force on 1 July 1994. My right hon. Friend the Member for Loughborough (Mr. Dorrell) announced that we would introduce those measures in the Finance Bill in June last year.

The object of the tax exemption is to enable societies to offer worthwhile life insurance policies to that sector of the population that traditionally the friendly society movement alone has served—smaller savers who need small policies which inevitably carry disproportionately high administrative costs in relation to the premium or benefit insured.
