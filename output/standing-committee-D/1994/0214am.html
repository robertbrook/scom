It is important to understand that, in the case of with-profits business, the mechanism for apportioning investment return between different types of business is determined by reference to the bonuses paid to policyholders of that type of business. Where there is a mixture of with-profit and non-profit business in a fund, an apportionment by reference to bonuses can underestimate the return to the non-participating business. There is therefore a further mechanism, known in the insurance trade as the floor, to allocate investment return according to the ratio of the company's liabilities to different classes of policyholder, which applies where it gives a greater figure than the bonus-based method.

If a company changes the way in which it calculates its liabilities to its policyholders, the floor can result in the profit from, for example, pension business substantially exceeding the overall profit from the whole business. That was not intended, and paragraph (16) of schedule 8 as it stands proposes to amend the operation of the floor by providing that a company may set off the amounts by which the bonus-method calculation has exceeded the floor-method calculation in previous years against the floor-method calculation in a year in which the floor applies.

Paragraph (16) currently applies only for accounting periods starting on or after 1 January 1995. Amendments Nos. 88 and 89 provide that paragraph (16) should apply for accounting periods ending on or after 1 January 1994 and take account of excesses arising since the introduction of the floor in 1990.
