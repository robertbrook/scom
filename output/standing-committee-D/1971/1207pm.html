Gentlemen wish to refer and which has been the subject of a variety of different solutions put forward by Amendments, arising from those bodies which are now authorities of one sort or another but which, as a result of the introduction of two-tier system of government at a bigger scale than the existing structure provides for, will cease to be local authorities. The second range of questions which the Committee may wish to consider centres around the advisability of undertaking this exercise at all. What would be the benefits and disadvantages flowing from a decision of this sort? First, there is the valid argument that some functions will be exercised at the most local level, in other words, the parish level, ab initio, from 1st April, 1974. Parishes in these new dispossessed borough areas, if created, will be able to exercise some of the minor functions now in the hands of parishes, and this will benefit communities in dispossessed borough areas.

In addition, one wants to preserve the historical identity of many of these communities wherever possible. I think that this change would help to preserve such community interests, and that is an argument on the other side of my more technical argument—that local govern- Col. 266 ment reorganisation will be served better by allowing the new authorities proposed in the Bill to get off to an unimpeded start.

The third possible argument is that to have parish councils at the dispossessed borough level might enable the borough dignities to be preserved at the parish level.
