Mr. Morrison
: This Amendment attempts to deal with the territorial qualification of councillors with which this Clause is concerned. The form of the Amendment is related to an earlier Amendment, proposed by the hon. Member for Widnes (Mr. Oakes) in Clause 8( b), which was concerned with wards.

Before going further, I should like to say that there is a difference, in respect of my Amendment No. 638, between the situation which exists in the urban areas and in the rural areas. Amendment 638 aligns the provisions with regard to territorial qualifications all across the board, both n town and in country areas, I accept that this may be too far-reaching.

The principle which is involved in Amendment 638 is that the great en- 1448 largement of districts, which arises out of amalgamation of urban and rural areas, justifies a reappraisal of the qualification of candidates. The present principle of qualification is that a candidate may come from the whole of the local authority area in which an election is taking place. A new situation will arise in which the wards, or areas within a local authority total area, will be much greater than was previously the case. In consequence, there is an argument, in any case in the rural areas, for changing the principle of territorial qualification.

Furthermore, as the Bill is drawn at present, there is a danger of unbalanced representation if it continues on that basis. On the old basis, it would be possible for all, or a high proportion of, candidates for a district, to come from perhaps one substantial town, or even from one village, somewhere in the district, which happened to be the communications centre for that district. Clearly, if this happened—it may not be likely, but it could occur—this could mean that there would not be as good a representation of the areas and people involved as there should be. If representation was to become almost solely from an urban area within a district, the rural areas, and the arguments in respect of rural interests, would tend to be neglected, however hard the electorate's representatives tried to make a case for the rural areas. This would be due to the fact that those people living in rural areas, in certain instances, might be able to bring a rather different viewpoint to bear.

I hope that my hon. Friend will be able to give sympathetic consideration to the Amendments.
