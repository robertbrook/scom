    : I was much encouraged during the Minister's speech when he took up some of the points that had been raised on these Amendments relating to the proposed West Yorkshire County.     600 The Minister asked if I could provide evidence about the feelings of the local population in Horbury regarding this transfer. Using the opportunity of the Recess, such evidence has now been provided and within the urban district of Horbury an opinion poll has been held, the results of which I am able to give the Committee.

    At the outset, we attempted to get round the whole electorate of Horbury, but in the time available just 43 per cent. of the total electorate was contacted, although the 43 per cent. was spread as fairly as possible through all the electoral polling areas of Horbury.

    From this poll, in which ballot boxes were taken round to people's front doors for them to be able to cast their votes secretly, the total number of ballot papers counted was 2,457. Out of a total population of 8,914—not electorate—this represents a very large sample. Of the votes counted and not spoiled, 226 indicated preference for Horbury's inclusion in the district of West Yorkshire ( d) which includes Huddersfield and Dewsbury, and 2,131 votes were cast in favour of Horbury's inclusion in District ( e), which includes Wakefield and Pontefract. So it is a virtually 10 to 1 majority in favour of Horbury's inclusion in the Metropolitan District West Yorkshire ( e). Among the valid votes counted, 19.3 per cent. of the sample favoured inclusion of Horbury in the Metropolitan District ( e).

    That is overwhelming evidence of the wishes of the people of Horbury. I was able to visit the area during the Recess and, travelling by road from Wakefield to Horbury, I was hardly aware of moving from the county borough into the urban districts. The Ml motorway runs through, but certainly in this instance, and probably in most others where motorways are proposed as boundaries between local authorities, the motorway has more of an attracting effect than a repelling effect.

    Travelling further on the A642 from Wakefield to Huddersfield one goes through Horbury and then continues over the River Calder and through a village called Middlestown. Middlestown is, in effect, one of the parts of the parish of Sitlington in the Wakefield Rural District, which is to be included in Metropolitan District ( e) based upon Wakefield, and we have the situation where two parts of the proposed district, namely Wakefield JPEG 601 and Sitlington, are joined by a main road which passes through Horbury. Therefore, there is an argument for Horbury to be included in the same metropolitan district.

    Turning to the situation concerning Ossett, which neighbours both Horbury and Wakefield, obviously if Horbury is transferred from Metropolitan District ( d) to Metropolitan District ( e), then the case for Ossett similarly to be transferred is strengthened. Another public opinion survey has been conducted during the recess within the Borough of Ossett and I have the results here, but because Ossett has a larger population than Horbury, it was not quite such an ambitious survey, and the aim was to cover 1 in 10 of the population.

    I have here one of the ballot papers, which looks very official and looks like a normal election ballot paper. Altogether 1,146 were issued, which compares with 1,246—one-tenth of the Horbury electorate. Of the valid, unspoilt ballot papers counted there were 781 in favour of the inclusion of Ossett in Metropolitan District ( e) based on Wakefield, and 91 in favour of inclusion with District ( d), which includes Dewsbury and Hudders-field. So, although the majority is not quite so overwhelming as in the case of Horbury, 82.3 per cent. of the sample is in favour of Ossett's inclusion in the metropolitan district which includes Wakefield.

    Looking at the map one sees the close affinity between Ossett and Horbury. Socially, in a number of ways they are linked. I know from my own experience that Ossett and Horbury are within the same organisational unit of the Methodist Church which is strong in that part of the country. Similarly, if one looks at the community of interest, it will be seen that Ossett looks towards Wakefield as strongly as it looks in any other direction. There may be spheres of open country dotted around Ossett, but there is certainly a belt of open area which divides Ossett from the County Borough of Dewsbury. Thus Ossett has as strong a case for inclusion with Wakefield as for inclusion the other way. In view of the results of public opinion in the area, I submit that the case for Ossett, as in the case of Horbury, for inclusion in metropolitan district ( e) is very strong indeed.
