So I propose to quote from this letter. The writer says at the end that she does not want her name disclosed or her daughter who lives with her would be ashamed. She writes:

"I think you should know all the facts about some of us widows."

Very often the widows are much worse off than even a lot of the old age pensioners.

"When my husband died 6 years ago I had to give up my work. I am now registered as a disabled person although I am now able to be out and about. After working all my life I received 30/-pension plus £3 sickness benefit. After about a year everyone got a rise on their sickness and I had then £3.10 plus my 30/-pension total amount of £5 p.w. When the new increases in widows' pensions began last October I then received £5.58 pension but I did not get any more insurance money. So what I am trying to tell you, Mr. Hamilton, is that after 6 years I am still living on this disgraceful amount of money although I do get a rent-rates rebate. It is shocking to ask any women to live on this amount, as we have to pay the same for fuel and groceries as people with large incomes. I got quite sick when I read your page about the pensions Ministers receive. I would say our ways of living are a complete slur on our society …"

She then goes on:

"I have one daughter unmarried at home with me who is a shop assistant with a small wage and she has to look respectable at all times, so you will know she is not able to give me very much. At the moment Royal Ascot is on television with those daft women and their stupid clothes while most of us cannot get a decent bite to eat. Since my husband died I have never been able to get any clothes at all, but my married son does buy me shoes when I need them. Last year I applied to Society Security for bed-clothes. They came to see my bed which is most embarrassing for us both but they did grant me something in the region of £7–£8. I do not remember exactly."

And so it goes on, and every Member of this Committee and of the House receives letters in that vein.
