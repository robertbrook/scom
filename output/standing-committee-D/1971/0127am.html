It is obvious to every member of this Committee that we are talking about an airport which is often referred to as "London Airport-Gatwick", and there is basically something of a nonsense in the suggestion that one should transfer what is essentially a London-oriented airport away from the new county adjacent to Greater London to another county one stage removed which is very substantially oriented towards the South Coast.

No one will argue that the population and growth effects of an airport can be limited to any particular county boundary. Such effects will spread over into Sussex and Surrey, but much of the impact of this airport will continue to have its effect over Surrey. The noise factor, which is a dominant consideration when deciding airport matters, will continue to have its impact on Surrey. The line of the runways will ensure that it is the new Col. 1014 Surrey that will continue to suffer noise and which will have to take steps to adjust to that factor. All the communication links with Gatwick, the road and rail factors, and the service factor such as hotels and so on that one associates with an airport, will spread out naturally along the communication links northwards to London and not southwards into West Sussex.

Therefore, whenever one looks at the effects of this airport, with the one exception of residential expansion, Surrey obviously has the greater vested interest in continuing to maintain the area around Gatwick Airport. I think it is very valid to point out to the Committee that Surrey has in recent years acquired an international reputation in relation to its planning administration of the area around Gatwick Airport. I am told, and I am sure it is correct, that my right hon. Friend's Department. When it receives inquiries from foreign planners, about airport matters, actually sends these foreign observers to Surrey County Council because Surrey has an unrivalled reputation for the way in which it has handled all the very difficult problems associated with airport growth. It has very clear policies that have been effective in ensuring that around Gatwick Airport proper environmental considerations are taken into account. It has an environmental study that ensures that proper planning is undertaken in the area around the airport, a policy to protect the open land wherever possible, to provide for industry, offices, warehouses and such matters, and residential accommodation, but to ensure that it is in the right places. So, again, I am sure there is no doubt in the minds of anyone that Surrey has unrivalled expertise and experience that it has built upon this very specialist subject in recent years.

If it is to be transferred to another county administration, it will obviously be quite a few years before a new body of expertise and experience is built up by the new administrators. That cannot be a healthy thing to anticipate when we are talking about a matter as crucial to the lives of people as planning the facility of an airport.

This is an important planning factor. I also emphasise that it is a natural county boundary between the two and a boundary that has been created and defended by successive Goverments as a major factor in planning policy.

One can understand local fears that if one puts Gatwick and Crawley into one new district, within one new county, the green belt could well be eroded by the inevitable and desirable expansion of a successful and thrusting new town. There is a natural boundary between these two.

I know that hon. Members will often have been to Gatwick Airport and there is a superficial association in people's minds of Gatwick and Crawley. But I suggest that it is superficial when considering local government boundaries and I am sure that my right hon. Friend will not have made any superficial judgment about this matter; it will be exceptional if he has.

Again one draws the obvious conclusion that with an airport of this nature there are widespread, far flung economic links with the whole of the surrounding area.

It is significant to note that Maud did not propose that the airport should be transferred into the southerly county. They suggested that it should be kept together but that it should be transferred to Surrey. What we are facing here is a direct reversal of the Maud Commission proposal. I am not necessarily against reversing Maud—we have done quite a lot of that—but we can draw certain lessons from it. On public opinion grounds, and airport planning grounds, there is no case for transferring this into Sussex.

I suspect that what we are really discussing is the whole question of planning for the growth area in the context of the South East regional studies. I. believe this is what has motivated the Government in trying to move this important airport and its neighbouring communities into Sussex.

In the Strategic Plan for the South-East, this area was described as growth Col. 1016 area No. 6 and, at that time, it spread over different county boundaries. This is the sole reason why the Government are prepared to transfer it, simply to ensure that one has this one growth area, growth area No. 6, under the one county administration. On the face of it, that is the logical argument. I then ask the Government, is it their policy that all such growth areas should be under one county administration, or are there any areas where they are not following such a principle?

One does not have to look very far—only to the other side of the county—to show that the Government have already totally conceded that it is not necessary for such a growth area to fall within one county. One has only to look at the area which is described as growth area No. 8, the Reading, Wokingham, Aldershot and Basingstoke districts, a major growth area, which is to be administered, under the new plans, by joint planning committees of three counties, Hampshire, Berkshire and Surrey. Not only have the Government conceded this case totally, but they went a bit further because, as a change between the White Paper proposals and the new plans, they reconsidered and have allowed part of the area that was in Surrey and was proposed to go to Hampshire to stay in Surrey. We have a total concession on that particular point.
