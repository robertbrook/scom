It made clear that, when notifying a change of name, all other names that had been previously notified must also be provided.

I can confirm to the hon. In connection with that, the hon. Member for Bolton, North-East (Mr. Thurnham) asked about registration provisions for children in Scotland and England. The requirement is for their names to be registered for any period that might apply following a conviction for a registrable offence.

The hon. Member for Dumbarton (Mr. McFall) asked me to explain the position concerning the act of adjournal and the accused serving a notice on the prosecutor under clause 8(4). I shall not detain the Committee for long, but I wish to explain that technical point. Subsection (4) proceeds on the basis that, if an act carried out in a foreign country constituted an offence under the law of the country and would constitute a listed sex offence in Scotland if done there, for the purpose of the Scottish prosecution there is no need to establish that the act was an offence under the foreign law unless the accused gives notice that he requires the prosecution to establish that.

The dual criminality test will not have been established overseas. It requires to be satisfied as part of the Scottish prosecution. The approach is to deem it to be satisfied unless the accused requires the prosecutor to show that it is satisfied. Under the Bill, the prosecutor ought to be satisfied that the dual criminality test had been met before bringing the prosecution. However, subsection (4) gives the right to the accused to test that point. Under court procedure, the accused must give notice that he wishes the dual criminality test to be examined. The act of adjournal merely prescribes the time limit for serving such a notice. It would then be for the court to decide the issue.
