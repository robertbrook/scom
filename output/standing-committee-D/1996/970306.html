Fifth Standing Committee on Delegated Legislation
Thursday 6 March 1997

[MR. JOHN MCWILLIAM in the Chair]

Draft Medicines Control Agency Trading Fund (Variation) Order 1997

10.30 am

The Minister for Health (Mr. Gerald Malone)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the draft Medicines Control Agency Trading Fund (Variation) Order 1997.

A full consultation report on this matter has been before the House since 11 February. I am happy to respond to any questions that members of the Committee may have as a result of their perusal of it. The order implements the change set out in the consultation report. I commend it to the Committee.

10.31 am

Mr. Kevin Barron
(Rother Valley)
: It is common sense to add the responsibilities outlined in the report to the trading fund. However, I wonder why we are 4 here to consider the small issue of extending the operations of the fund when no one in the House or the country objects to the proposal. The annual accounts of the trading fund show that it saved money on its administration costs between 1994–95 and 1995–96. Why does not the House consider saving money on its administration costs? Of course, if there were an objection to the order, I would understand why we were in this Room to consider it. However, there appears to be no objection.

The Chairman
: If that were in the nature of a point of order, I must tell the hon. Gentleman that we are here to consider the order because the legislation is required and because the Standing Order says that we must consider it.

Mr. Malone
: You, Mr. McWilliam, have dealt absolutely with the point raised by the hon. Member for Rother Valley (Mr. Barron). I have nothing further to add.

Question put and agreed to.

Resolved

That the Committee has considered the draft Medicines Control Agency Trading Fund (Variation) Order 1997.

Committee rose at twenty-eight minutes to Eleven o'clock.
