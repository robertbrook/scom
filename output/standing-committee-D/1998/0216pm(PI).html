Schedule 3 demonstrates, as did schedule 1, that the Bill is about the humiliating emasculation of the Department of Social Security. Many of its key functions—policy functions in the case of schedule 3; operational functions in the case of schedule 1—are to be transferred to the Treasury and Revenue. It is to submit any remaining functions to the Treasury. No longer are these independent Departments of state. The Department of Social Security becomes the servant—a mere clerk—of the Treasury.
