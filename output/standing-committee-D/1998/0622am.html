Mr. Foulkes

Member for Chesham and Amersham (Mrs. Gillan) who asked about employees, including whether we had considered the Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 1981. We do not think that they will apply when the CDC becomes CDC plc, because there is no relevant transfer in the meaning of the regulations. There is no transfer of undertakings from one person to another. The CDC will be the same corporate body before and after transformation. Its employees' contracts of employment will simply continue after transformation, and the employer will remain the same legal person.

A similar argument applies to employees' pension rights, another subject raised by the hon. Lady. We believe that that statement also holds true for all jurisdictions in which the CDC currently has employees. In the event that continuity of contractual arrangements is not possible in a jurisdiction, the CDC will ensure that affected employees are in no way disadvantaged as a result of transformation. The CDC will continue to review its employment terms and conditions with a view to ensuring that it remains able to attract and retain staff of the calibre necessary to achieve its objective.

The CDC will be a public limited company, which in some definitions is a public body, but it will be the same legal entity. The employer will be the same person. As the two Opposition spokespersons are eminent lawyers, I know that they will understand that precisely.
