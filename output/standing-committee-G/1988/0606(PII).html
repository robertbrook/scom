Mr. Ian Taylor: It is not often that one has so long to reflect on one's peroration. I shall bring my remarks to a close, slowly. I hope that the Financial Secretary will have major points of technical discussion with the Inland Revenue to ensure that the proposals are as lacking in restriction as possible. In a sense, there is nothing new in a fiscal context. There were case law provisions for tax deductibility of corporate contributions to employee benefit trusts, and they still exist. Will case law continue to pertain? The change is that there is now a statutory recognition of the form of employee share ownership trust, which is identified in the Bill and has those qualifying conditions. Those of us who wish to see an extensive spread of the ESOPs principle are worried that the qualifying conditions are drawn too tightly in relation to options, start-up companies and the composition of trustees.

There is also concern about the CGT implications, to which I referred earlier. They should be examined carefully, as they could act as a disincentive to a trust that was fulfilling the important role of acting as a market maker, or even as a jobber, between workers leaving the company and those coming in. An employee benefit trust has a role other than that of getting the shares to the employees as quickly as possible, which is to ensure that workers have the opportunity to sell when they leave and that new workers have the opportunity to buy. As there may be a difference in price in those transactions, I urge the Financial Secretary to examine the CGT implications.

Employee share ownership can make a great contribution to the broadening of the spread of capital. The arguments were well rehearsed this time last year, so I shall not repeat them. The Financial Secretary has introduced a tremendous advance in the provisions. I heartily commend them and hope that he will consider my reservations about some of the details in the qualifying terms that he has laid down.

The purpose of the amendment is to align the requirements governing the shares that may be acquired by the trust more closely with those that apply in the case of shares used in the approved employee share schemes.

In aligning this provision with that applying in the case of approved employee share schemes, the amendment has the effect of permitting the shares used to be subject to a requirement that if held by a director or an employee they must be sold on his departure, but only if the terms of such a disposal apply equally to disposals of all shares of the same class by other shareholders.
