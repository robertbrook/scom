Sir George Young: I am grateful to my hon. Friend the Member for Harrow, West (Mr. Hughes) for the moving speech that he was about to make before we rose at One o'clock.

I welcome the separate powers in the clause for the promotion of economic development by local authorities. Previously, it was regarded as a marginal activity, perhaps to be funded under section 137. That was unsatisfactory, and I am delighted that the Goverment are introducing these powers to fund economic development. To have the powers explicitly set out makes for clearer legislation and government.

However, the voluntary movement has expressed anxiety about whether the clause, as drafted, enables local authorities to fund, through voluntary organisations, the activities which the Government have in mind. The key words are in lines 27 and 28 of subsection (2)(a) which refers to

"the setting up or expansion of any commercial, industrial or public undertaking".

Amendments Nos. 101 and 102 would simply add "or voluntary organisation" to make it clear that the partnership with the voluntary sector, on which my right hon. Friend and his colleagues are keen, can continue under the provisions in clause 25.

The voluntary sector feels that there is confusion in that the category of public undertaking might exclude voluntary organisations because, elsewhere, section 137 defines voluntary organisations as not being "public bodies". It would be clearer if the provision referred directly to voluntary organisations Col. 460 in their own right, as the amendment suggests, as being eligible for funding under the power, especially as there seems to be no policy reason for not doing so.

My right hon. Friend knows the valuable work done in many inner cities by voluntary organisations in providing training and small workshops and in encouraging first-time entrepreneurs. For example, the Ealing enterprise agency—a voluntary organisation under the dynamic leadership of Gordon Foss—has done much useful work during the past three years. The Park Royal enterprise trust in my constituency, under the chairmanship of Ansell Harris, has also done useful work in economic development and especially in training to provide skills which are in short supply in west London.

The voluntary groups responded to Government suggestions that they should become more involved in training and activities aimed at generating employment and creating wealth to strengthen inner-city economies. They have done so and hope that clause 25 will enable them to continue their activities.

That successful partnership approach to economic development should continue under the new power. Local authorities should not be precluded from assisting local advice and development agencies and voluntary sector bodies involved in economic development projects.

I understand that the official advice currently being communicated is that the voluntary sector can be funded under the provisions in the Bill. My amendment would simply make that power explicit and remove any confusion.
