The unquoted sector is an enormous tranche of economic life in this country. The Committee will be aware of the seminal work on the subject by the two Oxford economists, Hay and Morris. I am worried about the effects of capital taxation, especially on the unquoted, long-standing family company. My hon. Friend the Member for Dover (Mr. Shaw) mentioned those who wish unquoted companies to be taken to the unlisted market, or perhaps to a full listing on the stock exchange and so on. Since the second world war, the unquoted sector has been subject to three considerable sources of tax, which have removed from many parts of the country substantial long-term family businesses. I refer first to nationalisation. The takeover by the state of family companies in the north-east, the north-west, the midlands and in Scotland, and the removal of decision-making power from the shipyards, the steel industry and so on to London, has had a damaging effect on our economic life.
