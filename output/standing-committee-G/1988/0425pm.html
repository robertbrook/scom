The interest of the applicant in the property

4.30 pm

Question again proposed, That the amendment be made.

The Chairman
: I remind the Committee that with this it will be convenient to take amendment No. 632, in page 83, line 28, at end insert 'except in special circumstances'.

The Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for the environment (Mrs Virginia Bottomley)
: I wish to assure the hon. Member for Newham, North-West (Mr. Banks) that, in most cases, we believe that responsibility for essential repairs and improvements of property rests with the landlord, not the tenant. When it clearly does not, for example when the tenant has a repairing obligation, the tenant should be eligible to apply for a grant. The Bill provides for that.

Were we to permit all private tenants to apply for a grant, we would provide no incentive to landlords to carry out the work that is properly their responsibility. Moreover, a landlord may have the incentive to collude with the tenant in not carrying out the work and may encourage the tenant to apply for a grant in the knowledge that he may be treated more favourably than the landlord. Such a provision would not be a proper solution, as it would take place at the expense of another who might need a grant.

We must also consider such a restriction on granting aid to tenants in the light of the strengthened powers that we gave to local authorities in the Housing Act 1988 to take action against landlords who were unwilling to carry out essential repair or improvement work. Those steps, together with the freeing of the market for tenanted properties under the Act, emphasise our determination to ensure that private-sector landlords take seriously their obligations and carry out the work for which they are responsible.

It would be wholly misguided to give tenants with no such responsibilities the right to a grant. That would benefit no one, apart from the less scrupulous landlords. We recognise that many private tenants without repairing obligations need minor works carried out to their homes if they are to live in comfort and dignity. That is why we intend to make a minor grant available to them under new clause 8. I assure the hon. Member for Newham, North-West that the position of private tenants is likely to be improved rather than made worse by the provisions of the Bill, together with those in the 1988 Act.

New clause 12

Qualifying buildings and external works, etc

'(1) A building is not a qualifying building in relation to a group repair scheme unless, at the time the scheme is prepared, the whole or some part of the exterior of the building is not in reasonable repair and that lack of reasonable repair affects at least 75 per cent of the houses contained in the building.


(2) Every group repair scheme shall relate to at least one qualifying building (in this section referred to as "the primary building") which was constructed so as to comprise not less than four separate houses and may also relate to one or more other qualifying buildings if the following conditions are fulfilled with respect to each of them—

  1. the building was constructed so as to comprise at least one house and is contiguous or adjacent to the primary building; and
  2. The exterior of the building is not in reasonable repair and is in need of works similar to those required to the exterior of the primary building; and
  3. carrying out the works to the building and the primary building at the same time is the most effective way of securing the repair of each of them.


(3) The question whether a building was constructed so as to comprise not less than four houses or at least one house shall be determined according to the configuration of the building at the date of its construction.


(4) For the purposes of this section—

  1. a terrace of houses shall be regarded as one building except that, if it appears appropriate to a local housing authority to do so, having regard in particular to the requirements of subsection (1) above, they may treat part only of the terrace as a building; and
  2. if, apart from this paragraph, one building would be regarded as containing two or more purpose-built flats and one or more houses, the part of the building containing the purpose-built flats and the part or parts of the building containing the houses shall be regarded as separate buildings.

(5) In relation to a group repair scheme, "external works" are works to any part of the exterior of a building to which the scheme relates and, so far only be necessary to give satisfactory effect to such works, additional works to other parts of the building.

  1. any part thereof which is exposed to the elements of wind and rain or otherwise faces into the open air (including, in particular, roofs, chimneys, walls, doors, windows, rainwater goods and external pipework); and
