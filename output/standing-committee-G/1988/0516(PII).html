Given that we know the conclusion that we wish to reach, why have we deemed it appropriate to introduce the ruling incorporated in schedule 3? After considerable scrutiny of the terms of the judgment by legal experts, it was concluded that it was not permissible to retain zero-rating for construction services supplied for charities' business purposes. The Court ruled that, whereas zero-rating for housing was acceptable, the zero rates for industrial and commercial buildings and community and civil engineering works were not, because the supplies concerned were not provided to final consumers. The judgment defines a final consumer as a person

"who acquires goods or services for personal use, as opposed to an economic activity"

and finds that zero-rating for construction is permissible only when supplies are made to such a person or to someone so close to the final consumer that the supply may be regarded as being for the final consumer's benefit. We have concluded that that justifies the continued zero-rating of supplies for charities' non-business activities, as in this context they are final consumers, but not for their business activities. "Business' is a widely drawn concept in European Community and domestic VAT law, and includes virtually any supply of goods or services for money or its equivalent, irrespective of whether a profit is made or aimed at.

It might help the Committee if I read out the underlying text from the "Richtlinie zur Harmonisierung". Article 4(2) states:

"The economic activities referred to in paragraph 1"—

which were presumably referred to in the Court's ruling— 90

"shall comprise all activities of producers, traders and persons supplying services including mining and agricultural activities and activities of the professions. The exploitation of tangible or intangible property for the purpose of obtaining income therefrom on a continuing basis shall also be considered an economic activity."

It has been ruled repeatedly that obtaining an income does not require one to make or retain a profit. In this case, we had to recognise that village halls that hired themselves out were obtaining an income and exploiting tangible property and so came within the EC definition of economic activity and business.
