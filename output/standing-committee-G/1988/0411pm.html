The Minister for Local Government (Mr. John Gummer)
: The Committee will remember that, when we adjourned, I was in media res and explaining what will happen to housing associations that are companies or industrial and provident societies, which they almost all are. I use the word "almost" only lest I am proved incorrect.

The associations will be included under part V and, if they meet the test, will be subject to the regulations as controlled or influenced companies. That is right because they would be extensions of a local authority rather than independent bodies. They are covered in the Bill for practical and sensible reasons and not for dogmatic purposes. They are included not because of their name but because they fit the requirements of the Bill. I assure my hon. Friend the Member for Ealing, Acton (Sir George Young) that he is right to ask that associations are covered, and that they are brought within the scope of the Bill.

People who do not want housing associations or other bodies to be covered by the Bill must explain why a body that in material respects is controlled or considerably influenced by a local authority should be able to operate outwith capital controls. Those who argue that the capital controls should be evaded because the amount of capital spending allowed is not sufficient should urge us to allow further capital expenditure. They cannot suggest that we should give some authorities greater capital expenditure than 786 their needs suggest by allowing capital expenditure by a controlled or substantially influenced company instead of making it a direct and clear responsibility of local government.
