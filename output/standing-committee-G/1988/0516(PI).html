The Chairman: With this it will be convenient to take Government amendments Nos. 50 to 53.

Mr. Lilley: We accept your wise ruling on the previous matter, Mr. Cormack, and will naturally try to comply with your wishes and ensure that such circumstances do not recur.

The amendments in the name of my right hon. Friend the Chief Secretary are in response to consultations since the original announcement was made about car tax relief for cars for the handicapped, to meet certain problems that had arisen with the trade. The clause as drafted provided that a car tax registered trader must hold a certificate

"at the time when he sends it out".

It being the vehicle. That provision is too restrictive because it is the practice for car manufacturers and importers to send out vehicles to dealers on a sale or return basis. The existing clause does not allow relief on vehicles supplied from such stock, because at the time of supply the vehicle has already been sent out.

Adoption of the amendments would extend relief to vehicles supplied from the dealer's sale or return stock provided that he holds the certificate on the date on which the vehicle is supplied. It would fit in with trade practice and allow the handicapped person to take delivery of the vehicle more quickly.

The clause will be widely welcomed by the Committee as it has been already by Motability and all those concerned with providing vehicles for the handicapped. The relief applies whether the handicapped person proposes to drive the leased car, or is driven in it by someone else. It applies to ordinary cars, not just those that must be specially converted for the handicapped. The relief reduces the financial burden on those who, through handicap, cannot use public transport. Motability estimates that as many as 30,000 cars a year may eventually benefit from the relief, because it will reduce costs, especially initial costs, enabling more handicapped people to lease cars than at present.

I hope that the Committee will accept the amendments, which simply improve the working of a widely welcomed relief.
