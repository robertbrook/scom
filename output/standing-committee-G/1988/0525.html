Clause 52
ELIGIBLE CONTRACTS

The Chief Secretary to the Treasury (Mr. John Major)
: I beg to move amendment No. 164, in page 41, line 26, leave out from "and" to end of line 28 and insert

"being either a national of a member State or a company or partnership formed under the law of any part of the United Kingdom or another member State and having its registered office, central administration or principal place of business in a member State.".

This is essentially a technical amendment, which corrects the drafting of clause 52(8)(b). Its effect is slightly to restrict the definition of "qualifying insurer" to what has originally been intended. Our intention is that tax relief will be available only on eligible contracts of private medical insurance that are issued by qualifying insurers. That includes all insurers lawfully carrying on medical insurance business in the United Kingdom, but it also includes 226 insurers of European Community countries who are not carrying on business in the United Kingdom by virtue of establishing a branch here but who nevertheless wish to sell tax-relieved medical insurance contracts here. It is of the essence of the freedom of markets within the EC that they must be able to do so.

The clause as drafted at present would go rather beyond that. It would also allow non-EC insurance companies whose only presence in Europe is a branch in another EC country to offer tax-relieved contracts. That goes well beyond what the Government intended and well beyond the requirement of the European Community non-life insurance services directive. The amendment therefore limits the relief to contracts issued by insurers lawfully carrying on business in the United Kingdom and genuine European Community insurers with no formal presence in the United Kingdom. I hope that hon. Members can accept the amendment, which restricts the tax relief, although I understand that Opposition Members object to the general principle of the relief. However, in this instance it restricts it to the original intention, and I hope that on that basis the amendment will be acceptable to the Committee.

Instead of having each individual 258 contract certified, the insurer will submit a standard form of contract to the Revenue to certify. The intention is to save work for the insurer and the Inland Revenue.

Clause 52 envisages that approach, but clauses 53 and 54 as drafted do not provide the means to achieve it. The proposed amendments to clause 54 therefore allow the board, in regulations, to make the necessary provisions for certification of standard forms and for appeals against refusal by the board to certify standard forms. The regulations will provide for the certification to be carried out on the basis of the conditioins outlined in clause 53 for individual contracts.

Amendment No. 171 is a small drafting amendment to correct a self-evident defect. As drafted, the clause would not have allowed the Inland Revenue directly to prescribe the form of the various documents needed to operate the scheme effectively—for example, claims for relief and annual returns from medical insurers. The normal approach for other schemes is to allow the board to prescribe forms direct, and that is what the amendment would achieve for that specific relief. I trust that the Committee will accept the amendment, which is uncontentious and a drafting correction.
