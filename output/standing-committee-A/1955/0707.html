As I understand my hon. Friend's Amendment, what he would wish to do is to have a district list of highways as well as a county council list. In other words, it would be an entirely new departure. But the county council can delegate this function, or it can ask the district council to contribute voluntarily to the improvement of the roads, as is done at present in the case of the Highlands. There is nothing whatever to prevent district councils putting up proposals to the county councils for roads to be improved under this Bill, but we do not think that it should be the district council which should put the proposals to the Secretary of State, because in the first place this would mean duplication and overlapping; secondly, it would involve what are really fundamental changes in the present system of responsibility for highways; thirdly, it would almost certainly necessitate some amendment in the powers of 6 district authorities to levy rates which would be quite outside the scope of this Bill; and, lastly, the system has worked quite well in the Highlands under the Congested Districts (Scotland) Act and we believe it will work well also in the Bill as now drafted.
