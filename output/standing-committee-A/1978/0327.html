The Parliamentary Secretary to the Ministry of Overseas Development (Mr. John Tomlinson)

"14. Negotiating and making contracts for the assembly, installation, commissioning of maintenance of equipment.".

The Chairman
: With this we arc to take the following amendments:

No. 5, in clause 5, page 5, line 10, at end insert—

"(ll) subject to subsection (3), assembling, installing, commissioning or maintaining any equipment procured and sold in pursuance of paragraph (l);".

No. 7, in clause 5, page 5, line 23, at end insert—

"( aa) the power under subsection (1)( a) to provide any of the services mentioned in subsection (2)( ll);".

Mr. Tomlinson
: Amendment No. 35 is largely self-explanatory. As drafted, the Bill empowers the Crown Agents to procure property as agents on behalf of their principlas. The effect of this amendment would be to allow the Crown Agents to place contracts on behalf of their principals for the assembly, installation, commissioning and maintenance of equipment. In most cases these services will relate to equipment that the Crown Agents have procured, but since this is an agency service there is no reason to restrict it in this way, and the amendment will leave the Crown Agents free to place contracts for such services on behalf of principals for equipment which they themselves have not procured.

These are services which the Crown Agents already offer as agents and we think they should continue to do so. Not to be able to offer these services would seriously reduce the Crown Agents' effectiveness as a procurement agency whenever 54 installations or maintenance were factors influencing procurement. I hope, therefore, that the Committee will accept the amendment.

Amendments Nos. 5 and 7 concern the same services, although their extent is slightly different. They authorise the Crown Agents to assemble, instal, corn-mission and maintain equipment procured in their own right under Clause 5(2)( l), subject to ministerial consent. From the point of view of the principal, the installation or commissioning of a piece of equipment may often count as an integral part of its procurement, and it therefore seems right to make it possible for the Crown Agents to offer these services as part of their own procurement function.

Amendent agreed to.

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

Mr. George Cunningham
: This is the 102 clause where, in my view, we could deal, if we so wished, with the point raised by the hon. Member for Norfolk, North-West (Mr. Brocklebank-Fowler) about prohibiting the Crown Agents from doing things, even when they are acting as agents. I wish to make the point at this stage because, as I have said before, I feel that there should be some limitations upon what the Crown Agents can do, even when they are acting as agents. I do not think that it would be right—and it would be blatantly in conflict with agency status—to have advance rules.

I think that the Minister should use her power to request information in order to satisfy herself at any given time whether they are doing anything of this kind that she does not like. If they are doing something—such as investing in brothels, or whatever it may be—she should use her powers of direction to prohibit it. I think I am right in saying that at the moment she would not be able to do that if they were acting as agents. I should like to leave the point for consideration later whether, even at this late stage, it would not be right to reverse the position and make it a qualification to their total freedom to do whatever they are permitted or instructed to do by an agent by giving the Minister power to give them directions. That would involve deleting the words "in their own right".

Mrs. Hart
: Of course, I shall give careful consideration to the point made by my hon. Friend. I am grateful to him for not pressing it unduly.

Question put and agreed to.

Clause 23 ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Clause 24 ordered to stand part of the Bill.

Clause 25
THE CROWN AGENTS HOLDING AND REALISATION BOARD

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

Mr. Spearing
: This clause sets up the Crown Agents Holding and Realisation Board which deals with the Four Millbank group of companies. Schedule 5, with which JPEG 103 we will deal in a moment, has six complex pages of regulations, which bind this board much closer to Ministerial direction than the others. Clause 1 and Schedule 1, however, do not mention the word "board".

The point that I wish to raise is a practical one. The Board of the Crown Agents will consist of six to 10 members both at home and abroad, appointed by the Minister. In common parlance some word will have to be used, and it will be "board". I suggest that the use of the word "board" in the following schedule might cause some complications, both in general language and possibly in understanding.

Mrs. Hart

Friend will appreciate that it would be immensely desirable—as we are at the last edges of completing our business today—if perhaps he could write to me on the points that he wishes to raise. Both sides of the Committee would appreciate it if we could conclude our business in these next four minutes.

Mr. Spearing
: I was about to come to my last sentence. I hope that the Minister will look at this point. If there is anything in what I have said, I hope that she will think about it and perhaps do something.
