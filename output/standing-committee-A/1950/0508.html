This Amendment concerns a more substantial point. The words that I propose should be omitted enable the Treasury, in the scheme which they make under Section 58 of the principal Act, to lay down the manner in which questions as to who is liable and to what extent he is liable are to be determined. The ground on which I would prefer to see these words omitted is that it is for Parliament and not for the Treasury to decide how the application of any Act of Parliament is to be determined.

Normally, of course, unless any other provision is written into an Act of Parliament, the interpretation is referred to the courts. In certain circumstances, where it has been thought more convenient, it has been laid down in an Act that decisions shall be taken by other tribunals or by Ministerial authority. But it seems proper that it should be Parliament itself which decides whether an interpretation other than from the courts should be admitted.

By Clause 4 we are now, in 1951, jobbing backwards. We are now saying, "We are changing the law.
