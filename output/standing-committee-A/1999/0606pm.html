: In respect of local authorities that adopt executive arrangements under part II, the clause will disapply the requirement in the Local Authority Social Services Act 1970 that local authorities must discharge their social services functions through social services committees. Because all social services functions will, under executive arrangements, be the responsibility of the executive, there will no longer be a requirement for a social services committee.

Subsection (1) cannot simply repeal the requirement for a social services committee, because part II will not extend to the City of London and the Council of the Scilly Isles, which are social services authorities. Moreover, there will be a transitional period in which some councils to which part II will apply will have adopted executive arrangements, while others will have yet to do so.

Subsection (2) deals with the role of the director of social services. Local authorities will be required to JPEG 415 appoint such a director, but the requirement that the Secretary of State's approval be obtained before the director discharges non-social services functions will be repealed. That requirement has proved restrictive and burdensome, and we want to get rid of such provisions. Subsection (3) redrafts the definition of social services functions, and replicates an order-making power in the 1970 Act. I hope that such background to the clause has proved helpful.

Amendments Nos. 306 to 317 are minor and technical drafting amendments that will give full effect to the policy in the clause. Amendment No. 306 makes it clear that the requirement for local authorities to establish social services committees will not apply to authorities that operate executive arrangements under the Bill. In effect, amendment No. 307 will move the content of subsection (3) from a new subsection (6)(a) of the 1970 Act to a new section l(a) of that Act.

Amendments Nos. 310, 311, 315 and 316 will amend various enactments that are consequential on provisions in clause 90. Amendment No. 311 will omit subsection 2(2) from the 1970 Act, and amendment No. 317 will add that subsection to the list of repealed provisions in schedule 5. Amendment No. 315 will amend the Registered Homes Act 1984 and paragraph 6 of schedule 6 to the Children Act 1989. Amendment No. 312 is a minor drafting amendment and 416 amendment No. 314 is in part consequential on amendment No. 307.

As you can see, Mrs. Michie, the amendments are indeed technical and minor, and I trust that there are no anoraks among Committee members who wish to debate them fully.
