Mr. Eyre: I beg to move amendment No. 25, in page 12, line 40, after "refused", insert "or withdrawn".

The effect of the amendment is to reduce the scope of the defence in subsection 1(a) so that an old public company and any officer in default will be guilty of an offence under the subsection if, having applied for re-registration as a public company under clause 8, it has at that time withdrawn the application.

Clause 9 is aimed at ensuring that old public companies take the necessary steps to determine their status under the new classification. Clause 8 provides a flexible system for doing this, but it is obviously important from the point of view of shareholders and creditors that companies should not remain in no-man's-land. Clause 9 therefore imposes criminal sanctions on companies which remain old public companies at the end of the re-registration period, unless they are in the process of becoming public or Image Col. 131 private under the new classification. Paragraph (a)provides a defence when they are becoming public and paragraph (b)when they are becoming private.

Paragraph (a), however, is considered to be too generous. It aims to identify companies which are in the process of seeking to become public and so excludes companies which, although they have applied, have had their application refused by the registrar. It ought also to exclude cases where a company applies but, before the matter is decided, withdraws the application. This is what the amendment achieves. It also brings paragraph (a)into line with paragraph (b), which contemplates the revocation of the special resolution, which is the equivalent of the withdrawal of the application under paragraph (a).
