Mr. Eyre
: I beg to move, That the clause be read a Second time.

The clause lays down similar prohibitions and exceptions to those contained in new clause 42, but in relation to Crown servants. The sort of information which would prevent Crown servants dealing is the same as for insiders, in that it is information acquired by virtue of the Crown servant's position, which it would be reasonable to expect him to hold in confidence and which he knows is unpublished price sensitive information, as defined in new clause 46. The clause will ensure that the wide range of commercially sensitive information which Crown servants are likely to receive will not be made use of for personal gain.

(2) In this section—

"off-market dealer" means a person who—

  1. holds a licence under section 3 of the Prevention of Fraud (Investments) Act 1958 (principals and representatives' licences for dealers in securities); or
  2. is a member of a recognised association of dealers in securities within the meaning of that Act; or
  3. is an exempted dealer within the meaning of that Act.

(3) For the purposes of this section an off-market dealer shall be taken to deal in advertised securities if he deals in such securities or acts as an intermediary in connection with deals made by other persons in such securities; and references in this section to such a dealer's officer, employee or agent dealing in such securities shall be construed accordingly.

(4) For those purposes an individual shall be taken to deal through an off-market dealer if the latter is a party to the transaction, is an agent for either party to the transaction or is acting as an intermediary in connection with the transaction.".—[ Mr. Parkinson.]

Brought up, and read the First time.

Mr. Parkinson
: I beg to move, That the clause he read a Second time.

As I explained in my opening remarks on new clause 42, we did not find the clause in the previous Bill, on the transactions other than those on the Stock Exchange, satisfactory. We believe that what is important is to extend the provisions to cover similar markets to the Stock Exchange. We wish to apply the 606 same provisions to other markets as we apply to the Stock Exchange in the various clauses of the Bill.

Off-market dealers are defined by reference to those who are authorised for the purposes of the Prevention of Fraud (Investments) Act 1958. The detailed provisions are set out in subsection (2). The provisions do not apply to all deals conducted by or through such dealers, but only those in advertised securities. This means listed securities and securities with, in effect. a published price. This is the effect of the definition in subsection (2). It is necessary, we believe, to impose a cut-off point to ensure that shares which once had a published price and were dealt with in a more general market are excluded if they have not been dealt in recently, and this is the purpose of the six-month period in the definition of "advertised securities".

Subsections (3) and (4) serve to define when a person deals through an off-market dealer and when, indeed, an off-market dealer himself deals. This ties in with the prohibitions in subsection (1).

It should be noted that subsection (1) has been redrafted to make its effect absolutely clear with regard to the secondary offences of counselling, procuring and so on. As drafted in the consultative document, there was some danger that it would not be appreciated that these offences were picked up, as indeed they were intended to be, by the final four lines of subsection (1) of clause (C) in the consultative document.

New Clause 45
CONTRAVENTION OF SECTIONS (PROHIBITION ON STOCK EXCHANGE DEALS BY INSIDERS, ETC.) AND (PROHIBITION ON ABUSE OF INFORMATION OBTAINED IN OFFICIAL CAPACITY)

"(1) An individual who contravenes the provisions of section (Prohibition on stock exchange deals by insiders, etc.) or (Prohibition on abuse of information obtained in official capacity) above shall be liable—

  1. on conviction on indictment to imprisonment for a term not exceeding two years or a fine, or both; and
  2. on summary conviction to imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months or a fine not exceeding the statutory maximum, or both.

(2) Proceedings for an offence under this section shall not be instituted in England and Wales except by the Secretary of State or by, or with the consent of, the Director of Public Prosecutions.

(3) No transaction shall be void or voidable by reason only that it was entered into in contravention of section (Prohibition on stock exchange deals by insiders, etc.) or (Prohibition on abuse of information obtained in official capacity) above.".—[ Mr. Parkinson.]
