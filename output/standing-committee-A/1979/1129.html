The problems in relation to loans by banks to companies associated with any of the banks' directors is alleviated by the tailpiece of subsection (5), which exempts companies with which a director is associated but which he does not control—broadly, those companies in which the director has a 20 per cent. to 50 per cent. interest—from the definition of a connected person for this particular provision.

Such companies will be connected only if the director controls more than half the voting power or owns more than half the share capital. This is necessary as it would be very difficult for the bank to keep constant track for the purpose of complying with these provisions of all transactions between the bank and the wider range of companies comprised in the broader category of associated companies, particularly as most of these transactions are likely to have been arranged at branch level throughout the clearing bank system.
