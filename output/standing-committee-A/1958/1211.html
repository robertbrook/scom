If he finds that it is not being properly expended, it is his duty to provide, under his own control, for methods of protection to see that that money can, if necessary, be reclaimed, or at least that no more shall be spent. It is not strictly speaking the same class of case as that dealt with by the Franks Committee. It is right that the Minister should retain this power under his own jurisdiction and for his own—I will not say officials—representatives. Therefore, I hope that my hon. Friend will realise the distinction. It is only because of the need to safeguard the expenditure of public money in this way that it is done in this form.
