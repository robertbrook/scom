    Mr. Portillo
    : On a point of order, Sir Michael. It is customary at the end of a Committee's proceedings to offer thanks to those who have made it possible for our deliberations to proceed smoothly. Because it is customary, there is a danger that we might appear simply to be going through the motions. But with this Bill, for which we have sat late into the night three times a week, our thanks are far from perfunctory. The Committee could not have operated without the tremendous co-operation from the people who keep the House of Commons ticking at all times—the policemen and attendants in the Room, the Clerks who assist you, Sir Michael, and your colleagues, and the Hansard staff who have been under a great strain. I hope that it is not invidious to single out the Hansard staff. The record of our debates has been of an extremely high standard. That has been very valuable.

    I should also like to thank, on behalf of all hon. Members, the officials from the Departments of the Environment and of Social Security. They have given us tremendous assistance until very late at night.

    : Our thanks extend also to the officials from the Welsh Office and the Scottish Office. They have done a marvellous job too.

    I am grateful also to all hon. Member's who took part in the Committee, irrespective of party allegiance. We have all been through the late hours together. I am very grateful for the support from my side and for the work done by Opposition Members.
