I was quoting the report of the advisers to the Department of the Enviroment, CSL. The Minister referred to the same report and used that as a basis for saying the everything was going well in relation to the software and other problems involved in getting the council tax ready. That is not so. Indeed, CSL's report makes that clear. It says:

"Approximately 75 per cent, of authorities in the sample have said that they have backlogs in one or more of the areas of Community Charge register maintenance, recovery or benefits.  The council tax is due to be implemented the following day. The report continues:

"As a result they will be unable to free existing resources for the Council Tax implementation, with the result that additional resources will be required in the short term. It is essential that backlogs are cleared prior to Council Tax systems going live."

 All the evidence points to that not happening. When officials from local authority associations and from the Department of the Environment met as recently as 18 November, CSL was quoted as saying:

"Backlogs of poll tax will knock council tax over if anything will."

 CSL went on to say:

"Also, the majority of authorities are experiencing difficulty in collecting Community Charge. Authorities feel that with the abolition of Community Charge the problems of collection and recovery will increase, creating an additional workload which will prevent existing resources being available for implementation."

 That is a long way from the rosy picture painted by the Minister. Those backlogs will increase because many people feel that the poll tax is finished. The Government will be spreading that message in the period up to the general election in the hope of nullifying the poll tax as an election issue. That will create problems. With their collection difficulties, councils cannot put all their resources into properly implementing the council tax on 1 April 1993.
