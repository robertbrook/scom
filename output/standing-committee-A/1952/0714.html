The Parliamentary Secretary to the Ministry of Works (Mr. Hugh Molson): I beg to move, in page 3, line 6, to leave out from "land," to the first "or," in line 7, and to insert:

The hon. Member for St. Pancras, North (Mr. K. Robinson), in his Second Reading speech, pointed out that there was a difference in the definition of land in Clauses 4 and 5, and said that he preferred the definition in Clause 5. He asked why there had been a difference in the definition. The reason was that Clause 4 deals with buildings, land and so on, in respect of which the Minister may make grants out of moneys provided by Parliament, and it was thought that it would be desirable for that to be confined to such land as was comprised in the amenities of the building. "comprising, or contiguous or adjacent to, any such building."

which I understand means not necessarily actually touching the building, may be included.
