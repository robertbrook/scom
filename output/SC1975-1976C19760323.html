One oriterion is the "only" and the other is the "principal" source of livelihood derived from work on the holding. We go from the one extreme of "only", that is to say, exclusively, to the other of "principal". I am querying why we denude the express and exclusively absolute criterion of "only" to "or prinicipal" source.

Have the Parliamentary Secretary and his advisers given proper and careful thought to the routines adopted by Her Majesty's Inspectors of Taxes reflected in the various tax Acts to treat income as that derived from trade, profession or vocation? That would be the literal interpretation of "derived from the holding", but that would not necessarily exclude a disputatious argument about income which might be categorised by the Inland Revenue under Schedule D, Case 6 type income, which need not necessarily inhibit the type of definition for which we are striving.

In any event, further clarification is called for in so far as there is this curious juxtaposition of "only" on the one hand, which is denuded to "or principal" on the other, and we require explanation and enlightenment.
