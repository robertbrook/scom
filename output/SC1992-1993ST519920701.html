DRAFT VIENNA DOCUMENT 1992 (PRIVILEGES AND IMMUNITIES) ORDER 1992

10.30 am

The Minister of State, Foreign and Commonwealth Office (Mr. Douglas Hogg)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the draft Vienna Document 1992 (Privileges and Immunities) Order 1992.

The purpose of the order is to confer privileges and immunities on observers, inspectors, evaluators and auxiliary personnel, in accordance with the provisions of the 1992 Vienna document of the negotiations on confidence and security-building measures.

The document was adopted in Vienna on 4 March, and it marks a further step in advancing military transparency and predictability in Europe. The Committee will appreciate that a necessary component in any arms control regime is the ability to monitor compliance with the 4 regime's obligations. The 1992 Vienna document seeks to do that by means of observations, evaluations and short-notice on-site challenge inspections, carried out by representatives of individual conference on security and cooperation in European states. The document requires that certain persons—observers, evaluators and inspectors, together with accompanying auxiliary personnel—are granted during their mission privileges and immunities in accordance with the Vienna convention on diplomatic relations. Such missions take place rarely in the United Kingdom. We are liable to receive up to three inspections and two evaluation visits a year. The United Kingdom has been obliged to host only one observation since 1986.

Mr. George Robertson
(Hamilton)
: Despite the temptations which are offered by an audience—which we all too rarely see—I have no objection to the proposal. I welcome the CSCE process of on-site challenge inspections and, therefore, also welcome the immunities and privileges that will be conferred on those who come under its auspices.

Question put and agreed to.

Resolved.

That the Committee has considered the draft Vienna Document 1992 (Privileges and Immunities) Order 1992.
