Draft Building Societies Act 1986 (Modifications) Order 1991

10.30 am

The Economic Secretary to the Treasury (Mr. John Maples)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the draft Building Societies Act 1986 (Modifications) Order 1991.

The order makes two minor amendments to the Building Societies Act 1986. First, it brings a definition of subsidiaries into line with the new definition in the Companies Act 1989. Secondly, it 4 removes the exemption on building societies having to publish accounts of their subsidiaries to pave the way for the EC bank accounts directive. This is an entirely technical measure to pave the way for the implementation of that directive, but if members of the Committee have any questions, I am prepared to answer them.

Mr. Stuart Bell
(Middlesbrough)
: I am grateful to the Economic Secretary for his brief and lucid statement on the order. We note that it implements an EC directive and that we are merely fulfilling our obligations. There are other times and places when one can make comments on the way in which EC directives relate to our banking sector, but this is not the moment for that.

Question put and agreed to
