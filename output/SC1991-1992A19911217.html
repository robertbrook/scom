The Minister for Shipping (Mr. Patrick McLoughiin)
: I beg to move,

That during the further proceedings of the Transport and Works Bill, the Committee do meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays at half-past Ten o'clock and with effect from Thursday at half-past Four o'clock.

In moving the motion, I am not criticising any members of the Committee for their speeches. I simply want to ensure that progress is made on the Bill. In our two sittings, we have dealt with two clauses. There is interest in the Bill and we need to sit in the afternoons to allow all members of the Committee to express their opinions.

The Minister laughs, but the scrutiny of statutory instruments does not take place because of a parliamentary quirk. Like most progressive aspects of parliamentary work, it happens because of outside pressure. Parliament is too complacent to reform itself. Ministers made several faulty statutory instruments, which led to outside pressure for improvement. Parliament then set up a Committee, which has become the Joint Select Committee on Statutory Instruments. We do a useful job. Our work is not glamorous; television cameras do not record it. We strike publicity gold rarely. That stirred some interest, as can be imagined, but, like gold, such luck is rarely encountered.
