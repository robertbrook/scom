clause Clause 134
title ANNUAL PAYMENTS UNDER CERTAIN INSURANCE POLICIES

Question proposed, That the clause stand part of the Bill.

10.30 am

Mr. Alistair Darling (Edinburgh, Central): Could the Financial Secretary tell us why the clause is necessary?

The Financial Secretary to the Treasury (Mr. Michael Jack): I shall try to deal with that. The clause introduces an income tax exemption for payments received by or in respect of individuals under certain types of insurance policies that people take out, or may contribute to through employers' schemes, to give themselves financial protection in times of sickness and unemployment. Such policies come in many different guises but they share the common feature that they provide regular payments during those times. They may be earmarked to meet mortgage payments or other financial commitments, or they may simply be used to meet ordinary living expenses.

The clause was introduced after discussion in the public prints because doubt had been raised by a conversation between a taxpayer and the Revenue over the tax position of payments under a mortgage protection policy. My right hon. and learned Friend the Chancellor deemed this to be a matter of genuine public concern. We do not want tax to be levied on the proceeds of that type of risk policy. A comprehensive examination of a range of other types of insurance was undertaken and, as a result, clause 134 was included in the Bill to put the tax position beyond doubt.
