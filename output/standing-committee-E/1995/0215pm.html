(Edinburgh, Central)

Mr. Jack
: I thank the hon. Member for Edinburgh, Central (Mr. Darling) for his courtesy in inviting me to give a little introduction. That will be a useful start to the debate.

This clause ensures that the mere giving up of employee share options by an employee in return for replacement options does not give rise to a capital gain or loss at the time that they are given up. Instead, any gain will be charged, or any loss allowed, when the new option is disposed of for consideration, or when shares obtained by exercising the new option are sold. Like clause 102, it simplifies the capital gains treatment of share options, reduces the need for difficult valuations of options and eliminates tax uncertainties that can arise as a result.

Question put and agreed to.

Clause 103 ordered to stand part of the Bill.
