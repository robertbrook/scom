Question again proposed. That the amendment be made.

Mr. Stephen Byers (Wallsend): Clause 30 would transfer liabilities from the local authority to the funding agency when schools opt out of local authority control. This morning I stressed that two important points must be tackled. I was halfway through my explanation of the first, which is simply that a clear public statement should be made of the cost of the grant-maintained sector to Britain's taxpayers. By concealing debt charges and by allowing the costs of premature retirement to remain with the local authority, the Government will provide a hidden subsidy for the grant-maintained sector. Many Opposition Members find that unacceptable.

The second argument is more complicated, in that we must enter the Alice-in-Wonderland world of local government finance in more detail than we have so far. It concerns the way in which the Government calculate debt and the restrictions placed on local authorities over the use of capital receipts to help capital projects in their areas. All hon. Members know that, until recently, there was what amounted to a clamp down by the Treasury on the use of those receipts, which could be used only for the redemption of debt. The autumn statement changed that. For a one-year period, local authorities will have the opportunity to use capital receipts for capital projects. That period is due to end in April 1994, when capital receipts will no longer be available for new projects.

Because the use of capital receipts is linked to the total amount of debt held by a local authority, reduction of that debt through the transfer of liability from the local authority to the funding agency would give a true reflection of spending on the grant-maintained sector and would liberate some of the capital money that would no longer be tied up in debt redemption. Such a reduction would allow the possibility of freeing some of local authorities' capital receipts.

Of course, the sums are notional, rather than real, but that proposal would be of great assistance to many local authorities. I hope that the Minister will consider it. Local government finance is a difficult subject. There are many experts but few certainties. However, there may be considerable benefit in reducing local authority debt in that way and transferring it to the funding agency.
