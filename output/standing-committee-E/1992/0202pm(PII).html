The Department for Education and the Government generally hold that Shakespeare, as an important part of our literary heritage, should be available to all students. In schools that I know, the assessment format adopted by the Government has forbidden or made less likely the teaching of Shakespeare to children who had been taught Shakespeare texts prior to its introduction. Not only is it almost forbidden by statute for lower-ability pupils to be taught Shakespeare, but the strategies that teachers need to adopt to teach Shakespeare successfully to the whole ability range—reading texts, but also watching films, undertaking co-operative work with other students and taking part in drama and discussion exercises—do not shape the format of testing proposed by the Government for key stage 3 English.
