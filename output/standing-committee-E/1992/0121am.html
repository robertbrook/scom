Mrs. Taylor: The amendments and clauses that we shall discuss today are very important. The purpose of amendment No. 585 is to explore the Minister's thinking about whether it is appropriate for schools to have a change of character at the time they become grant-maintained or very shortly afterwards. I hope that the Minister acknowledges that some answers are required.

The amendment would introduce a requirement that if a school were to seek a change of status within three years of becoming grant maintained, it should obtain the approval of the local education authority and the funding authority before the change could go ahead. That is a reasonable request, so I hope that the Minister will accept the amendment.

On Tuesday evening the Minister told us that he did not want turmoil in schools: he did not want them to flip-flop from one type of organisation to another. The changes associated with grant-maintained status are of great significance. The White Paper suggests that grant-maintained schools should have a change of status only in very special circumstances. The Minister said that when parents vote in a ballot on opt-out, they should concentrate on the main issue: the appropriateness of grant-maintained status. Therefore, it is essential that we ensure that any decision to opt for grant-maintained status is followed by a period of stability.

The amendment covers the two most significant changes that grant-maintained schools might consider. Col. 966 The first is the size of the school and the admission numbers. I hope that the Minister will accept that there should be no change in the size of a grant-maintained school without local authority involvement in the context of a decision on surplus places in the area. Ministers and local authorities have recognised the problem. To allow grant-maintained schools to apply in isolation for extra places could be costly and waste public funds if there are places in another area.
