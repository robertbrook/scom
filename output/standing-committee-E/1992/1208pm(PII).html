I was explaining my view that only a few permanent exclusions from schools are the result of a pupil's physical aggression towards teachers or other pupils. From my work in an LEA and from reports, I know that hours are spent by psychologists and educational social workers and welfare officers persuading children who are poor attenders and who are disruptive in the classroom to go back to school. That is an extreme example but it shows why our anxiety about the problem is growing. It explains why schools need a tight legal framework within which to operate.

The wording of the first part of the amendment is taken from schedule 2, paragraph 7 of the Children Act 1989, which states that local authorities must take

"all reasonable steps to reduce the need for care proceedings".

We felt that the analogy was particularly pertinent to clause 8. It is not difficult to think of reasonable steps that schools could take to tackle the problem.
