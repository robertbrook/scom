Before such changes are on the statute book, further consultation is necessary with the organisations and authorities that play a part in delivering education. The amendment recognises that necessity.

The amendment was tabled because we were aware of the benefits of consultation in previous education legislation. There was genuine, open consultation before the Education Act 1944 which brought with it a consensus about the way forward in the provision of education. The Government must want as wide a coalition as possible on the future provision of education; the amendment would achieve that goal.
