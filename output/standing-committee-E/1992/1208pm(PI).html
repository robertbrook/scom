Mr. Byers
: Amendments Nos. 181 and 182 would allow parliamentary scrutiny of a decision by the Secretary of State to make an order under clause 7. Amendent No. 183 would provide flexibility so that if circumstances changed and an order was no longer appropriate, it could be revoked.

Amendments Nos. 181 and 182 would enable Parliament to investigate and to consider the reasons for, and the motives behind, a decision to make an order. The Minister has suggested that the 10 per cent. entry figure does not automatically trigger the introduction of the funding agency: there is flexibilty at the edges. He said last week that an order under clause 7 would be invoked by the Secretary of State only when it was appropriate. We have had no guidance about what factors the Secretary of State will take into account to help him decide whether an order is appropriate. In the light of that vagueness, Parliament should be able to scrutinise the Secretary of State's decision to use those powers. It would help the Committee, and the House at a later stage, to know what those factors are.

There will be a transfer of responsibility and power from a democratically elected LEA to the Secretary of State. Such a development causes Opposition Members great concern. It is unusual to take away powers from a democratically elected body without giving Parliament the opportunity to scrutinise the Secretary of State's decisions. Through the negative resolution procedure, Parliament should be able to investigate the reasons for, and the circumstances surrounding, the Secretary of State's decision to use his powers under clause 7.

There are similar situations. As a result of the reforms in the national health service, if powers are transfered from a district health authority—which is not a democratically 542 elected or accountable body—to an NHS trust, the procedure is subject to parliamentary scrutiny. When responsibility for the provision of education is transferred from a publicly accountable elected body, to the Secretary of State and then to the funding agency there should be similar parliamentary scrutiny of the procedure. Amendments Nos. 181 and 182 would provide parliamentary scrutiny of the Secretary of State's decision.

Amendment No. 183 arises from our recognition of the fact that that it may be necessary in certain circumstances to change a decision to invoke clause 7. We are aware that school populations are not fixed; they change when, for example, the local population increases or decreases. The 10 per cent. trigger brings in the funding agency, but what will happen if the figure falls below 10 per cent? For example, if a school is closed because of falling rolls, instead of 11 per cent. of pupils in the secondary sector being educated in grant-maintained schools, there may be only 9 per cent. There appears to be no power in the Bill for the clause 7 order to be revoked. It would be appropriate to give such a power if the figure falls below 10 per cent.

It is important to remember that there will soon be another period of local government reorganisation. The Government have stated that they intend to reorganise the county councils and the shire districts with a view to establishing unitary authorities. That will affect the education service, which is at present delivered by the county councils. In an existing county council area, more than 10 per cent. of pupils may be in the grant-maintained sector. When unitary authorities are established, however, less than 10 per cent. of secondary or primary pupils in the district authority area may be in that sector. The Bill gives no flexibility to revoke the introduction of the funding agency in those areas where fewer than 10 per cent. of pupils are in grant-maintained schools. That will be a real possibility if the LEAs in England are reorganised as a result of the introduction of unitary authorities in the present county councils. Boundaries will be established and new education authorities will be set up within the county council areas.

By acknowledging the possibility that such changes will be made and that it may be necessary to revoke a clause 7 order, the amendments would improve a bad Bill. We are trying to be constructive as we move towards Christmas and I hope that the Committee will accept the amendments in that spirit.
