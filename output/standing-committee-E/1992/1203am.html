For example, the funding authority may decide that projections on school populations are such that a new school should be established. The local authority and funding authority may not only have duplicated roles and spend scarce resources, they may make different population projections. Even if they agree on those projections they may come up with different solutions—a new school or an expansion of existing schools. If the funding authority gets its way in such a case and encourages the building of a new school, that will have an impact on existing schools in the local authority sector.
