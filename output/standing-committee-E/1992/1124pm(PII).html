I am sorry that the hon. Member for Rugby and Kenilworth missed my excellent rendition of "Happy Birthday" as I sing even better than he does.

I always believe what the Minister says because he never tells a lie. He stated that there was no level playing field under the new arrangements and that extra money would be given to assist schools that took grant-maintained status. I accept that. However, he said, too, that the new arrangements would not involve increased public expenditure. That is appalling; it means that the Government are deliberately striking at schools that wish to stay under LEA control. Is that fair and just and good governance? It most certainly is not. I urge the Minister to reconsider his attitude to the issue so that the schools that want to stay with local education authorities have a fair deal. As my hon. Friends said, the crucial central services, will be pared away. I am thinking not only of services such as the educational psychologist, but of what used to be called the school bobby, which we will discuss at greater length later. If we are to tackle absenteeism, which—I deny it not—is a serious problem in some schools, we must be serious about doing it. There is no evidence in the clause that the Secretary of State will compel the funding authorities to spend the money necessary for the social inquiries, the social reports and the time that teachers will need to take off to take people to court or to solve problems that people have at home.

I appeal to the Minister's good nature. I ask him to examine the clause again and realise that he wishes something democratic, not dictatorial. We would join him in redrafting the clause. Then we could all go home to our beds happy.
