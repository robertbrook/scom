'(2) The agency shall have a duty to act fairly as between those persons regulated by it.'.

 The amendment is on a point that I hope will not detain the Committee for long, although it may be 746 significant. The Minister may be able to assure me that the matter is adequately dealt with elsewhere.

The amendment is animated by the belief that the SIB will have a difficult task. There is the problem of its uncertain status, as a result of the amendment passed at our previous sitting, and the question of the extent of its powers. But, more particularly, it will have to deal with, regulate and supervise a disparate group of regulators. On the one hand it will have a body such as the Stock Exchange, which has a long record of experience and expertise, and on the other hand it will have to deal with several self-regulating organisations that have yet to be put in place and whose competence is in question. Furthermore, there will be many individual people or institutions seeking direct authorisation.

The task of the SIB in trying to deal satisfactorily with all those groups will be difficult enough, but it may be eased if they are assured that the SIB is obliged to deal fairly between them: In the absence of a provision such as the amendment suggests there may be a danger that SROs or individuals may fear that they are being penalised by the SIB. They may fear that too much weight is being given to the Stock Exchange or, conversely, because of the pre-eminent position of the Stock Exchange, that too much effort is being made to accommodate the interests of other SROs. But in either case it would gravely weaken the authority of the Securities and Investments Board, and thereby gravely prejudice its chances of success, if there were thought to be any chance of anything other than impartial treatment between them.

An amendment that would impose directly in specific terms on the SIB a duty to act fairly may well allay potential fears that otherwise might stand in the way of effective regulation.

Mr. Howard
: I entirely agree that the designated agency should act fairly between the persons that it regulates. But I do not think that the amendment is necessary to achieve that. The agency will be exercising statutory powers and will have to do so subject to the usual restraints, including the restraint that it must not act unreasonably. If an action by an agency were to be actuated by other than relevant considerations, that would not be a reasonable use of the powers, and it could be challenged.

It might be helpful if I were to spell out the various safeguards against unfair use of the agency's powers. First, there is the availability of judicial review against an unreasonable action on the agency's part. Secondly, any decision of the agency of importance to individuals or particular businesses would be referable to the tribunal. The tribunal would not consider whether the agency was reasonable in taking or proposing to take the decision: it would consider the case afresh and come to its own conclusion on the correct decision. Any unfairness on the part of the agency could be overturned in the process.

Thirdly, there are the competition provisions. If the agency took action which treated one class of persons unfairly, without adequate justification on investor protection grounds, that could constitute an anticompetitive practice, and the Secretary of State could JPEG 747 resume transferred functions or direct the agency to take steps to change its rules or practices. Finally, the agency's immunity from liability for damages extends only to things done or omitted in good faith. Any unfairness on the part of the agency which constituted bad faith would still be actionable.

Mr. Campbell-Savours

The reason why the exemption is in the Bill is that the Government, recognising that fact, are intent on ensuring that the fact is hidden from the investing public.

The public's perception to policing arrangements is generally that policing arrangements are in the hands of publicly accountable authorities and not in the hands of private authorities.
