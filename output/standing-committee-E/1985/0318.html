clause Clause 120
title DISCONTINUANCE AND SUSPENSION OF LISTING

Mr. Howard: I beg to move amendment No. 298, in page 92, line 27, leave out

"and in such circumstances as may be specified in those rules".

Clause 120(2) deals with the suspension of listing. It enables the competent authority to suspend listing in accordance with the listing rules and in such circumstances as may be specified in those rules. Suspension of listing is not uncommon and often takes place at the issuer's request. It is resorted to if normal dealings become temporarily impossible for any reason, for example, if there are rumours that a bid is in the offing and the normal functioning of the market in the shares is impaired.

The condition that suspension may take place in circumstances specified in the listing rules was intended to give the competent authority the maximum discretion to specify in its rules the circumstances in which listing may be suspended. The stock exchange has made representations that it is not practicable for it to specify in advance all the circumstances in which suspension may become necessary or desirable, and that the wording will reduce its flexibility. Accordingly, it has asked for this amendment to be made, and, as we do not wish to make the competent authority's task unnecessarily difficult, I commend the amendment to the committee.

Amendment agreed to.
