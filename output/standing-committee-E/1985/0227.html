That sector of the market place has already grown considerably, and is likely to continue to do so. There are several reasons for that. The market is, therefore, not only vulnerable, but growing, and we must give consideration to it. We should operate a number of basic principles in approaching the market. First, I hope that the Minister will agree that the principle of tapered safeguards, as I shall call it, underlies the Bill. In other words, fewer safeguards are needed at the more professional, Eurobond end of the market, than at the bottom end of the market, which we are discussing, where caveat emptor is more appropriate and the safeguards must be stronger, more precise and more particular.

Secondly, in the future—we cannot achieve it immediately—those people operating in the specialist market place should have at least some competency through training. I do not claim that we can achieve that abjective immediately under the Bill, but I suggest that we aim towards it over a period of three, four or five years.
