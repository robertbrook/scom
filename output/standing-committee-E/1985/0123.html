Mr. Norman Buchan (Paisley, South): I beg to move amendment No. 9, in page 3, line 24, at end add—

"(4) The Board of Governors of the Museum shall, in each year after the coming into effect of this Act report to the Corporation of the City of London and to the Secretary of State and the financing, activities and programmes of the Museum of London, and such report shall be laid before Parliament by the Secretary of State.".

I wish to speak only briefly because the amendment speaks for itself. The amendment would ensure that board of governors gave a report to the Corporation of the City of London and to the Secretary of State and that the report was laid before Parliament. It is useful for Parliament to be made more aware of the work of museums but it is particularly useful for Parliament to be made aware of the work of the Museum of London, partly because of its importance but mainly because it will be unique in that half of the board of governors will have been appointed by the Government. If the number appointed by the Government were half plus one, it would be manifest that such a report should be laid before Parliament. However, even as it is, we believe that it is equally important to see how the museum's work is progressing.

We have reservations because we have reached clause 2 without succeeding in getting written into the Bill some of the statutory duties that we wanted the museum to undertake. Therefore, Parliament should keep a watchful eye on the museum's progress. As the board will not be independent when half of it is financed by Government, we believe that a report should be laid before Parliament.

The amendment speaks for itself. I shall be interested to hear the Minister's views. Perhaps we should hear his answer first so that we know whether it is satisfactory to us. As always, we wish to make progress.
