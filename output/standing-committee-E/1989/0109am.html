"I sold all interest in my company well before the last general election. I have no further interest in it".—[Official Report, Standing Committee E, 19 December 1989; c.37.]

He is an honourable man and he declared that interest even though he thought that he did not have to do so.

The company accounts for his company do not reveal that; they reveal that six days after the general election, he resigned his directorship of, that company. During the period up until his resignation date, which is recorded as 16 June in those company accounts, he is shown as receiving not only a Col. 42 director's salary but a chairman's salary or allowance. Therefore, what is recorded in Hansard for that date in relation to our Committee proceedings is not strictly correct. No doubt the Minister will have an explanation for that.

The problem, based on the extensive work that I have done, is that Michael Forsyth Ltd is acting in the market as if it is connected to a Minister of the Crown. It trades in his name. When a director of the company was asked about that one and a half years ago by Mr. Robin Dunwoodie of The Scotsman, he said:

"It is quite simple. We purchased the company about 12 months ago from Michael before he became a Minister, and we purchased the name of the company. That was part of the assets — there wasn't any point in buying it if we didn't buy the name".

Then there was reference to a forthcoming conference. The journalist asked the director:

"Wasn't this trading deliberately on the name of a Minister?"

The director replied:

"I am not prepared to comment any further on that".

We have a Minister in this Committee who may not have a direct pecuniary interest in a particular company at this stage; I shall come to that in a moment. But in Scotland, and perhaps in other parts of the country, that company is trading on his name. In discussions with Companies House in private yesterday, we were told that it would not now be possible to register a company in the name of any Minister if it could be seen that the intention was to use that Minister's name in the conduct of business. Therefore, I begin to question the right of this Minister to sit on this Bill.

Committee Members may be interested to learn this. I am informed that the resignation was seen in the trade as only temporary while the hon. Gentleman remains a Minister or is a Member of the House of Commons. The general view in the trade is that if the Minister were no longer a Minister or did not hold his seat, he would be reappointed and could acquire new shares.

Daily contact with the company is kept through the Minister's secretary, a Mrs. Griselda Hayes, who, I understand, works for the Minister in the House of Commons at his parliamentary desk. Griselda Hayes is married to a man — [Interruption.] — who purchased half the shares in Michael Forsyth Ltd when the Minister became a Minister. So there is a direct link between the company and the Minister.

Michael Forsyth Ltd is now owned by two gentlemen, a Mr. Robertson and a Mr. Hayes. Mr. Hayes's wife sits as secretary at the Minister's parliamentary desk — or she did until a few months ago. No doubt the Minister will clarify the matter. The House of Commons and Members' interests computer shows her to be his secretary. The House of Commons switchboard regards her as his secretary. She is on answerphone this morning in that name, so perhaps I am wrong. But she is the wife of the man who bought half the shares in the company.

Any member of this Committee who writes to a Minister might put the letter on the letter board downstairs. I might wish to write to the Minister about privatisation or particular contracts in Scotland. I have a list of contracts in Scotland that have been given to private companies. The list includes companies that Image Col. 43 have been represented by Michael Forsyth Ltd either directly or through various privatisation — interested associations.

But is the relationship between the Minister and me compromised when my correspondence passes over the desk of the wife of a director of a company that is trading in the name of the Minister? I believe that this matter is of great importance.
