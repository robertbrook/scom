PENALTY FARES

The Minister for London and Construction (Mr. Nick Raynsford)
: I echo your hope that our consideration of the matters before us will be speedy and thorough, Mr. Winterton.

The clause deals with penalty fares. As hon. Members will be aware, the Government have tabled new clause 60 and new schedule 5 to provide for the transfer of the current penalty fares regime. We are providing for a scheme that is almost exactly the same as that which was created by the London Regional Transport (Penalty Fares) Act 1992. We are doing that in the Bill rather than simply by giving the Secretary of State powers to prescribe a scheme by regulations. In that way, we will be in conformity with your wish, Mr. Winterton, and with that of the Committee that provisions be made as far as possible in the Bill. We have taken that approach even 870 though it will involve further Government amendments. I hope that the Opposition accept that the present proposals are in the spirit that we have discussed and that we have an opportunity to give them full scrutiny.

The new measures will remove clause 170 from the Bill and replace it with a new clause and a new schedule, which set out the provisions.

The Bill must make provisions for the penalty fares scheme that already operates on London Transport's tube and bus services and for that which operates on the docklands light railway. New clause 60 and new schedule 5 provide for a scheme that is almost exactly the same as that which was created by earlier legislation.

The penalty fares regime for Transport for London will cover the underground and buses, whether they are operated by TfL or under an agreement with it. The mayor will be able to determine whether the penalty fares regime applies to particular services or classes of service. He will also be able to determine the levels of penalty fares. Before making any change to those levels, however, he will be required to consult the Secretary of State and others with an interest, such as local authorities and transport users. The mayor will be required to consult the Secretary of State so that a consistent approach can be adopted to setting those penalty fares for which the mayor will have responsibility and those on surface rail services, for which the Secretary of State will have responsibility. That will avoid incoherence and inconsistency between the different regimes.
