I am happy to respond to the amendment, which seems to be based on a misunderstanding. The clause substitutes for section 68 of the Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992 new sections 68 and 68A.

Section 68(1) of the 1992 Act gives a union member who has certified to his employer that his union membership has been or, from a particular date, will be, terminated, a right to stop deductions from his pay for union subscriptions. Section 68(2) specifies that:

"The employer's duty under subsection (1) applies from the first day, following the giving of the certificate, on which it is reasonably practicable for him to comply with that subsection."

 The considerations which apply to section 68 of the 1992 Act do not apply to the circumstances covered by the new section 68(1). It is reasonable to allow an employer who is already making reductions a certain period of time to act on a notification to cease making those deductions but it does not make sense to apply the test of reasonable practicability when individual authority is required for the first time. In cases in which such circumstances apply, they have been taken account of—that concerns new section 68 (3)(b), which deals with circumstances in which a worker withdraws his authorisation to check-off deductions.

Practical issues are involved. The employer who is already making deductions has no way of knowing, until he receives notification, that the employee intends to withdraw from check-off. It is right and proper that the employer has time to make the necessary adjustments and that the condition of reasonable practicability can apply. I hope, on that basis, that the hon. Gentleman is absolutely clear about the subject and will withdraw his amendment.
