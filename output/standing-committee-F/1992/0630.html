Mr. Rhodri Morgan
(Cardiff, West)
: I had half-expected Mr. Martin or Mr. Winterton to be in the chair, so it is an added pleasure that you, Mr. Hogg, are making what I take to be a surprise visit—it is all the more welcome for that.

That is a need to tighten the wording of subsection (1)(b) by removing the word "associated", which is far too loose for the proper conduct of parliamentary business involving items of public expenditure, and replacing it with the purposive and descriptive phrase, to which I should have thought that we could all agree,

"consequential environmental and coastal protection".

 The need to tighten the definitions should have become apparent during last week's proceedings. The Government's cost calculations are far too loose and freewheeling in that they allow alterations. I shall give the Minister the calculations that I think should have been in the financial update given to us last week, and which were referred to as a comparison of cost figure.

Over the weekend, my constituents, to whom I supplied copies of the financial memorandum, have written to me about the cost calculations. I think the Committee is agreed that the Minister's figure of £167.4 million refers to the barrage package, which is the barrage plus the works that must be carried out and without which there could be no barrage—for exampe, environmental and coastal protection works. The figure is based on the hypothesis that construction of the barrage will start in 1993 and finish in 1998 or 1999. In the view of my constituents, the figure of £167.4 million should read £185.1 million.

Some of my constituents are considerably more expert on such matters than I, and we believe that the Government have forgotten about £9 million of additional expediture. It was referred to in January in the Secretary of State's own Stoner report on the cost of providing proper protection to properties, principally on the west bank of the river Taff. Mention of the west bank reminds me of the Israeli Labour party's astonishing performance last week.

The additional £9 million was referred to in January 1992—after the Second Reading in November 1991. The additional £9 million makes a considerable difference, partly because it is a snapshot figure and, therefore, lower than the equivalent outturn figure which is based on a hypothetical construction period from 1993 to 1998

If my constituents and I are right, the figure of £9 million in paragraph 7 (iii) of the Stoner report will have to be updated. On pages 9 and 10, Mr. Stoner states that the number of properties that will require remedial work has increased from the 1,650 envisaged before the report was published in January 1992, to 5,695. It will cost a lot more to remedy rising damp or the ingress of water to basements in 5,695 properties than it would have cost in only 1,500 or so.

It appears that the Welsh Office machine has omitted the £9 million in Stoner; if the figure were set on an outturn basis, it would probably be closer to £11 million. I put it at £10 million to keep it at round figures.

I think that the Government accept—it was in the Minister's statement—that they have also omitted another £6 million or £7 million, to be spent by private treaty on so-called mitigation works, whether on Wentlooge levels or further away in the constituency of the hon. Member for Monmouth (Mr. Evans) on the Caldicot levels—it depends where the Government get the best deal. In the Stoner report, there is a minor item of expenditure which may be between £500,000 and £1 million for remedial drainage works to be carried out by Cardiff city council but, plainly, to be charged to Cardiff Bay development corporation. That, too, is on page 9 of the Stoner report, at paragraph 7ii. It gives a cost of £440,000 for the work, but, on an outturn basis, I think that it could be £750,000.

There is also what I allege is a phoney figure on the second material page of the notes for Members. The figure of £14.80 million, for diversion and modification of sewers, was given in the financial memorandum on Second Reading last November. In January this year, that figure was also given in the Stoner report as £14 million, on a snapshot basis. It is bizarre that the Minister is claiming that that figure is not touched by converting it to an outturn basis, as some of the work has already been carried out by the council and Welsh Water, such as adjustments to the Cogan outfall and sewer diversions made necessary by the construction of the peripheral distributor road. Mr. Roy Stoner, the Secretary of State's adviser, did not seem to be aware of that in January this year.

I have added £2 million to arrive at a proper figure rather than at a fiddled, Robert Maxwell pension fund type figure which is suggested by the mysterious similarity between the £14.80 million and £14 million figures, which are given in spite of the changes in prices since November 1988 and since October 1991. I have added £17.7 million to the £167.4 million outturn figure and that totals £185.1 million. In my opinion and in the opinion of my expert constituents, that is the true cost of the barrage and of, not "associated" works, to use the Bill's loose wording, but of

"consequential environmental and coastal protection", to use the tighter and more precise phrase used in the amendment.
