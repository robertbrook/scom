Ms Short
: These amendments are narrow but extremely important. They would amend the Bill so that the availability of jobs in the local labour market is taken into account. Government rhetoric constantly stresses that the number of jobs registered at jobcentres can be multiplied by three to give the number of jobs available in the economy and claim that research shows that repeatedly. The crude equation is that there are 700,000 vacancies and 2.4 million unemployed, according to the much-reduced Government figures. It is suggested that if people only tried harder and looked for work more actively, unemployment would be significantly reduced.

There is a lot of misunderstanding here: some hon. Members genuinely misunderstand and others pretend to misunderstand how the figures are made up. Therefore, I shall explain briefly the nature of vacancies and skill shortages and how they are filled, and then come to the details of the local labour market. The 700,000 vacancies that exist in the economy at any one time no more represent a static pool of unfilled vacancies waiting to be occupied by the unemployed that the figure of 2.4 million unemployed claimants represents a static pool of unemployment. Both figures are snapshots of substantial flows. Each month, some 400,000 people enter unemployment, either by leaving or losing a 242 job or by newly entering the labour market. A similar number obtain jobs or quit the labour market through retirement, motherhood, full-time education or whatever.

The official unemployment figure at any one time is the accumulated or reduced balance between the two flows. Similarly, vacancy figures for a particular day represent the rising or falling cumulative balance between a constant inflow of new vacancies and a constant outlow of filled vacancies.
