Amendment No. 613 deals with another problem. Local authorities received a letter on 31 January from the DSS. We are now getting to the nub; the Government's position is fatuous. The letter outlines the proposed housing policy subsidy arrangements for 1989–90. The letter states:

"Where a local authority is unable to reduce the eligible rent under the provisions of regualtions 11 and 12 of the Housing Benefit (General) Regulations, subsidy at the rate of 50 per cent will be payable on benefit awarded on that part of the eligible rent above the rent officer's determination."

Regulation 11 deals with cases where the claimant's home is larger than is reasonably required by the people living in it or the rent is unreasonably high. In such cases the local authority can reduce the eligible rent or rates on which housing benefit is calculated, but not if a member of the family is over 60 or incapable of work or if there is a child in the family and they cannot be expected to move to cheaper accommodation. In other cases no reduction is made for the first 13 weeks if the claimant could afford the accommodation when he or she first moved in.

In all these cases, although the local authority is obliged to pay housing costs based on the full rent for at least 13 weeks and in many cases a much longer period, only the "reasonable market rent" will qualify for a full subsidy. Benefit payments for rent above that level will qualify for only 50 per cent of subsidy.
