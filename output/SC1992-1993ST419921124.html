Double Taxation Relief (Taxes on Income) (Falkland Islands) Order 1992

4.30 pm

The Financial Secretary to the Treasury (Mr. Stephen Dorrell)
: I beg to move,

That the Committee has considered the Double Taxation Relief (Taxes on Income) (Falkland Islands) Order 1992.

This morning another Committee discussed the double taxation arrangements between this country and Guyana. Until that treaty is given effect, there will be, and in recent years there have been, no double taxation arrangements between the two countries.

That is not the position between Britain and the Falkland Islands. Indeed, the subject of this morning's debate was positively visionary by comparison with the order this afternoon, which is an extremely detailed, even technical amendment to existing arrangements between the two countries. It concerns the relief due to United Kingdom companies in respect of dividends that they receive from Falkland Islands subsidiaries.

The 1984 order extended that relief to take account of tax spared under the specific Falkland Islands Income Tax Ordinance. In 1987 the Falkland Islands Government decided to revise their legislation in this area and introduced the Taxes and Duties (Special Exemption) Ordinance, which had substantially the same effect as the order which is referred to by name in the existing double taxation arrangements between this country and the Falkland Islands. The amendment enshrined in the order merely updates the statutory reference in the existing arrangements and is therefore a textual amendment with no practical effect on the intention underlying the arrangements.

We have also taken the opportunity of the textual amendment to introduce wording that provides for the application of UK tax relief to any substantially similar provisions that may be enacted in future. The amendment is one of very limited scope and I hope that it will commend itself to the Committee.

Mr. Dorrell
: The best way in which to answer the Opposition's questions may be to focus the Committee's mind on precisely what we are discussing. The 1984 arrangement was brought into effect by Order in Council in 1984. That arrangement contained a provision to ensure that we did not tax profits which were exempt from taxation in the Falkland Islands under the provisions of the pioneer company element of its domestic legislation. The 1984 arrangement, which was put into effect by the 1984 order, referred to the domestic legislation which provides for that pioneer company arrangement in the Falkland Islands. Since the Falklands Islands Government amended their legislation and introduced a new ordinance in 1987, the double taxation arrangement has been out of date because it refers to legislation which was repealed and replaced by the Falkland Islands Government with effect from 1987. The sole purpose of this arrangement is to delete in the existing order reference to the legislation on the Falkland Islands statute book which was repealed in 1987 and replace it with a reference to the legislation which the Falkland Islands Government introduced in 1987. That is the reason for the retrospection to 1987.

I was properly asked about the position in the mean time. If a claim had been made, we would have allowed relief which was not directly provided for in legislation since the arrangement refers to Falkland Islands legislation which is no longer on the statute book. It is clear that the intention from 1984 onwards has been to grant relief when the Falkland Islands Government provide relief to pioneer companies. The arrangement is being backdated to 1987, giving it retrospective effect. The hon. Member for Newcastle upon Tyne, East properly described the measure as a relieving rather than an imposing one. The reason for backdating it is to ensure that there is consistent statutory 20 authority from 1987 onwards for any relief under the terms of this arrangement, the intention of which has been the same from the day on which it was introduced in 1987. The statutory authority under which the provision is implemented as delegated legislation is now, although it was not in 1984, in section 788 of the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1988, subsection (5) of which contains a definition of pioneer companies.

I was asked why pioneer companies are entitled to that relief for 10 years under our domestic legislation. The purpose of relief for pioneer companies is to encourage investment in developing countries. It is reasonable that when that investment has been made, has taken root and is generating profits, the special arrangements that were made available to encourage the investment should come to an end.

The hon. Member for Hartlepool referred to the question of residence. I assure him that the provisions for residence in this arrangement as in the treaty covering Guyana, are the OECD model terms governing residence. The Labour party when in power was content that those terms should form the basis of double taxation relief arrangements.

The hon. Gentleman also asked what would happen if the arrangements for sovereignty of the Falklands Islands changed. That question would have to be covered in any legislation which changed the status of the Falkland Islands.
