Lord James Douglas-Hamilton
: The hon. Lady asks the important question as to whether an exceptional cost of bringing in witnesses would be covered. The answer is that it would. An outlay could be provided for separately. The same applies to expert witnesses.

The clause would allow the Scottish Legal Aid Board to make prescribed fixed payments to solicitors in respect of criminal legal assistance provided by them. It does not remove existing legislative provision in relation to payments that can be made from the legal aid fund. It simply allows the Secretary of State the option of prescribing fixed payments and brings the position in Scotland into line with that in England and Wales where the legislation governing legal aid allows for such payments.

Fixed payments would give a greater degree of control over legal aid expenditure than is possible under the current payment arrangements. They would also reduce the administrative burden on both the board and solicitors. We therefore consider it sensible to ensure that the option of making payments in that way is available.

The clause gives the option of prescribing fixed sums which can include all or some outlays. For outlays such as waiting or travelling time it may be fairer to continue to let those be charged separately. However, that will be the subject of consultation with the Law Society. It should be possible to devise a system that recognises the complexity of certain types of cases and the simplicity of others.
